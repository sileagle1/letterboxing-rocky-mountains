Yarrrrrr...this be big fun! The whole town has gone piratey (it'll be
a long week for poor La Paloma), but the most fun be on the
RMLetterboxers list as usual.

Two years ago the treasure was hidden in the very outhouse used for
the outhouse stuffing. Y did a dance about the whole thing feeling
it's sides and all....but didn't think to look in the vent flue up
top, Garrrrh! But that reminds me about the days of outhouse oriented
clues that lead up to that fateful day.

So...looking for a theme, here are all the clues up to this point
with the latest one added. I probably won't be able to check in until
tomorrow, but I'll update you about our snoop around town when I can:

1) Look at the aft of the map. (Behind a Chamber Map or outdoor map
or map on the poster or brochure?)

2) From West Derby look towards New Mexico. (They played baseball in
derby hats. Also West Derby is in Englandnorth of Liverpool)

3) I'm thinking of a number between 2 and nay, (neigh?). (Nay is
Indian for wind, April Wind is a shop on Main.Nay also means
NO.Neigh for wild horse saloon).

4) You won't need a shovel to dig for this treasure. ( you might
need an Oar?)

5) You are being watched. (This could mean the web-cam that is at
College and Main, although there is another one run by CDOT at 550
and 160 by the Doubletree Inn that is on the south side of town).

6) The treasure you seek is not in plain sight. (Not on the plains
but in Mountains..Pedal the Peaks? Not in Plain sight, but in the
website?)

7) A New German directs you to look to the sunrise. (New Germany is a
town in Minnesota----Minnesota Vikings? I don't know of any Germans
or Vikings pointing east in town.MennonitesSt. Pauli Girl).

8) You'll find it at a lower altitude than last year. (That could
mean about anywhere in town, because the disc golf course is on the
mesa with the College above town).

9) How would you say that in Spanish? (Gold in Spanish is OroOre
House).

Fare thee well, scoundrels,
