Box 1 - Fairy Moon:

A Fairy Blessed Me
Today a Fairy blessed me and danced upon these walls
Left a scent of roses as she beckoned to my calls
Gave me comfort, and wiped my tears
blessed my mind of all those fears
Touched my eyes so I could see
Whispered softly, "Blessed Be"
copyright 2005 Katie G

If you happen to be in the Glenwood Springs area, head up highway 133
past Carbondale to the small town of Redstone. Stop in and visit the
local shop owners of this lovely little town, or continue about 2
miles farther up the road, between mile marker 49 and 50, to Hays
Creek Falls. There isn't a lot to park in, just a little pull-out off
the side of the road so watch your youngling and pets closely.

Check out the falls a perfect fairy home, don't you think? If you
wander around the Redstone side of the falls, near the road, you may
see a sign marking some underground phone lines. This muggle sign is
simply to keep folks away from the fairy who lives here. If you head
not but a dozen feet behind this sign, you will find two brother
rocks leaning on one another guarded by pines and narrow leaf
cottons. Between these brothers, tucked waaaayyy back in, is a
perfect place to keep one's wings out of the spray of the falls.

This is a drive up, but watch for people as they sporadically visit
her abode to take a photo or two. Please be discreet and re-hide well
to keep away prying eyes and so others may leave their mark in her
guest book.


Box 2 - Draig-Uisge (Water):
Head west of Idaho Springs on I-70 to the turnoff for Fall River
Road. Follow the signs for Fall River Road, and head up about 9 miles
until you see the trailhead marked "glacier trail". Park on the left
just past the trailhead sign About 3/4 of a mile up this VERY rocky
path, you will find St. Mary's Glacier. (Make sure you bear left at
the 1st two forks you come to.)

Check out the lake and the glacier itself but make note - there are
no amenities here such as bathrooms!

Once you are done poking around, head to the south side of the
bridge. Once there face away from the glacier, and set your compass
to 136. About 65 paces away, you will see a tree that has been
knocked over, and its roots are showing. Head to the root side of
said tree and take another bearing at 110. You will see a very small
stump no more than 13 paces away. Once there, check out the view at
10. Framed between a 'V' shaped pine tree, you should see a rock
with a small-ish pine tree behind it. On the north side of this pine
the dragon who favors water should be sleeping.

Note: This can be a VERY busy place depending on the season. Please
be sure that you re-hide this box better than you found it, and be
discreet when finding and hiding so that others may enjoy it.


Happy hunting!

~RavenWolf~
