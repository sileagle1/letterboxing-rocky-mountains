From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-03 20:14:36 UTC
Oh, gosh, you all are the nicest people. :::group hug!:::

I've had a chance to visit with 'the Keepers of
Charlotte'. ::laughs:: Their first response had been to race out to
rescue her and their first glance looked disasterous. This was the
conversation:

Colorado Celt -"Greek! Charlotte is in danger! Go get her now!"

The Greek looks up from his lunch and says, "Now? Can I get it after
lunch?"

CC - "NO! We are the keepers!"

With grim determination The Greek pushes back his chair and mounts
the rescue mission immediately.

After close examination (with lots of frowning Mom faces and muttered
threats to unknown boxers), CC determines that only the journal is
ruined. Which is the saddest loss of all to me because of the
wonderful history it held. But the main 'prop' (you Pirate King
Alumni know of what I speak) is fine as is everything else. Aside
from the loss of the stamps in there, CC tells me that the box was
left sitting in it's spot with a single rock on it and the 'marker'
off to the side. Now, those of you who have been there know what
that must have looked like! ::laughs:: Something out of a
Frankenstein movie.

I'm a little ashamed to have made such a big deal out of a box full
of trinkets and papers. But, like Y said, it's not like it doesn't
say 127 times that you have to repack it carefully and rehide it
completely if you want to play. But there you go. I know who it was
and they are pretty much blacklisted for all my boxes in the future.
Just sayin'.

So, no worries. And thanks to everyone for the lovely notes and
comments. If Charlotte returns those of you who are wonderful boxers
will know and be invited to visit her.

Es, who can think of lots more important things to be worried about.
Like that pie shop in Colorado Springs getting shut down by the IRS.
