From: Amy Eckert (aeckert1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-01-22 09:34:46 UTC-08:00
y-nought asked:

"How do you "condition" a stamp with a sharpie. I have
a stamp that I
carved out of PZCut Grade B and you can see a line
where the ink
doesn't get picked up as well......do you think this
might help??"

It may! PZ is my favorite carving medium, but the
PZKut stamps don't always pick up ink well right out
of the box (pun intended). I condition them by
basically going over the stamp with a Sharpie and
letting it dry. It basically acts as a primer to help
the stamp pick up ink.

I believe some people also go over the stamp with
acetone, but I've had good luck with the Sharpie
method.

Sometimes very fine sand paper will get a line out, but you have to
watch out on a stamp that's already carved, not to ruin the detail.
If you notice it BEFORE carving, you can be more agresstive. I've
also had good luck with acetone, used sparingly.
