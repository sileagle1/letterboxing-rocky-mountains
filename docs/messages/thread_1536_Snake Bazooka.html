No Es, you can't kill those. "Snakes, even poisonous ones, provide
humans a valuable service by eating rodents that can spread disease."
What you need is the patented JeLyWorks Snake Flinger 9000. You've
seen those tennis ball flinger thingies that people use for their dogs,
right? Well this pretty much works the same way, except that it folds
up into a compact size for carrying in a backpack. If you encounter a
snake, you slowly back away, then push a button and the flinging arm
telescopes out about 20 feet and latches onto the snake. Then you
turn, place the arm over your should for leverage, and whang that
sucker about three miles. It's the latest thing. I understand there
will be an exhibition of snake flinging as a new potential sport at the
Beijing Olympics.
