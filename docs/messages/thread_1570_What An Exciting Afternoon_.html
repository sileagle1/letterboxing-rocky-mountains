What An Exciting Afternoon!

From: cameoboxer (cameoboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-29 16:03:43 UTC
PRE and FrontRangeHiker spent the weekend in my area, so it was a
perfect time to meet them. I was so excited! You see, I've met only a
precious few letterboxers (outside of my family!). There was the
cranky family I met on the trail in 2004 (they wanted nothing to do
with us!), and the thrilling experience of hosting the Cabin Fever
Event in Oklahoma (2005) where I finally met about 15 fellow
letterboxers. That was 3 years ago, and I've not met anybody since
(and none here in Colorado).

You all know how it is to wonder about the person behind the trail
name. Well, now was my chance to meet two of them. Woo hoo!

We met up in Carbondale at a lone picnic table in the yard of an old
historic schoolhouse. I'd brought my clingy puppy (3 pound chihuahua
named Bean) who, immediately after dropping his leash, decided he'd
like to go for a swim behind my back. I saw PRE jump, and when I
turned around I saw Bean looking stunned as he struggled to swim in
this little creek that was dragging him away! Thanks to PRE's quick
reaction (she ran over, fell to the ground, and snatched him up), Bean
was soon safe in his mama's arms again, shivering in the 90 degree
heat. Huh. Larious.

After that little ice-breaker, we went unloaded all our goodies on the
table and had a good ol' time exchanging. There were two especially
exciting things that happened: I got my very first (and second)
personal traveler(s), and FRH got her 100th EXCHANGE!!! Congrats FRH!
She was so excited; so cute! We gabbed away as poor PRE's arm (that
was facing the sun) got redder and redder. We had to break it up so
they could escape the sun and start the long drive home.

I want to thank you both for all the new plants nearby, the updates,
the exchanges, and for switching up our HHs to refresh and replenish
our respective hostels. I hoped to post a couple of pictures of our
mini, mini, mini-meet (including a soaked Bean being coddled by his
rescuer), but my computer swiped all the pics off my memory card -
ARGH!!!

Thanks for reinvigorating my desire to letterbox!
Cameo

In addition to meeting Cameo in person, pre and I had an excellent
time finding some of her WEL series letterboxes along the West Elk
Loop.

We started and ended our letterboxing weekend meeting other
letterboxers and sharing our love of the game. Thanks for both
Cameo and CW Sun Seeker for making the trip so much fun.

I'm often impressed by the artwork created by our talented Rocky
Mountain planters. There is so much creativity put into the stamps,
logbooks and clues in our area. I appreciate it even more after
visiting other areas of the country and checking out the letterboxes
there.

Looking forward to more adventures on the western slope this weekend.
