Missing boxes suggestion

From: claudey (erin@erinterry.com) | Date: 2008-01-31 23:08:52 UTC
I was wondering if I could make a friendly suggestion that maybe we can encourage our
members to do a little pre-spring cleaning on the letterboxing lists... I noticed that there are
several boxes listed on both sites that have been missing for over 2 years. Perhaps the
owners could either archive them to keep them from showing up in searches, or replace
them. I know it's hard when you have missing boxes out of town from where you are, but I'm
sure that members living in those cites would be more than willing to help replace them,
myself included! I hope no one takes offense at this suggestion. I just know that I was
frustrated when I did a search for boxes in a particular city, only to find that 1/3 of the listed
boxes were MIA. Not frustrated at the placers, mind youthose dirty rotten thieves who took
them! Still, it would be nice if the list only showed active boxes, or boxes that will be
replaced in a month or 2. Just my 2.

claudey

Re: Missing boxes suggestion

From: Deb Brooner (broonerdeb@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-02-13 16:22:46 UTC
It has taken me a whole lot of boxes (and a lengthy trip into CA) to
figure this out, but there are several suggestions I have for the
frustrating "no box" situation. Probably this is old news to seasoned
LBers, but hopefully it will help the newbies. First of all, when I
go onto the LB North America site and get to the state I am looking
for, I go to the top and hit the "sort by placement date" selection.
This allows me to see the most recent boxes. For boxes tht are not
as new I always check the clue page and/or the membership page to see
when the last found date was. I then can make a decision as to
whether or not I want to take a chance at a particular box.

I was rather surprised to find that this was not covered in Randy
Hall's book-it would sure make it less frustrating for those just
starting out.

Deb
