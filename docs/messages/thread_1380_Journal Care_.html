We were devastated as well to learn from our Pirate Queen about the
condition of her first box in her well LOVED WOM series! For any
boxer not to care for the journal like a RARE BOOK, which it IS, is
heartbreaking!!! That's what this is all about afterall!

I wanted to bring to the board another incident that concerns journal
care. We were told that our Moffat Road journal was, well, misused,
to say the least! It seems that a family found the box and allowed
for one of the children to use the stamp in the box to stamp nearly
every page of the journal!!! Now, if we wanted that stamp on each
page, rather than the finders stamps, we would have done that
ourselves! What makes it even more difficult is that this box is NOT
NEARBY and we don't know when we can get up there to replace the
journal.

PLEASE, families, teach your children to CARE about the boxes they
find and DON'T allow them to trash the journals that are made to
record the finders of the box. This isn't the first time we've
encountered this situation. We've seen journals where kids scribbled
on multiple pages. I'm just askin' to TEACH YOUR CHILDREN TO CARE
AND RESPECT other people's property. It's just the RIGHT thing to do!

Thanks for listening! And HUGS to ES who plays down a devastating,
unnecessary loss! It isn't just items in a box, it's a WELL LOVED
and RARE book that is now LOST to letterboxing history!!!

A heavy hearted Peach

Re: [RMLetterboxing] Journal Care!

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2007-08-04 18:06:23 UTC-06:00
Journal care can be a tough one! I've seen scouts wipe out an entire
journal in one visit using their fingerprints. I've seen people use the
box stamp, because they don't have their own. Journals get exposed to
rain and then are stuffed back into their Ziplock bags and left to mold,
etc. Certainly, there are parents who allow their children to mishandle
journals, but there are also irresponsible adults with no children in tow
who do plenty of damage to journals. The growing popularity of
letterboxing and the ease of access to clues imperils the hobby.

One journal tip from us: We save the little desiccant cylinders from
vitamin bottles and add one to each of our journal bags. Of course, it
will not help for any severe problems.

~SW

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2007-08-05 06:03:19 UTC-06:00
I agree, the folks that care to be on this board are unlikely to be the
source of these etiquette problems, but this is a place to share and
sometimes vent our frustrations.

There is a learning curve for new boxers. Common sense etiquette comes
naturally for some folks and others seem completely clueless. Some folks
will read through the code of conduct on the websites and some just
breeze by, grab some clues, and start on their merry way. When I noticed
a letterboxing workshop to be scheduled for the summer reading program in
Estes Park this year, I inquired about who was leading it. I spoke with
that person (the children's librarian, who had recently taken up
letterboxing with her family) and asked if they'd be discussing
letterboxing etiquette, etc. In short, we ended up having a nice chat
and were asked to come talk about etiquette and our own personal
experiences. The librarians did a great job and included a "Code of
Conduct" in their packet of information. Our participation enabled us to
share about some of the problems that esp. come up on the Front Range
with rehiding and journal care, etc., share with them about how much time
and care goes into making and placing these boxes, show them our journal
and an example of a finished journal from Nimbus' Blue Box. I expect
that the eight or so families that attended will not be adding to any
frustrations in our wonderful Colorado letterboxing community.

~Silent Wings, wishing you all a very nice Sunday
