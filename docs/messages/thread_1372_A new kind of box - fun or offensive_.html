A new kind of box - fun or offensive?

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2007-07-28 19:27:16 UTC
Hey kids,

I had an idea for a new kind of letterbox, but wanted to get your
thoughts on it first. It is sort of a traveling bonus or parasite
box but works in reverse; you find the parasite first and it tells
you how to find the original. I am tentatively calling it a
Changeling Box, and here's the idea:

1) You get the clues for some normal box like usual and head out to
find it.
2) Upon arriving at the spot where you think it should be, you
instead find another box, an imposter, a Changeling.
3) You stamp into the Changeling like you would a normal box. On the
most recent entry in the logbook, you find the directions to find the
original box, hidden somewhere nearby.
4) You find the original box nearby and stamp into it like normal.
5) You take the original box back to its original spot and re-hide it
where it was supposed to be. You take the Changeling with you when
you leave.
6) Next time you go out to find a letterbox, you take the Changeling
with you, and when you find the box you set out for, you temporarily
remove it an put the Changeling in its place.
7) You look for a suitable hiding spot nearby to hide the original
box.
8) You put the directions to find the original box as the newest
entry in the Changeling log, rehide everything and be on your way.

What do you think, is this fun or offensive? Would you object to
having someone temporarily remove one of your boxes? If you hate the
idea, don't be afraid to say so, I can take criticism.

JeLyBean, all ears

From: Ron D (rmed_mountain@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-28 23:33:05 UTC-05:00
Know I'm just a visitor here, but...I think this is hijackin'!

I'd NEVER do anything to someone else's box without their prior approval
(minus replacing a ziploc or so). A while back there was a brief lived
thing I believe was a virus which was basicly the same thing which involved
relocating boxes, even a short distance and there was a huge uproar about
it. I believe no one should have the right to change the experience set out
by those who place the box, minus the standard additions of logs into books
or HHs and so on.

I think it is a bad idea to encourage anything but proper re-hideation.
Encouraging moving boxes is BAD. Folks set up boxes in specific places with
specific clues. If folks begin re-hiding boxes where THEY desire, then it
is not the same experience as what the planter intended. Plus those who
relocate might put them in compromising locations. Plus if a channgling
goes missing the origional box goes missing as well.

People place these boxes out of good-will and shouldn't have the additional
worry of this business.

Lnd-Crzr

Re:A new kind of box - fun or offensive?

From: Mandy Bennett (cameoboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-02 21:34:58 UTC-07:00
My opinion is this: Woo HOO!
More boxes for your time and effort! However, the fear of getting one of our own boxes replaced is legitimate. You'd have to trust people to do things correctly (and with no ill intentions). But don't we already do that? Putting a box out at all is a risk. We put a little plastic box on the ground in the middle of nowhere, left to the wild and elements; then we put information on the internet that ANYone can access describing how to find it. Anyone who's placed a box knows there's always risk involved. And how many hitchhikers have we let loose into the world? Little bitty boxes that we've put so much work and love into with the expectation that letterboxers will take good care of them? I'm not necessarily saying we've GOT to do Changelings (though I'd want to find one!), but I do feel like we all ought to give it some thought before jumping to a NO-WAY conclusion.

Imagine what we'd be missing out on if traditional letterboxing was the only accepted form? No virtuals, postals, cooties, personal travelers, hitchhikers, etc. This idea could lead us to experience yet another world of delight!

My 2 Cents,
Cameo

Re:A new kind of box - fun or offensive?

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2007-08-03 05:58:39 UTC
Hey, thanks to everyone who responded to this idea, either here or
offline, either positively or negatively. I've gotten a lot of good
thoughts from you. After some consideration, I've decided to drop the
idea.

I've been letterboxing almost since the historic Smithsonian article
was published and I have to say, there was a time when this idea may
have found a better reception. Seems like some years ago (almost ten
now!) letterboxers seemed more respectful of other people's boxes. But
the hobby has expanded rapidly to today, and there are some good things
about that, and some not so good things. When I see a private, word-of-
mouth box being trashed - and one that obviously involved a tremendous
amount of time, effort (and money) - that pretty much tells me I can no
longer rely on people to respectfully move a box, and respectfully put
it back. If even special WOM boxes are not being treated well, then
this idea probably won't fly. So, not wanting to jeopardize anyone
else's boxes, I've decided not to pursue this. I'll keep searching for
other ways to make this hobby more and more fun.

J
