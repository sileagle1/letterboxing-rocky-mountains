Ok Folks, I'm not kidding this time. Someone at work just gave me an
entire paper box full of Altoid boxes - over 100 of them!

I now challenge the entire Rocky Mountain Letterboxing planet (and your
kids' chronically underfunded art classes, or anyone else you think can
use them) to take some of them off my hands.

Let's have a mini box extravaganza! PLEASE! I hated to say no to them,
but I really don't have the space to store them. I can confirm that
they work great as magnetic boxes with 2 small strong magnets from the
hardware store affixed to the back.

Contact me on or off list for how best to get them to you.

Thanks in advance,
preboxed/HER

Oh funny! Now, it sometimes takes me a bit to throw fun tins out, but
hundreds of them? I don't want to see what that house must look like if
other things are saved.

I'm not really in a spot for mini boxes right now, but best of luck.
Remember to hide them well in spots that will stay more clear from
moisture. I have seen boxes destroyed so quickly with access to the
elements, and I've seen one I have last for years. It's all about how you
do them and where they are.

Have fun!
Cadenza
