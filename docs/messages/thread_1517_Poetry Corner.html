From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-05-06 22:19:59 UTC
I think the ninja/pirate debate expresses two styles of letterboxing.
And I think we all can use each style when it suits us most. To
illustrate my theory I would like to offer some incredibly awful
poetry. The poems' only redeeming quality is that they are in pirate-
limmerick or ninja-haiku form.

The Limmerick of the Letterboxing Pirate:

A pirate with cluesheet in hand
And a ship that sails over the land,
Over miles will tramp
For the tiniest stamp
And think that their plunder was grand.

Or this little ditty:

Es was a pirate who thought
Some mystery clues could be bought
She plundered a bakery
For pies and cheesecakery,
To use as a bribe, and y-nought?

On the other hand, the Ninja Haiku:

On a busy street
A woman ties her shoelace
and snags tupperware.

Or:

Invisible man
Leaves one sign of his passage
A Pink Jellybean!

I'm sure you folks can do better than these....and I would sure like
to hear some more!

y-nought
