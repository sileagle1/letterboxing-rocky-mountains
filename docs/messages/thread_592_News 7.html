Here's why I like letterboxing. I like the "hunt." I like being in on
the "secret." I like hiking in our beautiful mountains, along our
refreshing rivers and through our awe-inspiring canyons. Letterboxing
provides me with an additional purpose for that exploration. It (and
geocaching) take me places I would never have thought of investigating
without the impetus it provides.

At the other end of the spectrum, letterboxing also includes the
opportunity to implement my creativity and artistry in a very unique
manner by planning and contructing stamps, log books and boxes, and
writing stories and clues. I enjoy some of the ancillary aspects of
the hobby, like hitchhikers, travelers, and postal letterboxing. I
like the liberating facets of the game that are limited only by our
collective imaginations.

I have met - online - many boxers who are supportive, positive, and
downright hilarious. Letterboxing allows the possibility of having
friends to whatever degree satisfies me. I could never actually meet
any of these people face-to-face and thereby preserve my anonymity. Or
I could choose to attend gatherings, learn to match faces to trail
names, and build on those relationships.

Letterboxing provides me with both physical and mental expression,
both left- and right-brain exercise. And mostly it is just a whole lot
of fun!

Boxer Lover

everyone!
