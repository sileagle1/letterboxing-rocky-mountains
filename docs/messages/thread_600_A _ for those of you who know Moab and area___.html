Re: A ? for those of you who know Moab and area...

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2005-04-02 01:59:04 UTC

There are endless hiding places in Moab. Check out the area while
you are here and see if anything calls out to you. Off-hand, I think
the Moab Rim, Hunter Canyon, Corona & Bowtie Arches, right-hand Mill
Creek Canyon, Slickrock Bike Trail, are all a few places that scream
for boxes. The area is so huge that you could easily hide them on
trails that already have boxes also. Negro Bill Canyon is long
enough to support a series of boxes. The Portal Trail has 4 boxes on
it, just to coax boxers to climb the whole thing! Hidden Valley has
three boxes on it now, by three different placers! Awww, so many
places, so little time!!!!

By the way, everyone, check the new list, I planted a new box in
Moab while the LB site was down a couple of days ago, yayyyy!!!!

Kat

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-02 04:04:48 UTC

Kat is right, there is no way you could strike out on finding a good
spot. If I were to narrow it down out of Kat's suggestions, I would
think the Moab Rim Trail was ripe for a couple of boxes.

Yesss. Right across the River from the Portal boxes and on a trail
that connects with Hidden Valley (eventually). It is kind of steep,
though, and there are a few folks that DRIVE that trail. So you would
want to keep the box away from the jeep tracks.

There is a little pamphlet called "Moab Area Hiking Trails" that is
free at the Moab Visitor Center at Main and Center Streets. That
little list of trails might save a lot of time for hopeful planters.

I'm lacing up my shoes now for the new one out Kane Creek. The last
couple of sentences of those clues have my curiosity piqued, big time.

Oh, man, I can't wait.
