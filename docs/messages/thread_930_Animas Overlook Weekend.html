Animas Overlook Weekend

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-16 18:34:24 UTC
La Paloma went hunting the boxes at Animas Overlook this weekend and I
tagged along. It felt like we had a lot of you folks up there with us
as we looked through the stamps and comments in the logbooks. It
ocurred to me that what has happened up there has surpassed all my
hopes for that little place because I never dreamed there would be
that many visitors to that obscure little trail.

Thanks to Wanda for planting the first box up there, and to Esmerelda
for having the idea to make a series. Thanks to Kat and Elros for
adding their talents.

And thanks to all of you great folks who drove up there to stamp in.

y-nought

Re: Animas Overlook Weekend

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-11-16 21:37:22 UTC
All we got from that last storm was wind and cold. But we usually do
have snow cover that stays all winter in Durango. While it doesn't
get as cold here as it gets in Denver, we don't get those warm
winter days either. The Animas Overlook road will probably be closed
from December to mid-April. Engineer Mountain, Wandering Angus, and
Esmerelda's Wings of Eagles up on the mountains have been buried for
a couple of weeks. And I saw that Aljan's area is now in hibernation.

It has been a beautiful autumn. But I'm finding it hard to let go of
the summer since it was so much FUN.

y
