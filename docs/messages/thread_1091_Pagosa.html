From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2006-05-08 00:25:11 UTC
Well, team JeLy just returned the candy wagon to the store after a
great weekend in Pagosa Springs. It turned out to be more of a sit-
on-your-butt-until-you're-waterlogged kind of weekend than a max-out-
your-F-count kind of weekend, but I did manage to squeeze a little
letterboxing in.

We found two, one of which was littlewaav's Pagosa Hot Springs box.
The box had previously been plundered by villainous geocachers who
wrote in the log, "took stamp, left 50 cents." Fortunately for us
Beans, the heroic FunHog had visited the box a mere two days before
us, removed all the geocaching ...stuff... and (get this) carved an
excellent reproduction of the original stamp right there on the
spot. She certainly deserves the Congressional Medal of Honorable
Letterboxing if you ask me. Way to go FunHog, my many thanks.

We managed to leave one, the Piedra River Hot Springs box, clues to
which will be posted soon. We also left two HHs in the area.

The highlight of the trip was MEETING (yes!) y-nought and La
Paloma. y and LP, I'm sorry our time was so short, but you are as
great in person as you are in writing. I hope to talk with you a
little more over the summer when we get back down there or you get
up here.

I left in y's care another box which we had intended to plant but
ran out of time for... tentatively called the Land of the Ancients
box. Funny thing is goddess and I forgot to stamp our logs with it
before I handed it over to y, so we will actually have to go back
down and FIND it to get our own stamp in our journals.

JeLyBean

Yes....It's True!

I did get to meet the International Bean of Mystery for a quick
conference on tricky letterboxes. I should have known there was
something up when he asked if I had ever been to Piedra Hot Springs.
My answer was "no" so this will be one of those times where I get to
go to a new place right here in my own backyard, all for a
letterbox, and clothing optional to boot! Plus he took it easy on us
and wrote the clues to this one in a human language....English even!

I was also amazed that Funhog was able to carve a replica of the
original stamp for Pagosa Hot Springs right there on the spot. I
also noticed that she wrote, "took everything else out, because THIS
IS NOT A GEOCACHE." Which means Funhog at least got to keep the
fifty cents.

Thanks for coming down JeLy, and I hope to see you on a trail in
your area before too long.

Box Naked!

After getting a note from Funhog, I am even more impressed:

".... That silly letterbox was smashed beyond recognition, had no
stamp, no plastic bags, a wet logbook and several pieces of geotrash
in it. I just couldn't leave it that way! Besides, I wanted the
stamp image in my journal, too. Heck, I even got four whole quarters
out of the deal... Unfortunately, it cost me twice that to get that
Nestle Quick box. It was actually quite pleasant to spend the time
sitting by the river carving that little stamp. I know it wasn't
exactly the same as the original but I made an effort to reproduce
it...."

Way to go, Funhog, you are my hero!
