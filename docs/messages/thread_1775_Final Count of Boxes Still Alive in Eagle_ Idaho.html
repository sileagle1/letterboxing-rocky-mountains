Final Count of Boxes Still Alive in Eagle, Idaho

From: Kelly (blackvelvetrav@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-02-12 03:07:43 UTC
As many Rocky Mountain Boxers know we have been plagued recently by vandals here in Idaho. It seems that many of the ruined boxes were planted in Eagle. The ones that were vandalized had the stamps taken and the logbooks ruined. The Pei's of Adventure and I have been very busy hitting the trail night after night to check on my boxes and below is the final count. I will admit that it is a bit depressing to find so many have been vandalized and/or taken. We have also been victims of careless boxers not re-hiding boxes well in 2009. All and all the numbers speak for themselves. I never thought I would see the day locally where I would end up removing all of my clues from both LBNA and Atlas Quest. I will also be checking my Boise and Meridian boxes but I am not overly optimistic that many of them may have been victims of the same fate as my Eagle boxes.

Here is the final count for Eagle, Idaho

30 Boxes Planted Originally
3 Retired Recently by Me
2 Were Converted (Charlie and Phish Food) to Event Boxes and are with Felix Felicis in New York
9 are Alive and Well
16 Were Taken and/or Vandalized within the last 3 months


These are the boxes that are still alive and well in Eagle:


Big Dog Series (2)
Dragon Boat Festival
Gold Fish
Grand Mater of Romance
Little Ladybug
Scentimental Romeo
The Gift of Time
Wag & Walk 2007

Blackvelvetrav

Re: Final Count of Boxes Still Alive in Eagle, Idaho

From: Kelly (blackvelvetrav@yahoo.com) | Date: 2010-02-14 03:12:40 UTC
Thank you so much for everyone's on-line hug's, good juju and unique vandal stopping ideas. As you can imagine Capperpillar and I have been on the vandals trail. We even received some hot IT information from Ryan including IP addresses and the bogus profiles of our young vandals. One of the IP addresses is located at a local high school. Let's just say we have already been in touch with the schools IT department and the principal. We take our missing and vandalized boxes very seriously!

Although I have pulled my clues off of both LBNA and Atlas Quest there is a note on each page that I will share my clues with known boxers and/or ask for references of individuals I do not know (from boxers that may have introduced them to boxing). What was very interesting is not very long after I did that I received two requests from individuals who said they were new to boxing (learned it from friends and relatives in another state) and wanted to know if they could have my clues or how they could get in my good graces. I offered to meet in person with both people to show them the ropes and/or provide them with the clues to one box so I could ensure that they were taking proper care as new boxers before providing them with more clues. As soon as I offered that to both I never heard back from either. Seems a little fishy if you ask me.

I am going to get my blog updated again tonight (I am boxing daily now so updates are VERY frequent) and today I checked on 5 Boise Boxes. Three of the Five were vandalized. I will post more pictures of the vandalized logbooks on my Blog. Heavy sigh. I really won't know which are alive and which are not until I check every last one of them. The Capperpillar clan has been great trying to check on some of my boxes for me too. They located one today that had been moved slightly that I could not locate so that was a wonderful surprise.

But no fear for the boxers we know and love. I am mentally planning new boxes (instead of trying to replace all of these) that will be worth the Boise visit to find. My carving skills have greatly improved, I am dreaming up new log books and even more challenging clues. So all of this will eventually be just a bad memory as I will put out the welcome mat and wait for you all to come and visit. The vandals will never get the best of us Boise boxers. I think I have about 25 more to check in Boise. It looks like my evenings will be spent boxing after work.

Thanks again for all the support! Blackvelvetrav, Lizman & The Pei's of Adventure
