The 'Boxing Rocked!

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-11 14:58:10 UTC

I'm at work this morning after driving back from the gathering last
night and people are asking me why I'm still doing my little happy
dance.

The Box on the Rocks was even more fun than I imagined. The weather
wasn't a factor for the gathering itself, a little breezy but very
nice. There were a few rain and wind storms that challenged the box
hunters on the trails. But I think everyone found a few. Some of the
more...uh.....passionate letterboxers found more than a few and I
would be interested in hearing some F numbers from some of them.
There are even rumors that tupperware got left behind and new clues
might begin to appear. (Woot!)

There was a lot of pie eating and saluting to Es and TDG and a lot of
other folks on this list too. We need to do this more often. Really.
And allow more opportunity for everyone to meet up. For myself, I
have some GREAT stamps in my logbook, some GREAT memories of time
spent with some really fun people, and a real boost to my excitement
level about letterboxing. I saw some logbooks that were absolutely
amazing. Both because of how many stamps were in them, and how they
were filled in with notes and pictures and pockets (suhweet!). I'm
feeling a little faint just thinking about them.

I hope everybody makes it back home safely through the crazy weather
and long drives. And I want to thank everybody for coming so far to
be part of this.

And a HUGE thanks to Kat for the Event Box, the other boxes in the
Park, the delicious pies, and for her letterboxing energy which
really makes Moab such an awesome place to meet and find letterboxes.

Huzzah!

y-nought
