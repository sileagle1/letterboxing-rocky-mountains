Carrie:

Welcome to letterboxing! =]

All of my boxes are kid friendly... we have a 21 month old and a 3 year
old - both who INSIST they get to walk.... so, when we plant, we make
them easy to get to.

We are the Treasure Finders and you can find our 9 boxes on the main LB
website. (All are around Littleton, CO)

THere are also a few others in CO that we have found... Alchemists
Lizard, Something Special for Eoghan!, Rock Crawler and Bear In
Paradise, these were all boxes that the kids went with us. Alchemists
Lizard was a little long and the 21 month old ended up wanting to be
carried for part of the trail (of course, I should mention, we went and
did this one right after we found Something Special for Eoghan).

Hope that helps =]


Debbi

From: Cattail (MLK) (mkoski@lamar.colostate.edu) | Date: 2004-06-02 22:33:58 UTC
Hi Carrie - I just snagged my first two letterboxes in Wyoming -
they were really easy to get to, and the hikes were not more than
1/2 mile in on nice, level trails. Perfect for kids, I think. The
two boxes were called "Vedauwoo" and "Happy Jack", and clues to them
can both be found at letterboxing.org on the Wyoming page. I live
in Fort Collins and I left at 9:30 am and got back around 3, stoping
for lunch in Laramie on the way home. There is a $5 entrance fee,
but I think that will cover both places. And dogs are allowed if
they are on a leash. Good luck and have fun - they are in really
pretty places (especially Vedauwoo).

Cattail :)

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2004-06-03 16:42:06 UTC
Hey there Carrie!

Welcome - glad to have you here!

All of the Treasure Finders boxes are good. Our Alchemist's Lizard
can be a little long for young ones - but the terrain is very flat.
The two at Chatfield State Park are good for younger kids. (All of
these are in Littleton, Co, by the way). Our C letterbox in
Golden/Lakewood is VERY easy to get to - on par with the Treasure
Finders boxes. Dragon's Lair and Abert's squirrel in Bailey, Co are
also very easy to get to.

Most people will say in the clues whether or not a box is canine
friendly or not, as well as how difficult the terrain is and how far.
If it is unclear, an email to the placer usually provides the needed
information.

~RavenWolf~
