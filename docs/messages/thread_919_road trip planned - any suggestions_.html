From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-11-04 03:55:58 UTC
I'm heading from Denver to Portland for Tday with family...been
printing up clues like crazy for my road trip, pulling out the
atlas, doing county research on states I don't know so well,
AtlasQuest trip planning thingy, checking out all the talk lists,
etc so I do have some ideas, but I can't possibly track down all the
LBs I want to on this trip.......my first since I found this
wonderful new addiction. :-) Leaving 2 weeks from today, will be
gone almost 3 weeks.

Just wondered if anyone has any particular boxes they'd like to
recommend ...on or off list?

I am especially interested in comments re: boxes in or very near
Portland which you might have done, as well as the Oregon
coast....the en route thing is going OK, just needs a tad of
tweaking. There are a mind boggling # in Portland itself. This
Denverite is jealous beyond belief....how does that town get so many
and Denver awaits my own efforts?? ;-p

Any thoughts/comments much appreciated!

(has one RM boxer task I volunteered for while on the road...happy
to do it....) anyone else need maintenance/etc along I-80 or I-
90)...not too far off my route? If you think you might, please
contact me off list.

Boise, ID and Ogden/Promintory, UT are definite overnight stops
westbound, still working on the route back, but thinking at least
Thermopolis, WY for my last stop before home...the rest is....Open
Road! Oh and I'll be doing some Lewis and Clark and Oregon Trail
exploration in eastern OR and ID/MT.

Thanks!

Re: Ogden/Promontory

Along with some other RML boxers, I've been working with DerMadStamper
on his Golden Spike letterbox near Promontory UT. CW Sun Seeker and I
tried to locate it and it seems the National Park Service changed the
marker numbers on the Big Fill Trail! So that the referenced marker
number in the clues no longer exists. I have located the letterbox and
emailed DMS with the new information so he can re-write the clues. I
suggest you wait until the last minute to print out this clue sheet.
This is a good letterbox and I recommend you look for it if DMS has
revised the clues before you leave for OR.

I'm sure you've checked out the LBs on LBNA and AQ for the UT counties
you'll be passing through. There aren't that many of them. I've heard
that the 2 that Frank&Nadine have in Weber County are missing, but the
others are OK as far as I know. If you need help with county
identification along your route, let me know. Also if you'd like to
meet for a stamp exchange while you are in Ogden, contact me offlist
at jowagnon at comcast.net.

Boxer Lover

If DMS doesn't get the clues changed on the Golden Spike page in time
for you to print it before you leave, I don't think he would mind if I
help you out. Let me know.

The Big Fill Trail is good. It's cool to actually be able to see some
of the construction elements. There's a booklet at the trailhead which
points out the significance of the info markers. And it doesn't take
too long. Unfortunately the NPS has stopped the re-enactment of the
Golden Spike ceremony for the season - where they bring out the 2
locomotives to meet head to head. One of the do-it-yourself car tours
is still open though, and, if you collect the NPS cancellation stamps,
they have that there along with 2 extra stamps.

I thought of another box which is probably missing in my area. I
think it's clue page indicates that. It's Utah or Bust GFCS #2 in
Davis County, just south of Ogden.

Boxer Lover




Re: [RMLetterboxing] Re: road trip planned - any suggestions?

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-11-14 11:38:03 UTC-07:00
I have gone out to Promontory out of season too and still had fun. The trail
is interesting and there are some outdoors exhibit items. I enjoyed the
trail that Golden Spike is on. It isn't too hard of a hike at all. Keep an
eye out for snakes though, although I don't think they should be too much of
a problem this time of year.

There is also an interesting outdoor exhibit of rockets and space shuttle
stuff on the road out to GS. Follow the signs. It is a tiny bit out of the
way, but can be fun if you are into that stuff. I am a bit of a space
junkie, so I liked it.

Let me know if you head up around Idaho Falls. I would be happy to point you
toward any of the Eastern Idaho boxes and most of the central ID ones. Hope
you enjoy the trip!

Cadenza
