From: myraelise (myraelise@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-31 17:49:07 UTC
I posted this over on AQ, but someone suggested I post here as well.

My husband and I will be in southern Utah from September 3-12 to tour
as many of the parks down there as possible:) Does anyone have any
recommendations on (cheap) places to stay, especially over on the SE
side now (found a decent place for the first few nights over on the SW
side)? I'm not having much luck finding places on-line, at least not
places with rooms available. We plan to spend the first few days doing
Zion and Bryce, and then will head over to the eastern side. Camping
isn't an option since there is no way we can bring camping gear.

And if anyone has recommendations of "must do" or "don't bother, not
worth it" things, please let me know. In addition to Zion and Bryce,
we hope to see Monument Valley, Canyonlands, and a couple other parks
over in that area. And maybe a brief detour to Colorado to see Mesa Verde.

And if there are any good boxes in those areas.... My husband hates
to box, but he might have to suffer through a few.

karmakat

Greetings karmakat!

Last time we went over to Zion we stayed in a motel in Hurricane. That
town is in between St. George and Zion and we liked it because there
were restaurants and frozen yogurt places to enjoy in St. George, but
we weren't too far away from Zion either. I think we stayed in a Motel
6 or something cheap like that.

The only letterbox we found over there was Tadpole Country outside of
St. George. That was great fun.

Around Moab you just take your pick.....TONS of great letterboxes.

Have fun....it sounds like a GREAT trip!

Re: Vacation in Utah

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2008-08-01 19:04:03 UTC
Hi!

I agree with Y on staying in Hurricane. It's half way between nowhere and St. George, easy
to get too, and less expensive than places right by the parks. Kanab also is a fun tiny town
to visit or stay in, near Zion. It also has Best Friends Animal Sanctuary, my daughter's
favorite place in the world. If you have to make a choice, I would spend a lot more time in
Zion than Bryce, but that's just me!

When you come towards Moab, you probably won't have a lot of choices on where to stay,
and might want to book something soooon, if anything is left! The Moab Music Festival
runs from Aug. 28 thru Sept. 13 and everything gets booked solid during that time. It's
nice to stay right in town, because you can walk around town in the evenings when it it
cooler. Most of the less expensive motels in Moab are fairly comparable, so I would just go
on one of the Moab lodging websites and check out prices and availability.

There are tons of letterboxes around Moab, Arches, and Canyonlands, and a couple on the
way to Mesa Verde, but not much towards Monument Valley.

Have fun!
KAT
