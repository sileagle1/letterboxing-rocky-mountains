Activity Permits

From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-08-31 15:17:31 UTC-06:00
I just spoke with Cathy - she is the one that will approve or deny or
applications. I asked her how we were to fill out the applications
BEFORE we placed the boxes. She said we will need to go out and find a
spot, write up the exact directions to where it is located and then send
in the application w/ the information. The rangers go out, check the
spot and then she approves or denies. We have to be specific as to
where the box will be. It can't just be a general area. Oh, and if the
clues are coded - you need to uncode them for her =]

Oh - one last thing - she mentioned that the geocachers have gotten
together and one person is handling all the permitting aspects. She
said we can do that - but again, we have to be 100% specific as to where
the box will be found. If we want to go this route, we will need to
contact her. If we don't, then they only want 1 person's boxes per
permit. Of course, if you are taking responsibility for a box with an
out of town owner, you can put that on your own permit.

Hope that helps everyone with filing out the form.
