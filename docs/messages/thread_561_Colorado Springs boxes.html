Colorado Springs boxes

From: dixssps (Dixieh7@msn.com) | Date: 2005-03-13 21:21:06 UTC

Okay, I know I haven't checked in for sometime, but, I gotta tell
you all that I feel that I need a reward for my latest letterbox
adventure....pie would be great, ES!

Anyway, we spent Friday boxing in the Springs. Gorgeous day! It
happened to be our 26th Wedding Anniversary and I couldn't have had
a funner day! Our first box, Garden of the Goddesses, awarded us as
first finders! WHOO-HOO! We were thrilled! Thanks Goddesses!
Then we stopped for the second one in the area, Red Rock Canyon.
This park is so beautiful and we had the best time.

On to Pikes Peak. Our luck changed and it appears that the Cog Rail
LB went amissing. We then went up Pikes Peak Road for the Pikes
Peak LB. More bad luck. We found the box, but NO STAMP! It
appears that the stamp has been missing since it was placed (the
First Finders wrote "NO STAMP"). So, just FYI! But, The lake was
beautiful and relatively warm day for March. But, it was getting
late....

So, we went to our last Letterbox of the day....UTE PASS!
JELYBEAN! You scared the living daylights out of me! At the very
least, I have more white hair then I did at the beginning of the
day! Now, you did WARN us with your clues, but, in our haste to get
going that morning, we printed the clues without reading them....of
course, we didn't have a flashlight! We did have our hiking sticks
and that helped immensely with the climb...wouldn't do it without
them! But, TUNNEL #3 is a very, very, PITCH BLACK tunnel and your
imagination runs wild when you can't even see a pin prick of
light!! YOW! But, the stamp was well worth it! Coincidentally, we
were married on the old D&RG Winter Park Ski Train and so the train
stamp was very appropriate for us to find on our Anniversary! Alls
well that ends well, but, I would love some pie for an extra
reward....I mean this was scarier than any FEAR FACTOR stunt!

Home safe and sound and looking forward to more adventures in boxing!

Hugs, Dixie
