Roxborough Notes & New Policy

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2005-05-15 02:06:45 UTC
Hi all, JeLyBean here. Hiked Roxborough today, found Hiker's Boot.
Thanks Puppy Paws for leaving the Farley Flyer in place. Tried for
Fountain Valley, but it appears to have gone missing. If you
haven't been, you must go! Beautiful place! Also made it by Oh
Bother (a little wet but we dried it out - great stamp by the way).
Now the real news:

I spoke with a very nice Ranger, Heather Poe, who told me Roxborough
(and other Douglas County state parks) has recently become aware of
geocaching and letterboxing and has a new policy on letterboxes &
geocaches. That policy is, basically, no letterboxes or geocaches
are allowed in the park. They know of the boxes & caches that are
already there and told me they have not removed any (and thus they
weren't the ones who removed Fountain Valley). They have taken a
wait and see approach to the boxes already in the park, essentially
to see if there is any environmental impact since Roxborough is such
a sensitive area. They may remove boxes in the future. If you have
boxes there or in other Douglas County state parks, it might benefit
you to get in touch with the park and discuss their future. Sounds
like Roxborough is on the fence right now, they realize letterboxing
can bring people to the parks, but don't want to impact the
ecology. Heather's email is heather(dot)poe(at)state(dot)co(dot)
us. I invited Heather to the talk list so she can see what nice,
environmentally aware and non-threatening people we all are :o)

If you haven't gotten Hiker's Boot or Puma Prints yet, you might
want to do so before they disappear, and make sure you drop by the
visitors center and tell them you specifically paid the $5 fee so
you could find some letterboxes.

Happy 'Boxing
JeLyBean
