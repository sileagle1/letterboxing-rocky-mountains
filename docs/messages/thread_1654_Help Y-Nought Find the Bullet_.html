From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2009-01-28 02:56:59 UTC
Okay, it is time once again for me to beg for help from our brilliant
letterboxing team, For those of you who haven't seen me do this
yet.....here is the deal:

The Snowdown Silver Bullet is a treasure hunt that is part of our
(Durango's) winter festival. The clues are broadcast over the radio
starting on Monday, 5 each day, until it is found. The first clues
are not so helpful, but they get better and better until Saturday
afternoon when they basically announce where it is. The finder (Me!)
gets a prize package worth a few hundred dollars and the helpers
(You!) get to bask in the glow of eternal pride knowing that it was
your superior letterboxing clue solving minds that got me there.

Some years the Bullet is found by Wednesday and some years it goes
through Saturday. The whole dang town is looking for it so there is
some fierce competition. But two of the last few years this group has
gotten me, literally, feet away from the prize, (DISCLAIMER-- the
person you are helping, aka me, is a total dork and has been known to
follow clues to the, how shall we say......wrong side of the bridge
ito a rattlesnake!)

So, without further adieu....the clues (with my comments in
parentheses):

1. The Snowdown Silver Bullet has been entombed, (The theme this year
is Durango in Da Nile, so everthing is ancient Egyptian...if you go
to the snowdaown website the poster has heiroglyphs on it, hmmmm).

2. The Bullet is naturally preserved.

3. Look for a slightly out of place sarcophagus.

4. The pendulum has swung back to Snowdown time once again.

5. You may have to contend with snow.

6. The Bullet was enticed to this hidden spot.

7. You will not need a ladder.

8. You will not need a shovel.

9. You could be looking right at it and still not see it.

10. Do not take it apart, we have trained experts.


So, I am open to suggestions, ideas, or further questions. I have a
couple of ideas myself, but I think our strenght is in numbers, so
I'll keep those to myself at first. Brainstorming is what we need.
The more ideas I have to check up on, the more chances I'll have at
getting on the right trail.

Woot! It's ON!

y-nought

Is this an actual tiny Silver Bullet or a Beer? My brain storming is
listed below with the clues. I don't know the area at all or the
event so I am shooting in the dark here literally. Blackvelvetrav -
Boise

1. The Snowdown Silver Bullet has been entombed. Could it be inside
a clock, timepiece, or other mechanical device, robot or statue?

2. The Bullet is naturally preserved. It is not dressed up or
embellished in any way. Simple and natural but possible inside
something else.

3. Look for a slightly out of place sarcophagus. This would be
anything that you can see inside or around.

4. The pendulum has swung back to Snowdown time once again. There
was a Roman theme in 2002. Weren't the Romans around when the
Egyptians were around too? Is there anything left around from that
event like a statue, float, framed poster, clock???

5. You may have to contend with snow. - Ski Hill, Sledding Hill, Snow
Shoe Trail, Nordic Ski Trail, ice rink, is the area covered with snow
anyway. This would lead me to guess the bullet is inside something
but sitting outside.

6. The Bullet was enticed to this hidden spot. Historical spot with
either Western, Roman or Egyptian ties. Could it be magnetized like
my Hit and Run Boxes? Have any old movies been made in the area that
might be a tourist attraction? Are there any movie or event props
being used for decorations around town? Outside a museum?

7. You will not need a ladder. It may be a bit high but within reach.

8. You will not need a shovel. It is not underground.

9. You could be looking right at it and still not see it. It blends
in very well with its surroundings. Maybe in with other metal items
so it does not stand out.

10. Do not take it apart, we have trained experts. Don't take the
item apart when you find the bullet. Could it be in a locked box?
Antique telephone booth?
