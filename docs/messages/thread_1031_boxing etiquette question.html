Re: boxing etiquette question

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-10 04:13:55 UTC
I'll put in my two cents, but I'm not much for etiquette.

As far as finding a box that had no stamp, and then asking a friend to
get the stamp image later, I would say go for it, but try to get back
later and stamp it yourself.

As far as counting it goes....that is pretty much up to you. There are
so many different ways to count letterboxes that it becomes apples and
oranges after a while. I have never heard of anybody counting in
fractions before, so you might want to try that. Maybe eleven
sixteenths of a box until you get back over there and get the remaining
five sixteenths.

As far as finding a mystery box that hadn't been attempted in two
years.......BooYa! That is awesome! Just hearing that makes me lust for
letterboxes.

Like I said......just my dos pesos,

y-nought

Oh, we're not ignoring you. Sometimes I read posts when I don't have

numerous times, "there are no rules," which is one of the things that
makes this hobby so cool. There is no Internal Letterboxing Service
