The ULTIMATE transfer technique!

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2008-10-01 16:18:51 UTC
I know that several of you here don't check out AtlasQuest, so I will
re-post here. I want all of my Rocky Mountain friends to share in my
new discovery!

One of the questions always asked is "How do you transfer a design
onto the carving material?"

Some people trace it with tracing, or carbon paper. I favor printing
it, and then using either acetone, or wintergreen oil to transfer. It
works better than tracing.

But I have found the ultimate technique! It's like you have printed
your design right onto the pink stuff! (Or white stuff, or orange
stuff...)

Custom temporary tattoo paper. Yep. Those same silly things that you
used to plaster all over your arms when you were a kid. It sticks to
the rubber just like it does your skin. You can buy blank sheets, and
run them through either your laser or inkjet printer, put them on
your carving material and Voila! Perfect, easy to see, easy to follow
crisp black lines.

http://www.texascraft.com/hps/home.php?cat=304

You can get 5 sheets for about $11. WOOT!

~RW

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2008-10-01 21:11:24 UTC
I carve on the pink stuff and the white stuff. Pink is my favorite, but
I won 4 sheets of the white stuff at Live and Breathe. =3

I have tried ironing, but I didn't like it. There is the chance of
melting the rubber, and I think you get a comparable image to using a
solvent.

Xylene and wintergreen oil are good - don't get me wrong, but the
tattoo paper is *perfect*. Literally like you have printed it right
from your printer. Even with the xylene, or acetone, or whatever, those
little details can be lost. This is just like you have printed it on
paper.

~RW
