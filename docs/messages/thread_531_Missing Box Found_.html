Missing Box Found!

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2005-02-11 22:58:46 UTC

So, in January I had a box disappear at the coast and judging by the
water marks in the area, I figured it was washed out in the storms.
So, I bit the bullit and completely remade the whole box and hid it
farther down the path and higher up. Turns out, it didn't wash out
to sea, but was pulled by a state park ranger, who passed it around
the entire district, because none of them had ever seen a
letterbox, and they loved it. They loved it and kept passing it
around. The ranger just pulled my second box and actually e-mailed
me about it this time. So now I have two boxes with identical
journals and stamps that I need to pick-up from their office. They
had no idea how to deal with letterboxes in that area, so I filled
them in on how the Colorado parks deal with it. She thought that was
a great idea, and will bring it up to the superintendant. Well, at
least I'll get the 'twins' back!

Kat (feeling aggravated but happy they survived)

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2005-02-12 20:19:20 UTC

Well, the first box was uncovered when they found it, and they were
concerned because one of the wooden steps it was hidden next too had
been pulled up. Sounds like critters to me, but they thought it
might be an overjealous person looking for the box. They said it was
in the way of water draining right there (yeah, like they have those
huge flooding storms everyday!). The second box they found because
they went to the letterboxing site to check it all out. They sounded
relieved that they could talk to rangers in Colorado and see how
things are done over there, because they have no clue how to
handle "these things". Anyway, the boxes are good ones, and I'll get
them back and turn one into a HH!

Kat (holding her piece of pie up and saying "cheers")
