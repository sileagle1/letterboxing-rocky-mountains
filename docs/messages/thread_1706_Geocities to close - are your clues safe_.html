Geocities to close - are your clues safe?

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-05-29 23:31:59 UTC
In case you haven't gotten the word via postings on some of the other sites or talk lists:

Geocities will be closing - sometime this year.

If you have clues hosted on Geocities, now would be a good time to start looking for a new home for them. Quite a few people got seriously messed up a few months back when AOL closed their webhosting site with basically no warning - and their clues hosted there disapperared never to be seen again!

When I first read the news I thought "nah, doesn't concern me" but then I remembered some of my very clues *were* on Geocities.

Here's the text on the Geocities home page.

Important Announcement
After careful consideration, Yahoo! has decided to close GeoCities later this year.

You can continue enjoying your GeoCities service until then we just wanted you to let you know about the closure as soon as possible. We'll share more details this summer. For now, please visit the help center for more information.

There's some talk of trying to help with change overs and I've volunteered to help where I can and am now on an email list on that topic. I'll post more here if/when I hear more info.

Hi, Jay Drew here, one of the Letterboxing North America webmasters. We're just touching base with the last of our members who use Geocities to host their letterbox clues. According to the latest faq from Yahoo/Geocities, there is only about one month left before your clues are lost forever. I see that you have used an interesting combo of Geocities and LbNA text in your clue scheme, love it! But I'd hate to see you lose the geocities bits.

http://help.yahoo.com/l/us/yahoo/geocities/close/close

What we are suggesting at this late date is that people consider simply cutting and pasting their clues from either geocities or your home word processor into letterboxing.org. That's a pretty easy fix to avoid losing the clues, even if you plan on setting up your own isp in the future.

We would be glad to pitch in with this annoying chore if you need some help with it, please don't hesitate to ask.

Happy boxing, Jay in CT

Re: Geocities to close - are your clues safe?

From: preboxed (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2009-09-25 18:36:52 UTC
LbNA is NOT shutting down! Geocities (a Yahoo! product) IS however, and it will affect clues linked through LbNA to Geocities web pages.

The webmasters of LbNA figured out early on just what a pain in the butt this was going to be for folks, since a lot of folks use Geocities for the clues. Everyone really "learned their lesson" when AOL's web hosting service disappeared a year or so ago without *any* notice...at all. A TON of clues were lost when AOL went "poof" with no way to get them back.

Sooo..... better safe than sorry - please take a moment and check ALL your clues and make sure all is good. I had a few I had forgotten all about.

And Y: So glad you caught this message. Wouldn't want any crying about onions.
