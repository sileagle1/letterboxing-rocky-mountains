From: cadenza74 (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-07-26 06:49:30 UTC
Funny that the boxes in Cody, WY should come up. I just spent the
weekend hunting for them and some others. It was a fun trip. I
found most of the boxes. I enjoyed the trip. The box at the post
office was missing (in Wapiti) I found the rest in the series up
until one at a ranch. I couldn't figure out quite what the clue was
pointing to and the owner came out asking questions. After that it
was very dark and I still had to get into Yellowstone and find
somewhere to sleep. I finally decided that it was too late and I
reluctantly left the last few boxes in that series.

I also planted two boxes while I was on my trip. One between the
national parks to still be okay with the rules, the other is outside
of Island Park about 15 minutes or so outside of West Yellowstone.
The box between parks is one I carved up as a last minute whim while
I was getting ready for sleep the night I stayed in Jackson before I
went out in the morning. The stamp outside of Island Park is one I
carved quite a while ago, but I have been looking for a good spot
along the Nez Perce trail. It honors Chief Joseph. I have to admit,
I'm very proud of this stamp. I purposely chose a spot along the
trail that was close to Yellowstone so that visitors might still come
hunt it down.

I also have a series planted right in Island Park. They are some of
the first letterboxes I ever planted. Unfortunately Moose Droppings
LB was dragged off by wildlife. I'm sure the cabin box should still
be fine. I haven't heard how the third box is. It was purposely
hidden right off the trail so it could still be wheelchair
accessible. I will be tickled if it is still there, but it was
a "pretty gutsy hide" (according to Don and Gwen).

The main boxes around Jackson and the parks are well documented on
the LBNA site. I believe all the ones that are currently on the site
are active still.

By the way, I have been told by Amanda and Ryan that they prefer we
not discuss (in a public forum like this) certain boxes they have
planted that aren't on the LBNA site. Just thought you might like to
know. There are rangers in NPS that have a vendetta against them-
just thought you might like to know.

Let me know when you are coming and how far down you plan on
visiting. I still have a few boxes I hope to plant, but I'm not sure
when I can. Also let me know how far down you are coming. I can
give you info on all the boxes in Idaho Falls. I also have a series
there. One of them is also missing and I plan to recarve it soon.
There was a recent report of the first box being below the water
line, but I checked it and it isn't.

Okay, there was something else, but I forgot and it is the middle of
the night. If I remember, I will post it later. I am beat, but I
will try to post the clues tomorrow to my new boxes in the area you
are visiting.

-Cadenza

Re: WY, CO, MT & ID?

From: cadenza74 (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-07-27 06:30:07 UTC
I can deliver good news! The east entrance was closed due to the
landslide, but they have it all cleaned up now and they opened the
entrance on Saturday-just hours before I went to find the
letterboxes. For once, timing was in my favor. I thought I'd have
to cancel my trip to Cody since I didn't have time to go by the
northeast gate (faster than the south gate).

If you go to Cody, a fun little thing is to go to the Irma hotel (the
site of one of the letterboxes). It's an old hotel built by Buffalo
Bill Cody himself for his daughter. I arrived there just a bit
before their 6:00 "shootout." It was cheesy, but still a fun little
tourist trap. The rodeos there are also good. There's a lot of
history in the area.

I'm sad that I had to leave a few of the Cody boxes. It was a
