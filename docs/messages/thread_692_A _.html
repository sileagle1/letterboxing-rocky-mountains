From: Lisa Cook (lisadcook@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-23 10:35:53 UTC-06:00
I like to see the history in the clues/that way I can read it at my leisure (in case its dark/cold or rainy when I stamp in). I love history that is PART of the clues if possible. Esmereldas clues for example have history in them (and also in the log books I think) and some that are fictionial stories. The Zebediah Gump series are stories that SOUND like history that really make the clues fun (my daughter loved them-she wants to find all of them because they were fun.) So Id say put the history in the clues AND in the book.
Just my 2cents.

Re: [RMLetterboxing] Re: A ?

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-05-23 17:31:29 UTC-06:00
I like info that I can read early if it is long and will take a while to
read, but it can be cumbersome to a clue. I once had a mystery box that I
found that had an insert about the history that I enjoyed in the box, but it
didn't take forever to read. It made for an interesting box. I might
laminate it to protect it from the elements. I guess my answer is . . . hmm
. . . I don't know. I was going to vote for not in the box and in the clue,
but I'm not as sure now. I don't want a novel in the box though.
The way this mystery box occurred was fun for me as far as history goes.
The clue was incredibly vague (It was only one word in the name, then the
clue itself was just a small diagram that showed you where to find the
hiding spot once you got there. The diagram did not reveal anything about
the clue) The fun thing about this clue was that in order to look up the
box, you had to delve into history and figure out what the name meant. I
read up on quite a bit of history before I figured out for sure where the
box was. It made it really fun to find it and then getting there and seeing
a little bonus history was nice.
Okay, I'm rambling now. I need to head off for a get-together with friends.
I will talk to you all later.
Cadenza
