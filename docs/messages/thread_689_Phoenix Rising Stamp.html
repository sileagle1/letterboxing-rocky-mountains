And that stamp may stay in that spot for quite some time, probably
forever! I had planned on hiding it on this other wonderful trail,
but the river is flooding the trailhead right now, and from the
looks of things, it may not go down for a looooong time. They are
talking about the Colorado flooding it's banks here for the first
time in 23 years, and our daily river trips down the Westwater
section are getting downright scary! Our company has had two
evacuations off the river just this week!

Thanks for the offer of Fiery Furnace pictures, but I think I see it
too much as it is! (just got home from there an hour ago, 96 degrees
today!)

Puppypaws, I think a key to being outdoors in the really intense
heat of summer, is to start early when it is relatively cool and
warm up with the heat, then you don't notice it as bad. Once you
step into an airconditioned car, house, or building, it's all over,
and you'll need many pie flavored snow cones to cool you off! And
yes, the neck coolers seem to work a bit!

Yes, Es, we have banana creme, green apple, chololate creme, and all
sorts of pie flavored snowies here, today I opted for banana-
strawberry since it's 96 degrees today and supposed to be hotter
tomorrow!!! I don't know how Y's green apple tasted, but it sure was
a pretty color!

Kat (wondering where the cool spring weather went so fast)
