Moab visit

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-06-02 04:45:14 UTC
Hi all,
I spent a scorching couple of days in the fair city of Moab this past
weekend and found some boxes I had to relegate to the waiting list
during the April gathering due to lack of time - so many boxes! Also
found a few of the new boxes left by out-of-towners at the event.
Kennamtn's Negro Bill: What a wonderful canyon to hike - more
wonderful on the way up in the cooler morning than on the trek down in
the midday heat. Although there were many folks on the trail and at
the bridge, not one ventured to the west end while I was stamping in!
Nice stamp. I hope I was able to avoid the plentiful poison ivy -
VERY allergic. I appreciate all the warnings.
Kennamtn's Arches: It felt funny being so exposed while stamping
in! But I doubt anybody on the highway took notice of me. Another cute
stamp. I found my first HH here! And left it at another LB in the
area.
Kat's SnowShoe Kokopelli: It seemed a prudent idea to get out of
the 95 degree heat by heading for higher ground. No snow shoes
required to find this clever stamp today. Great views. I was
disappointed that the road to the Warner CG was still closed, so will
have to keep that box on the to-do list. The drive over the LaSal Loop
is breathtaking!
A-Bear's Art on the Rocks: Cute petroglyph character. Lots of
activity in the area for Memorial Day weekend; bikes, motorcycles,
ATVs, trucks with trailers. But I remained unseen from the road here.
Good spot.
Blackvelvetrav's Kokopelli's Hole in One: Who'd have ever
thought golf was that old! Delightful stamp. It's surprising with
all the comings and goings that no one stopped to look at the Birthing
Rock; maybe they've already seen it.
Aljan's Phoenix Rising: Those of you who have not yet been here
are in for a treat! Fantastic box: stamp, journal, location. This one
has it all!
I will upload some pertinent photos to this site after I resize them.
Lastly, I have placed my very first LB in your area. Hope you like it.
It's called When a Frog is a Horse.

News flash! I was just typing this when I got an email from Kat. She
and Kennamtn have already found my box! What a hoot! Their experience
with Onion Creek was exactly what I intended! Ok, now the rest of you
get out there.

Boxer Lover
