From: (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-10-06 00:55:30 UTC-06:00
Hi Ewok,

sorry it's taken a bit. I will try to tackle some of the boxes to let you
know, and hope I haven't missed you before your big trip. I will be
impressed if you make it out to all these areas, but since I don't know your
itinerary, I will try to include all I can remember off the top of my head.
I just wrote a brief summary of the UT boxes for someone else, so I'll past
that for you. It wore me out though (it's been a long week) so I don't
think I will feel up to doing the same for all of the boxes in each of the
states. I will try to give you any pertinent info.

Idaho: First and foremost, I would love an exchange :-) Feel free to let
me know when you are heading this direction and if you are interested we can
meet for an exchange. (How's that for self-serving?) If I have time, I will
also try to get out to replace a couple of my stamps that have been dragged
off my animals or other mammals. I'm in the Idaho Falls area.

Eastern: All of the boxes around Idaho Falls are intact and great finds for
families. There are at least seven boxes that just follow the park that is
along the river in the downtown area-easy finds. Two boxes are hidden by a
cave outside IF, they were easy finds and the kids might get a kick out of
the cave, but I wouldn't recommend going after dark. Swan Valley is MIA. It
is my first plant ever and is in a beautiful spot, so I will definitely
replace it as soon as I can. Swan Valley is on the same highway as Plant One
for Me and Elk Reserve in WY, they are still good. One of my three Big
Springs Boxes was dragged off by an animal, but it's marked in the clues
now. Big Springs is right on the road to Yellowstone if you are going in
the West gate (in Island Park). The Gem State letterbox is in a rest stop
that can only be reached on northbound I-15 and there have been some times
that the exit is closed due to construction. So keep your eyes out if you
are on the way, but don't go out of your way for now unless you have some
extra time.

Central: If you have time to get out there, I am very proud of my Land of
the Yankee Fork Series and the Sunbeam Springs and Dam Letterboxes that are
near it are fun too. They are near Stanley or Challis north of the Sawtooth
Recreation area. I think they are a great family stop because they are in
old ghost mining towns and show you a touch of U.S. history. I also did a
few boxes around Salmon. They weren't my best stamps, to be honest, but the
area is pretty, especially if you drive a bit north of Salmon into the
mountains. In that area is also a box that was planted for Don and Gwen at
the confluence of the Salmon River. It is a ways out (I ran out of time and
had to turn back) and Don and Gwen aren't sure it is still out there, if you
try and it is, let me know. There are a few other boxes that I haven't had
time to find yet, but I believe they are still good. Most of the Idaho
boxes are pretty easy finds. Letterboxing is relatively new here, so we are
just beginning to get some of the more involved boxes.

A-bear would be able to tell you more on the Boise area boxes. She knows
them better than I.

Western WY: Don and Gwen's boxes should all be good still. They are pretty
recent and they don't usually make you hunt too far. I have found Elk and
Jackson's Elk. Don't be too strict on the compass markings for Elk. They
didn't seem quite on to me, but we still found it easily and learned about
wildlife in the area while we were at it.

Make sure you check the mystery boxes in ID and WY. You may want to contact
Aryn (A-Bear) to see if she replaced hers. The others are all still good
and in interesting spots. I found the WY mystery box just a few weeks ago.
You may want to research them while you are still home with a computer on a
couple of the clues. They are easy once you know the general location. I
have been told that a certain lunar letterbox is a little tricky to decipher
once you are there. I haven't been out for that one just yet, but I hope to
go very soon after I return from San Diego this weekend.

I can't tell you much about the MT boxes yet or the other WY ones. All the
boxes I've found so far are family friendly and relatively simple. You will
have to let me know how it goes for you. Also, contact me off-list. I have
a few tips you might find helpful for some other boxes. This has turned
into a long email. Let me know if I've missed any and I will be glad to let
you know about them-it's late and I many have skipped a bit.

Best of luck and enjoy your trip.
Cadenza

ps. Here's the UT list too


The Rings-easy, as clue states it's right off the highway, take stamping
markers if you have them, it works better with the stamp. Rehide well as the
location is a bit exposed.

Golden Spike: historic box-1st in UT. It's out of the way, but a very
interesting location (at the spot where the world's first transcontinental
railroad was joined-make sure you go out to the visitor's center). I'm glad
I have it. Watch your step there are some snakes in the area. I haven't
heard of any biting incidents though.

Kwagunogwai: Great park and a fun little spot. I may well have driven it
wrong, but the driving instructions didn't leave me in quite the right
place, but it was very close though and I found it by exploring a bit. The
box was easy to find once I realized that the downed tree on a corner of the
trail wasn't the one the clue was speaking of. Keep going as the clues
direct and you will be able to tell you are in the right spot. I only
include this specific of guidance because I was able to find it without too
much difficulty, but I know that Don and Gwen drove all the way out there
and turned around in a spot just a few feet from the actual letterbox
thinking they were leaving the end of the trail if they went further. The
spot they described as giving them some difficulty had also thrown me for a
few, but once I pushed on, I found the spot clear as could be, just as the
clue described. (I hope I haven't given any spoilers here; I tried not
to-Heather, if you're reading this, I loved your box.)

A Walk in the Park: Great walk, lovely park, and relatively easy finds. I
enjoyed this one quite a bit when I found it last month. The added bonus is
it is right in downtown SLC. You don't need a car to get there.

Butterfield Canyon, Bingham Copper Mine, and Atoka are all my plants. The
first two were some of my earliest plants and stamps and I have had a couple
people tell me that they felt a little nervous stopping for the mine box,
but they told me they loved getting to see an interesting place they
probably wouldn't have gone to otherwise and the box was probably alright
for now. There wasn't much traffic the day I went and security was all over
the top, so I put it off the roadside and didn't notice that there were
signs saying not to stop on the road. You can get it in a moment though and
not have though. I think it's okay for now, but I may move it if I get a
chance to go back out there. I've been learning how to do better since!
Butterfield Canyon is just a bit south of the mine entrance on the same
highway that the mine entrance is on. Atoka was a bit more recently done
during a break at a training meeting I had. The drive up Ogden Canyon was
nice.

I hear Teddy's waterhole is still there, but I haven't had a chance to find
them yet.

I think that hits the Northern UT boxes. I hope this helps a bit. Now I
just need to head to the south.
