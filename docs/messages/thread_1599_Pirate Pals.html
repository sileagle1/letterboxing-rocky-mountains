Pirate Pals

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-09-16 08:00:10 UTC
Gar! Who's the luckiest pirate in the nation? Me! ::points to
self:: ARRRR!!

It whar a fine mornin'....the sky was as blue as an oxygen deprived
pirate and the Pearl shone like a... uh.. pearl.. in the bright
September sun. I raised anchor and sailed for the shore, singing
chantys and enjoying fine salty snacks all the way.

I sailed to a secret location and did a jig at the sight before me.
The silver mist hung heavy in the sea air...thick as something really
thick, it rolled along the sand and carressed the sides of the Pearl.
Soon, a little white schooner from Utah dropped anchor beside me and
the fun began! First the hugging! Boxerlover! ::hug hug::
CWSunseeker! ::hug hug::

Sailing home from Live and Breathe, the brilliant pair was making a
stop to find Buccaneer Gold and invited me to join them. Yar... I
loves a good treasure hunt! Using great boxer skills, BL had solved
the location for the mystery box.. the clues which can only be found
in a letterboxer trading card. In a cloud of maps, notes, google
earth print outs and history books, she waved the solution
triumphantly in the air and, singing A Pirates Life for Me in three
part harmony we hit the sand and headed for the treasure! Being the
planter I had a pretty good idea of where the bounty lay, but was
sworn to zippin my lips and along just for the ride.

On the way to the secret hidee hole we got distracted by some tide
pools. We poked starfish, squished anenomes and tapped on thousands
of mussels. There is a vague possibility that we were caught by an
evil sneaker wave, but you'll never know! HAR HAR HAR!

All poked and squished out, it was on to the treasure. They nabbed
the first finder prizes.. woo!

We zoomed off to find another local box and ran into some other
boxers there. Then off to Roseburg for dinner and lots and lots and
lots of chatting.

It were a little thing I like to call a dang good day.

Congrats to BL for solving the mystery!

And thanks to BL and CWS for sharing the day with me! So much fun to
see old friends and share some of my favorite places with them.

Now.. who's coming up next?!

Es, who would be happy to show people how she makes stuff, but
they'll have to come up here to see it!

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2008-09-19 17:28:02 UTC
Visiting with our southern CO resident pirate on foreign shores was
truly the icing on the cake for a fantastic 'boxing road trip that
encompassed 2239 miles, Live & Breathe, ocean beaches, lighthouses,
cool green forests, open plains, Crater Lake, a forest fire and a 200-
mile detour around that forest fire. We barely scratched the surface
in nine very full days; it would easily take a month to do the route
justice. It was fun seeing other RML buddies, too. Seems odd to
travel so many miles to see our 'neighbors,' but we will take our
treats wherever we can. Now I need some time to recuperate from my
vacation!

BL47
