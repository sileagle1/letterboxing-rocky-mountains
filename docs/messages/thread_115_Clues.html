From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2003-10-17 06:26:39 UTC
With all the fuss on the big letterboxing message list about posting
the status of boxes missing and found into a big database, I thought
I'd pose the following question.

I have begun posting the clues to boxes (colorado only so far) in
the "files" section of this message board. This is not to post the
status, but to have a way to access clues in our area should the LbNA
website ever go down for any reason.

Clues are being posted exactly as written on the LbNA website (or
linked site) with no alterations. Is this okay with you guys??? If
anyone objects to this (it's going to be a long, slow process to get
the boxes listed) I will gladly refrain from posting your clues.

(trying to stay out of hot water)
Autumn

From: Eoghan (eoghan@sharawadgi.com) | Date: 2003-10-18 00:49:33 UTC
--- In RMLetterboxing@yahoogroups.com, "Autumn" wrote:
If anyone objects to this (it's going to be a long, slow process to
get the boxes listed) I will gladly refrain from posting your clues.
(trying to stay out of hot water)

Autumn,
I appreciate what your doing but I would not like for my clues to be
posted this way. One of the things I like about letterboxing (as
opposed to geocaching)is that the LbNA site will link directly to an
offsite clue. This allows me to update, change, or remove the clue as
necessary without having to chase down various versions of the clue
in different places.
Maybe an alternative to copying _all_ the clues is to copy those that
are on the LbNA site (where people have relinquished control of the
content), and to create documents with the link only to offsite clues
(where people have preferred to keep control of the clues). That
works as a backup for the main site but keeps the control,
formatting, etc. of offsite clues in the hands of those who've chosen
to list their clues that way.

Thanks again (no hot water, just a request)
-Eoghan

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2003-10-30 05:05:05 UTC
My two cents: I think it is a great idea to have a backup, however,
I also agree with Eoghan that there needs to be a kind of
configuration control. In the past, people have set up their own
pages to be able to edit and maintain their own clues. But now, the
new LbNA web site allows you to do this on the national site, so I
keep all my clues there. I have on several occasions gone into the
site and edited my clues, added warnings about missing boxes,
corrected errors, or made the clues clearer. How will you ensure
the RML backup has the latest version? Without some kind or
updating or renewal, you run the risk of having outdated clues on
the RML site.

JeLyBean

From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2003-10-30 06:30:21 UTC
Good Point Jely (and welcome to the board, by the way)

I didn't really have a plan in mind when I began posting the backup
version of the clues. Only that I was worried that some of
the "unclaimed" boxes might be removed, and if something were to
happen to the LbNA system, we'd still be okay on some level. (I
probably should sell insurance for a living or something, eh?)

Anyway - Here's one solution for boxes in CO and elsewhere in the
rockies - particularly your own boxes ... These backup clues are just
Word documents. I believe that anyone can add or delete them (if
that's not the case, let me know and I'll fix it so everyone can, I'm
just too sleepy to check it before I post this message!)

If something about your box changes - just delete the file and post a
new one. For the folks who are not on this board, well - I guess its
a risk. I suppose the alternative is to just rely on the lbna
board.

For those folks who keep their clues on their own websites and
provide links to lbna ... I think it would be great to have those
links posted here as well. Perhaps just the link to your main site
to where folks can then go and find your clues.

I'm rambling and exceptionally tired ..
Off to bed,
Autumn
