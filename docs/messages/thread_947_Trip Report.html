Trip Report

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2005-12-10 02:18:45 UTC
Hiya:

So in scanning the archives, I see there isn't much of a tradition
of posting trip reports to any extent - for Major Road Trips with a
Boxing Purpose. I'm a life-long world traveler, and some of the
sites I subscribe to do have this tradition...in exchange, I
suppose, for help/assistance/encouragement given PREvious to a
trip....

As a newbie here/and a frustrated writer on this talk list, may I be
permitted to try it?

....when I can't box, I amuse myself.......sewing/thinking about
boxes for next year/etc.

If it's too much - please say so or just ignore this post.. and I'll
stop!

In the meantime, for anyone who cares to read on....

and yep, there IS Pie.....eventually....

***

Haven't Got a Clue and Frozen Crap.

My Thanksgiving Road Trip 2005 from Denver to Portland and back in
13 days and 3232 (.9!)miles..and doubling my letterboxing count, by
the way, a first and last time I'll get to say this.. newbie that I
am, but fact's is facts!

Sorry parental ones..the "Cr*p" word, was the best I could come up
with..just nowand it's quite intentional and indicative. Ya'll
will see why, very soon ..if anyone cares to read on....

***

Everyone who really knows me wouldn't believe that I was literally
pulling away from the curb at 6 a.m. that Friday the first day of
my first "real" vacation in 3 years.. But, those same folks would
know just how much traveling (and significant time off to do so)
means to me and that I CAN do stuff when I want totravel!
Heck, "only 2 weeks", pre???

I don't usually get out my passport/driver's license for less that 4
months.

The story that must now be told is that I should have known what the
theme would have been for the trip that very first morning. I
didn't of course, but I sure do now.

***

Car loaded the night before and having gloated about the trip for
weeks before hand, the morning of trip jitters (oh and the cats)
made sure I was awake, without alarm clock at 5 a.m. and raring to
go! Shower, coffee for the road, and last minute items (mostly
food) to be packed.

Then to the car arms loaded down.

Every single door of the little green Subaru was frozen shut, even
the back tailgate which has no glass to rubber seal. Not just a
little stuck haha, snort grrr! Tried kicking the metal part of
the door with a boot to try and break the seal between the glass and
the ice. Prying the window/rubber seal with car keys? Nope! It
hardly ever gets this cold in Denver and there was no snow on the
ground. But then again, I'm hardly ever up at 6 a.m. so what do I
know? I do know that several years ago I did finally break down and
buy a hair drier. I didn't bother to use it that morning, like most
mornings. Extra time and mornings are not two things usually
associated together in my life.

It took about 10 minutes of prying to finally get a door, any door
open to get into my car. My wet hair now frozen solid to my head, I
wondered how many of my neighbors woke up with the sound of
profanity that morning?

Then on to southern Wyoming. And a headwind. All day! Grrrr. All
10 hours of it to make it to the Ogden/Brigham city area. Tried for
several letterboxes to make nice stops along the way. Struck out
several times including one I really wanted, but since I'm still
new to boxing, I think my patience for actually finding some when
I'm in the right area still needs some work. My comments re:
attempts at finds reflect ONLY my own frustration/nebieness-dom, and
are NOT in any way directed at the placers of those boxes!

Der Mad Stamper's Lil Bug's Bungalow at Expedition Island where John
Wesley Powel set off for his Colorado River journey That location
has been on my list to visit for years and the weather was chilly,
but clear so all was not lost. A big huge rock was there, placed
since the place date of where this box sure seemed to be, sat where
the bearings lead me.

Encounter on the Oregon Trail, Don and Gwen a 40+ mile round trip
detour for nothing. I'm kicking myself for not stopping and really
registering the mileage in the clues when I didn't find the concrete
markers.I saw some brown metal road signs up ahead so went for
them.by then, I'd lost the .erm trail..sigh.In this case, so
much sage brush, so little time - which I didn't have. "The Oregon
trail, California Trail, Mormon Trail, and the route for the Pony
Express" all meet in one place (like SO many on my trip) and I
missed this box which really wanted. I never did find out where to
get more info on the "audio tour" mentioned all those signsit
probably would have broken up the bad spots on the radio dialI
asked at several National Parks, etc, en route on what the Audio
Tour was "all about." Many rangers tried, but could never
satisfactorily answer my Q....

Home on the Range, Heather YAY my first actual find for the 1st
day. Yipee! A nice little local park just off the highway. Wacky
geese and ducks with funky Mohawk hairdos greeted me when I got out
of the car. My first inclination that although in our normal
everyday boxing worlds "just off the interstate" boxes aren't of
interest.......on Road Trips.....Not True!

Imaginary Roadside Attractions: Improbable Parks/lisascenic. The
description in the clues about how this box came to be was reason
number #1 to seek it out. giggles! Been in touch with the placer
we talked bout significant detours "off route" to find nada, and
how folks should visit this box more often.come on you KNOW
you're bored to the point of suicidal at this point in the road!

The camping spot I'd planned on was right by the interstate. Ick!
They did have nice holiday lights set up at the park right by the
Great Salt lake.

The next morning I realized that I'd been feet away from The Rings
box by A and J Bears. And I even Had The Clue printed up for this
one on me already to goThis would also become a theme
Grrr. But it Sure Beats Working!

And that was only Day One

more soon-ish...

find it on 11-19-2005. Yah!

After CW Sun Seeker and I failed to find it last fall, I contacted Der
Mad Stamper. He related as much as he could remember about seeing the

should be. (To his credit, his memory was right on the money.) A-Bear
provided the map from when she had found the box (she must keep
EVERYTHING!) and guess what? The NPS has re-written the trail's tour
booklet and RE-NUMBERED THE INFO MARKERS!!! The marker referred to in
the clues is now in a completely different place. Indeed, there is NO
marker now within sight of the box location. Now armed with the old
map, CWSS returned to the trail and found the box. She emailed its
location to me and then I found it, too. I emailed new clues to DMS,
but he has been busy with stuff and hasn't yet updated the clues on
LBNA. He gave me permission to send the new clues to HER since we knew
Golden Spike was on her itinerary. So a group effort and many emails
later, it looks like it was a success! Way to go, HER!

Day Two of Road Trip: Part One

Found my first box placed by someone else in Central Denver today
(12/22/05) on the way home from work today.(Rocky Mountain Choo
Choo/Treasure Finders check it out and YAY!)

Today we were sent home early..to 11 whole paid days off! Wahoo,
don't mind if I do..

As promised, here's the next update, particularly at the urging of
Cadenza ;-) Whose stamp I got to see for the first time on this
tripbut that's much later

***

Day Two belongs, boxing-wise, completely and utterly to Boxer
Lover..! and my own style of dumb freaking LUCK! Oh, and Pengy.

I attempted to honor Boxer Lover when I did, in fact, log into
Golden Spike. Without BL's postings on this list, and a few private
emails before my trip I never, ever would have found this box. I
really, really, wanted this one. Boxer Lover is My Hero!!!!!!!!

Some of you may remember that I posted PREtrip that this box in
Promontory was on my list of "must haves" and definite stopovers.
This National Park has been on my "to do" list ever since I moved to
Colorado..in 1999, and having recently become addicted to boxing,
well, ya'll know the rest.

So, I'm also into penguins. Since this trip was a solo trip, at the
very last minute on that very cold morning when I set off, I decided
to bring Pengy with me.He's an approximately 9" tall stuffed
penguin on the trip, with a homemade stylish fleece neck gatorof
course!.Which is what I've been sewing "to beat the band"in larger
sizes, of course. Trip now officially paid off.

The truckers on my tripped noticed him on the dashboard many times.
One of my many philosophies of life..If no one else can/will amuse
you, do it yer own danged self.snort

Musta been the weather that cold morning. Penguins can't fly, don't
ya know so Pengy makes me drive him everywhere. He navigates (and
is crap at ithe wants to go everywhere and doesn't understand the
concept of Time Mangement!) I also think Pengy might also be
stealing my PREquent flier miles for vacations to warm places like
Hawaii, cause I sure haven't been redeeming them lately

So on the road from Ogden to Promontory there is a Bird
Sanctuary..I never knew that.Pengy got excited! We both wished we
could have stopped to explore. If anyone is interested in placing a
punny penguin to honor a bird from Antarctica.stamp in a bird
sanctuary located in desertlemme knowI'll carve the stamp!


I spent the 2nd morning of my trip talking to the Rangers at the
Golden Spike NP, doing some hikes and auto tours. Tried to find out
information on some of the Audio Tours noted with brown signs I'd
passed many times on the interstate. The ranger, bless them, bored
in off season, even got on the internet, printed me off a bunch of
stuff for other NPs and Welcome Centers, but could never hook me up
with info on what I wanted. If anyone on this list knows more, love
to hear from ya.

I'd highly recommend stopping by the visitor center before going for
the Golden Spike box . Of course you need to find the NEW TRAIL
marker for this box the numbers aren't even close to what's up on
Der Mad Stamper's clue sheet, but the interpretation in the guide,
imho, really makes the hike relevant from a historical perspective
even if (gasp!) there weren't even a box in the area. For those of
