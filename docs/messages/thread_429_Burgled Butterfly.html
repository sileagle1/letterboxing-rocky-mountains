Re: Burgled Butterfly

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2004-11-20 04:05:01 UTC

Thanks all for your kind words about the missing butterfly. This is
my 6th to 8th box that has gone missing (not sure about a couple of
them since they were in other states). One may have been trashed by
a construction crew, one appears to have been buried under a
decaying tree, one grew permanently into the root ball of a scrub
tree that popped up out of nowhere, one appears to have been dragged
over the edge of a cliff by some rodent. But at least four of these
appear to have been deliberately stolen.

Every time one of these happens, I ask myself, who would take that?
There's nothing of value in there, the stamp and log book couldn't
possibly be considered "cool" by teenage stoners stumbling upon the
spot... I mean, I'd expect kids to vandalize it more than out and
out steal it... I just don't get it.

But anyway, I have come to accept the fact that letterboxes do not
have permanence. That's one of the reasons I don't replace my
missing boxes, I view them as sort of temporary art that only a few
people will get to appreciate before they disappear. I think that
in a way that makes it a little more special to the people who have
that stamp in their log book. So, get 'em while they last, folks...

JeLyBean, having a beer and philosophizing on a Friday night...
