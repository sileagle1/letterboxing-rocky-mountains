From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-05-30 22:19:10 UTC
As far as I know there is nothing that prohibits letterboxes on
National Forest land, and that's where a lot of my letterboxes are.
They , and the BLM, are quite a bit more relaxed about how they
manage public lands. There isn't anywhere near the concentration of
people that the National Parks have. Plus they give out grazing,
mining, and logging permits to various contractors. In my opinion
these are much better places for letterboxing than Parks and
Monuments.

Just my two cents,
