From: Scott Talbot (scott_katytalbot@comcast.net) | Date: 2009-02-10 16:49:50 UTC-07:00
Well, lucky you. I actually am the resident expert on Windsor for the
Rocky Mountain Letterboxing group. I was born and grew up in Fort
Collins and moved to Windsor in 2000 after I got married. Windsor is a
great little town of around 18,000 people and is only about a 15 minute
drive to Greeley to the east, Loveland to the southwest, and Fort
Collins to the northwest. Therefore, everything is fairly close if you
can't find it in town. We have 2 large grocery stores with Walmart
coming next year along along with many of the usual fast food and a few
restaurants. Now is a good time to buy in Windsor because the house
prices have fallen a lot (bad for me but good for you). Taxes are
higher than I wish in Windsor because of a smaller number of large
businesses to draw taxes from and therefore the homeowners taxes help
foot the bill. An advantage however is that there is not any city sales
tax and therefore if you purchase something out of town like a vehicle,
you will only pay state tax at 3.2% or something like that. The school
district is VERY good and does not seem to have much of any drugs,
violence, or gang activity like you would see in some of the other towns
around. The community is very friendly and helpful as you can see from
the response to the tornado that tore thru here last May. I'm not sure
about the home rule thing, but I think that just has to do with the city
government and who controls the city I believe since small towns depend
a lot on the state. I believe that we switched to city government run
in the last few years. Windsor can have it's wind but is nowhere as
cold and windy as Wyoming and the weather is pretty nice most of the
time. Many people do commute from Windsor to Wyoming or to Denver for
work and therefore we are considered a "bedroom community" because many
people commute to other locations for work. This should tell you that
the area, the town, the schools, and the community must be pretty
special for people to choose to commute from here I would think.

Do you think I should apply for the Chamber of Commerce? Anyway, let me
know if you have any specific questions about Windsor and I'd be happy
to let you know.

Ramdelt
