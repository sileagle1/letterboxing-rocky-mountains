Re: New poll for RMLetterboxing

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-05-20 03:02:27 UTC
Even though I don't live in Colorado I thought I would
give my 2 cents worth on this. Since I don't know
the difference between Front Range, Western Slope or
High Country (your state is ALL high to me), I suggest
you just divide the state into 4 quadrants (NE, SE,
SW & NW), especially since it is a rectangle. It
might make it easier for out-of-staters to pick a
region. We have a similar problem with Texas; for
example the Coastal Region extends all the way from
the southern tip to the eastern boundary, a huge distance.
So if somebody is coming to any place on the coast, they
will see many boxes that are hundreds of miles away.
We chose to break the state up into smaller size
regions that make sense geographically. Maybe if
you and other states start sending in requests, they
will start working on them. Good luck!

Silver Eagle

From: (scott_katytalbot@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-05-20 03:19:41 UTC
That's a nice thought but really doesn't work too well for our state. Things would be very tough to put into one of these areas because the major cities all lie along the interstates that divide the state north from south (I-70) and east from west (I-25). Look at the Denver area for instance, would it be north, south, east, or west? It probably lies where most people would consider the middle point of the state. I would still stick with Front Range, Western slope, High Country, Southeastern Colorado, and Eastern Colorado.
