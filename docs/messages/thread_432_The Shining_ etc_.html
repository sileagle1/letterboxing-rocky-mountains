From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2004-11-19 18:13:58 UTC-07:00
The Catamount Carvers have come out of their temporary hibernation. We
went in search of two boxes today. We found The Shining in Estes Park,
but its box was crushed beyond repair. Since the owner is in Illinois,
we decided to pull it for repair. We should have it back in place on
Sunday or Tuesday. We'll send another e-mail when it's back in
commission.

The Shining Letterbox was crushed by its own top covering rock. We've
seen that happen to a few of those more disposable storage containers.
In comparison, the older, standard Rubbermaid boxes hold up incredibly
well.

We also took a hike up Crosier Mountain near Glenhaven to find the Rocky
Mountain Wedding Letterbox. It was a wonderful find with everything in
perfect order!

If the weather cooperates, we should be getting out a box or two of our
own in the coming week!

~The Catamount Carvers

Unless something new has happened to The Shining box in Estes Park, it
should be there. We pulled that box about November 19th and had it back
in place on November 24th. We sent a note to this list on both occasions
and was also in contact with the box owner. The owner obviously hasn't
taken the "pulled for repair" note off letterboxing.org. I'm very sorry
that we didn't see your e-mail earlier, but we were out of town. I will
try to contact the box owner again to inform her of this problem.

We were down in Arizona this past week and found some very nice
letterboxes in Tucson. I must say that one of the boxes was a murderous
hike with fairly unclear clues and lots of steep grade and cholla choking
our way. We gave up at one point, as we feared not getting back to the
car before dark. It's the first time our kids have had any complaints
about "letterboxing." We've had them on plenty of big hikes, but the
cacti factor put them over the edge. Oddly enough, we got it anyway!
The stamp was really cool ... hand-carved with a handmade scroll.

Happy Holidays!
