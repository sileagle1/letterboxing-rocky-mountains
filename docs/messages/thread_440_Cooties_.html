From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-12-09 19:39:21 UTC

Well, let me start with a disclaimer and say that I only know what I
have learned from the thread on Atlasquest.

They were invented by Pandora et al as a fun thing for kids to do at
gatherings or exchanges. You give people Cooties in person by
slipping them into their hand, pocket, backpack, etc... You DON"T
leave them in letterboxes. When you get Cooties you stamp in with
your fingerprint, so kids without personal stamps can play, and then
you try to give Cooties to someone else.

This wouldn't work very well for me because I've never met another
letterboxer in person (besides those in my family). So I will have to
get Cooties the old fashioned way......by being kissed by a
girl.....ewwwww!

I thought the whole cooties thing looked fun, too, but I'm not into
the fingerprint thing. I know one group of people have started a
kind of hybrid cootie where you use stamps, but it looks like the
originators aren't too keen on it. Of course, that whole getting
close enough to another letterboxer to slip the cootie into their
pocket thing is the real challenge. Skeetch would be all, 'dang it,
Es, stop putting these in my golf bag!' And I'd whine and say, 'but
you're the only letterboxer around here for a billion miles!' And
he'd say, 'I'm not a letterboxer! Now stop it!' And I'd chuckle
because I just stuffed another cootie into his tool box.

When we come down the mountain from visiting the bard, everyone will
be infested with cooties.
