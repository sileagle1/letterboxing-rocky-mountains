A quantity question about placing boxes

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-06-13 18:47:01 UTC
Okay.. since I don't seem to have a life and am enjoying spending all
my free time (and some of my not supposed to be free time ) making
boxes to place, a little question has pushed itself to the front of
my brain.

How many is too many in one area? I'd like to do several here where
I live, Palisade, but it is a VERY small town. Sort of like Mayberry.
Is there a saturation point that takes the fun out of it for
searchers? If you could find, say, 3 or 4 boxes in a couple of hours
would that be too many?

All the boxes I've done are historical and have something to do with
where they are planted. All the stamps are handcarved and all the
journals handmade. They all have first finder stuff. So I'm not
trying to pump out quantity versus quality. But is there some kind
of guideline for how far apart they should be to keep them
interesting?

Thanks for any help/suggestions/comments!

Esmerelda

Well, I'd say, as long as they not one after the other, all within 5
feet of each other, do it the way that you'd want to find them..... Do
you want 300 boxes in a single square mile? Is it all about the
quantity or the quality? I prefer quality over quantity myself, but
like I said.... Do it as you'd want to find it, if they were not your
boxes.

I guess my opinion on the density has to do with the style of
the boxes. Some places just cry out for letterboxes. Other locations
are more random and just happen to be a hiding place that the clues
lead to. I have placed boxes of both types. I guess I am saying that
it can get to be contrived if there are a lot of the second type of
box within a 15 minute drive of the placer's house. From what I have
seen of your clues, Esmerelda, they sound like pretty special spots,
and they definitely aren't all in downtown Palisade. I hope this
rambling makes some sense. And I guess the short version is- place
them and let the seeker decide what is right for them.

Also, for some perspective, a newbie in CT just started a couple
of weeks ago and has just surpassed F 100. There's some density for
ya.

haven't stamped.

I started. And still only one in Steamboat; none in the San Luis
Valley. But it wasn't that long ago when that was the case for Denver
and Colorado Springs. Things are changing and the boxes we are
planting now will be the "dear old" boxes when a few more people are
involved. It is pretty cool, actually.
