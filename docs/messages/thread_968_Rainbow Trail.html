Calli-K and everyone,

We found Rainbow Trail some time ago, as well. Yes, the plastic shoebox
is hard to hide and it is right next to the trail. We sent Edna a
message about its situation and never heard back. What do you do about
boxes like that? I consider it abandoned and personally hate to see
people go out of their way to look for it. Is their any way to get a
note put onto the letterboxing.org website, which states its condition???
If that isn't possible, can it be adopted and completely revamped into a
functioning letterbox? What happened to Ramdelt's idea a while back
about having a database with problematic boxes?

It is a beautiful area and it seems as though Calli-K's new box is in
that same general area??? Is it off the same trailhead?

Is there a procedure to take off a confirmed problematic box or can it be
adopted by someone else and have a new stamp carved? There isn't an
image of the old stamp and the new stamp is purchased.... I believe it is
a star ... something unrelated to the box name or place. I'm thinking
that we didn't stamp into the logbook. I do know we didn't put the new,
unrelated stamp into our journal. There are two edna's listed on
letterboxing.org ... one with a big E and one with a little e. The
little "e" placer has five boxes in Illinois. I've written to her asking
if she is the same placer. My guess is they are not one in the same, as
I thought the log at Rainbow Trail said it was her first box and two of
the placement dates in Illinois predate the boxes in Colorado.

~Catamount Carvers

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2005-12-30 21:10:19 UTC-07:00
Was this an issue that was also brought up on the main list? Just
curious. I can understand some concern about having a box listed as
being a problem, as someone could be looking in the wrong place. That
often happens. However, there are some boxes that are a CONFIRMED
problem and it is not good to have person after person go look for it.
Rainbow Trail is now listed as recently found in the database, but there
is critical information missing. It really is a long way over there to
that box. We happened to be in the general area for other reasons, so it
wasn't a big deal.

We encountered lots of missing boxes this summer in the northwest and it
was very frustrating. In most cases, it was obvious that we had found
the right spot, but no box. Essentially, we made it a point not to look
for any boxes that were remotely out of our way and that is sad for us
and for box owners.

I love all you box owners who follow up on reports of missing or damaged
boxes. I've found that to be the norm in our area, but I understand the
difficulty of that for boxes placed far from home! In fact, we went out
and found A Mountain Monogram today. It was our second attempt. I
appreciated the fact that No No Girl quickly responded to our report of
the A Mountain Monogram missing back in October. I personally think that
it was accidently found by the folks who repainted the A and they might
have passed it around for others to look at and then simply put it back
in the wrong spot. No No Girl and her Brother must have worked hard to
find it in all that white rubble. Good job!

That's enough rambling ...
~Silent Wings of the Catamount Carvers

From: callik4lb (gilbe@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-01-01 04:21:58 UTC
Renee and Dave wrote:

> It is a beautiful area and it seems as though Calli-K's new box is in
> that same general area??? Is it off the same trailhead?

No, the box I left for you folks in Colorado is off highway 50, seemed
like maybe the northern-most point of Rainbow trail, although I
couldn't be sure. We parked at a trail head that WAS the Rainbow
Trail, not a trail leading up to it, so it was either the beginning or
the end, or appeared to be. Having placed "A Gift From Oregon" and
having found Edna's "Rainbow Trail" I can tell you they are not in the
same vicinity at all. There are several postcards with Edna's address
on them, and I took one and mailed it today, telling her I had
e-mailed her as well. And yes, the stamp in there now is a commercial
stamp.

My concern over a box like that is that it says bad things about
letterboxing to hikers. It looks trashy sitting there with its broken
lid and all. A hiker seeing that would certainly not be very
intrigued by "letterboxing" - anyway, maybe someone will hear from
her. If I were a local I would just go on up there after not hearing
via e-mail and postal mail to her address and revamp the box. I'd
carve my own stamp and just put it in there, put in a new smaller log
book, and hide it the way a letterbox should be hidden, strictly for
the sake of letterboxing's good name. I'm not local, and I wouldn't
want to step on toes as a guest in your state, just my opinion as to
something that could be done without any guilt. One of the first
people I met to learn about letterboxing told me an untended box is
considered "trash." That's a bit harsh, because there are so many
people who place boxes all over the place and can't possibly tend to
them, but they at least stay in touch with the letterboxing site and
allow others to adopt them or tend to them if they like. Placing a
box and then never coming back to the web site or the physical site to
check on it seems kind of like abandonment to me, in which case anyone
interested should not feel bad about tending to it.

I will certainly come back here and let you all know if mailing the
postcard to her house results in anything at all.

Again, I sure loved your beautiful state - JeLyBean told me a critter
has been chewing on the box I left you - hope it got frustrated and
gave up and doesn't come back to do further damage.

Happy New Year to you all.
