Hi!

I just joined this group since we will be in the area for a short ski
vacation MAR 7-11. We'll be in the Keystone/Copper Mtn/Breckenridge
area, staying at a condo in Silverthorne.

Snow conditions will probably prevent us from finding many boxes (and
hey, we'll be skiing most of the time, right?), but we do plan to
bring our LB gear and a few clues along just in case.

If anyone has any specific suggestions regarding boxes (or good
places to eat!), we'd appreciate the info. And if any of you are in
that area and would like to get together for an exchange in the
evening on Saturday MAR 8, Sunday MAR 9 or Monday MAR 10, just let us
know.

About us: Wisconsin Hiker & Martini Man have been boxing since July
2003 (heard about it from a friend) and have been obsessed ever
since. We've found boxes in 23 states (and Scotland & New Zealand)
and planted in 14 states (and New Zealand & Mexico). We've always
been avid hikers, but the added fun of "the hunt" really appeals to
us. We really enjoy the creativity of many of the clues & stamps.
Letterboxing has taken us to some interesting locations and allowed
us to meet a bunch of great people across the USA!

Wisconsin Hiker (& Martini Man)

On Wed, Oct 1, 2008 at 10:03 AM, RavenWolf wrote:

> I don't suppose you want to explain the joke to those of us on
> the 'wrong' side of the Rockies?


I've thought about this, and thought about this, and even asked my hubby how
you explain why GreenJello is such a funny trailname for someone from Utah.

The best answer is that it's cultural, I guess.

It's a joke that it's an unwritten rule that all church potlucks must have
jello salad of some sort. And in Utah, people get weird creative with jello.
Green jello with pears. Green jello with shredded carrots. Green jello with
cabbage. Green jello with zucchini. It's kind of like an inside joke, but
for all of Utah.

The most popular Olympic pin in 2002 was the green jello pin. That sucker
sold for a LOT of money after they ran out. It still cracks me up!

GreenJello
