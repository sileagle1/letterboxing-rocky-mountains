From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2007-07-24 14:27:00 UTC
Oh, that's so sad. I knew this would happen one day, though. Some
years ago, I was talking about letterboxing with a relative and he said
something like, "I think it would be fun, instead of collecting just
the stamp images, to collect whole boxes." He was kidding, of course,
but I thought then, if he could think of this, someone more evil could
also think of it, and I was pretty sure it would only be a matter of
time before I was reading about this. I mean really, what is to stop a
person like that? It's not against the law to take boxes, in fact,
they could even be considered a hero of sorts for cleaning up "litter"
in these parks.

This is where it would be useful for an organization like LbNA to have
some formal structure. If it were incorporated, for instance, and
these boxes could be considered LbNA property, perhaps LbNA could work
with the parks to ensure letterboxes are not stolen somehow.

JeLyBean
