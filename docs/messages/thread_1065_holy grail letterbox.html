From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-16 03:08:59 UTC
Since I haven't really even looked at the Bard's clues I don't know
if this will help or not, but ya'll who are Bard obsessed (and at
least one other boxer with whom I've been having an email convo)
should have a look at this weekend's Sunday Denver Post (4/16/06).

There you will find a front page article on the original Elitch
Gardens site and a theatre there that is about to start a $14
million renovation (I live near here, they've been raising money for
years. So NICE to see it's proceeding and they have the likes of
Cloris Leachman, Debbie Reynolds, and Patty Duke on the advisory
board...) I might add, that since I go to the farmers markets and
the free weekly films I know that Robert Redford has his first ever
stage appearance at this place. I got to take a toure one night on
film night.

Anyway, when you flip to the inside page of the A section to keep
reading, an accompanying article begins with:

"In the Wild West days, anything having to do with Shakespeare had
to mean class. So when John Elitch decided to build a world-class
theater on his 32-acre farm in northwest Denver, he used the Bard's
Globe Theater as his model. The original painted linen curtain
depicted a cottage owned by Anne Hathaway, Shakespeare's wife."....

and so on.... I thought of all of you when I read this and knew I
had to share in case it helps...
