Olga and the Night Visitors

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-07-07 13:44:36 UTC
Once upon a time just a short while ago a Bean was visiting Durango.
Being a good letterboxer, he went up to Animas Overlook to nab all of
the boxes there.

Eventually he found Olga's box but, much to his dismay, when he
opened the box he found it full of geotrash and Olga's stamp was
missy! Quick as a .. uh... quick person... he messaged Es of the
dilema.

Es zoomed over to the geocaching site at Atlas Quest and sent up an
SOS. Within minutes someone had located the intended cache AND the
cacher who had 'found only one thing, left lots'. They had been up
there in the dark! Scary!

So, Es sent a nice note to the cacher saying how much Olga missed her
mule. First the cacher wrote back something like, I don't know what
you're talking about. Uh oh. But then she wrote, oh, you mean that
hand carved rubber stamp mounted on a slab of tree that's been there
for four years that I traded a used pencil for? Okay, she didn't say
that, but she did say, oh, I remember, the stamp of a mule. I will be
happy to give it back to you. I meant no harm. It was dark.

After a few emails, she decided she would feel best if she took it
back herself. So now Olga has her mule back and the box is open for
business.

Thanks Bean!
Thanks Y!
Thanks cacher Brwneyes!

Es, who wanted to share a story with a happy ending
