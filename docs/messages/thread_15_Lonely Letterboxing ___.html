Oh Cadenza ... I hear you!  There aren't many boxes in Colorado - and many of them are in the high country. 
Okay - I'm sorry but my fat old thirty-mumble-something body ain't gonna make an eight mile hike, or a hike at 11,000 feet.  I may try for some of those one day - but alas, many of the boxes out here are just plain TOO HARD.  (can we say one of Randy Hall's 4-star mysteries??)
 
Anyway- there are five boxes on the Western Slope (four hour drive one way) and let's face it - even a drive to Cheyenne takes the better part of the day. 
 
4 of my 13 few found are from out of state - using precious vacation days to letterbox because I'm addicted. 
 
I'm glad to have found some letterboxing compatriots out here in the Colorado Front Range.  Perhaps we can meet at some middle ground one day? I'd love to get to Idaho Falls, but its not gonna happen this summer, unfortunately.
 
You're right - it IS annoying to read about a letterboxing weekend where someone was able to find 10+ boxes, or more, and a hitchhiker every trip!  I was getting very discouraged.   But, I think we (we being all of us on the list) need to start planting more letterboxes.  In this way, we can hopefully encourage the passtime to grow (and feed our addictions in the process).
 
Cheers,
Autumn

Will do.  I suspect many of the posts will not really apply to me, but this is the closest any have come.  I do consider myself as being part of the Rockies (being a stones throw from Yellowstone, the Tetons, and others) but there just aren't others out here yet.  Luckily a few have been kind enough to come this way and leave some plants.

The thing that appeals to me about this group is being able to communicate with others who understand not only the terrain, but what it is like to be a "lonely letterboxer."  I have to huff a bit when I see posts complaining about only finding 15 of 20 boxes they looked for that day, or things like that.  When I started here, there weren't that many boxes in my whole state, and considering the fact that you could fit many of the eastern states into one state out here, that's a lot of territory.  I love reading their emails, but it is a whole different perspective when I still have to travel hundreds of miles to get to boxes that I didn't find in my first week (there were two in the east half of ID).  It is getting better, but it will be fun to know

Anyway, I'm rambling a bit-sorry! (:  I'll end now.  See you all later, and if anyone is interested in coming to Idaho this weekend, let me know.  We'd love to have more for our letterboxing cookout.
