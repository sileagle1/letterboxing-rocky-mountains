Boxing AGAIN in southern UT

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-04-25 19:10:46 UTC

Hello everyone,

This past weekend Al and I went to Bullfrog; he rode his motorcycle
and I drove my dry, comfortable 4-wheels. He's training for a cycle
trip to OK to visit relatives this September. And I just GO any time
someone says the word!

We found a few letterboxes along the way. 1) Cadenza's Historic Helper
LB - First finder to this one! Excellent stamp. Had to REALLY reach
for the box; good thing Al's longer arms were along. I put it back,
though, so shorter arms should be able to capture it now. It is still
well out of sight and contains my first ever HH. (I know I'm not
supposed to advertise, but I REALLY want someone to pick it up.) 2)
Kelly's Sharp Tooth - First finder to this one, too, and took the
charm. Thanks! Cool stamp. There was quite a bit of water in the
ziploc, but NOT IN THE BOX. Replaced the bag. 3) Y-nought's Navajo
Code Talkers - Deciphered the code at home before we left; that was
fun! Couldn't find a reference to SHUSH GAH-GHIL-KEID at the river. If
it was grafiti, it's been painted over, but found the LB anyway by the
rest of the clue. 4) Green Tortuga's Mexican Hat - It was fun to get
into the right position using the features and degrees. And what a
marvelous formation the hat is! All the stamps were wonderful and, as
always, I love the incentive to visit new places that boxing provides.

Lake Powell is down 140 feet, but the folks there say they are in
hopes of recovering 50 feet of that this year. The ferry to Hall's
Crossing isn't running, so we took the l-o-n-g route to Mexican Hat.

Puppypaws, I tried a search for Alex's Birthday Candle #4 HH on Atlas
Quest by placer and HH, but came up empty, so I'm guessing JACKS
didn't register it there.

Going to collect my mail for the past few days and hoping there are
postal LB's in it,
Boxer Lover
