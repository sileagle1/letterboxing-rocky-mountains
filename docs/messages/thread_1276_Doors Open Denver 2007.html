Not exactly letterboxing, but for those of you who might be interested
in a fun weekend of exploring Denver:

What is Door Open Denver?
DOD is a FREE 2-day event that celebrates architecture and design by
allowing participants to go behind the scenes at more than 70 of
Denver's significant architectural gems and lesser known treasures. DOD
is organized and hosted by the Denver Office of Cultural Affairs.

More info: http://tinyurl.com/26s5uh

This year's dates: April 14-15 so mark your calendars.

In past years we've gotten to go below the stage at the Paramount
Theatre to see how the organs are lowered and raised, toured small
museums hardly ever open to the public, poked around innovative offices
and work places, a behind the stage (even ON the stage) tour of the
Buell Theatre, lofts tours - even some private residences, and much
more.

Don't know yet if any more letterboxes might make their appearance in
time for this event, but for those of you who haven't boxed in Denver
for awhile ..There are ...well...more than there used to be before I
got addicted to urban boxing :-)

The specific building listing for DOD should be out soon, so look for
it on the link above. We usually get a small group togother to make a
day out of it on one of the days of the weekend. If you're interested
in joining our group, drop me an email off line.

preboxed
