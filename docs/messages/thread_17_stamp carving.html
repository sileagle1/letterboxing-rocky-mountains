stamp carving If there's a Michaels around, that's where I got some of my stuff, also Roberts, which I don't hear mentioned as often outside of this area.  If you can find the Speedball kit that doesn't come with carving material, I like that better.  It comes with a better handle and more blades, including the #1 blade that I use constantly for narrow cuts.  I don't think the #1 comes in the kit with the pink stuff.  If you can find it separately, buy it.

Personally, since starting to use the PZcut in www.stampeaz.com I have never gone back to the pink stuff.  It is easy to buy online and inexpensive.  She is trusworthy, and I think it carves much better than the pink stuff.

When I'm in the craft stores, I have only ever found Speedball stuff in larger stores, and it is always located with the erasers, pencils, and stamp rollers.  I've never found anything actually in a stamp section (ironic, isn't it?).  I actually discovered the speedball carving kits by accident.  I was looking for different erasers to try carving with.

Good luck!
Cadenza

Oh Cadenza ... I hear you!  There aren't many boxes in Colorado - and many of them are in the high country.  
Okay - I'm sorry but my fat old thirty-mumble-something body ain't gonna make an eight mile hike, or a hike at 11,000 feet.  I may try for some of those one day - but alas, many of the boxes out here are just plain TOO HARD.  (can we say one of Randy Hall's 4-star mysteries??)
Anyway- there are five boxes on the Western Slope (four hour drive one way) and let's face it - even a drive to Cheyenne takes the better part of the day.  
4 of my 13 few found are from out of state - using precious vacation days to letterbox because I'm addicted.  
I'm glad to have found some letterboxing compatriots out here in the Colorado Front Range.  Perhaps we can meet at some middle ground one day? I'd love to get to Idaho Falls, but its not gonna happen this summer, unfortunately.

Cheers,
