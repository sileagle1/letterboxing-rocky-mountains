New to Colorado

From: Mandy Bennett (cameoboxer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-10 10:52:43 UTC-08:00
Hello all! My name is "Cameo." I've been boxing since November 2003 and absolutely love it! Last April I finally got to meet some others at our Cabin Fever Letterboxing Event in Oklahoma City. Although it was totally exhausting (and that was in low, flat country in mild weather) it was a BLAST!

My husband (a resistor, but enjoys site-seeing, which letterboxing takes you to many wonderful, otherwise unknown places) and I moved up a couple of weeks ago to Carbondale, between Glenwood Springs and Aspen (you all probably already know that, hee hee). I'm still "moving in," but thought I'd check out the LBNA site to search for any clues of boxes close to home - none here in carbondale. I couldn't even find one of my favorites in Glenwood Springs - guess it's gone :( There are a few in Aspen, and one in Snowmass, so that'll do me good for a little while.

Proposition: Anyone feel like plantin' some boxes down highway 85? I'd sure 'preciate it! I'm also interested in some "easy" boxes (I have several autoimmune disorders that limits my hiking ability and sun exposure).

I've seen posts here and there about a gathering coming up soon. Can't remember who's holding it - would someone remind me? When is it? Where is it? Will it just be a "get to know your fellow letterboxers" weekend or a "there're letterboxes planted everywhere around here" type of gather?

Looking forward to meeting you on the trail and finding your boxes!!!

Cameo

From: Aljan Sundance (aljansundance@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-10 13:17:51 UTC-08:00
Welcome Cameo!

The Catamount Carvers are going to help me put on the gathering... It is the third weekend in August in Grand Lake - not too far for you! We will have a BBQ on that Saturday at my house. There are a lot of boxes in the county to be had. Mostly mine although there is one over in Winter Park planted by a team effort between myself, Dixie and Cappy and one by Silver Eagle here in town. So, there are good letterboxing opportunities. Plus there will be an event stamp and a chance to meet your fellows.

I am hoping to get over your way this summer to do some boxing. The only one I have from over there is the Sprouting Rock box at Hanging Lake - a hike that just about killed me. Nothing like being reminded how rediculously out of shape you are!

Glad to have you, Aljan

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-03-10 18:30:35 UTC-07:00
Welcome Cameo to our strange group. We can be a bit odd, but we are a fun
bunch and care about each other. There aren't too many of us--Colorado is
the place to be in this region though for boxing. Some of the rest of us
are up in other states with no boxing buddies to leave boxes for us.

I hope you will enjoy your association here with these wacky letterboxers.
It would be great to meet you at a gather. We tend to also end up having
some smaller ones at other times in the year too.

till then,
Cadenza
