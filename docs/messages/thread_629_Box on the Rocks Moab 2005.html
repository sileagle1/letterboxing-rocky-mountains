Box on the Rocks Moab 2005

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-04-13 22:25:54 UTC

I just got home last night and have been reading all the posts and
enjoying all the photos. Can you stand one more message about the
gathering? Of, course, you can!! I had such a fabulous time this past
weekend +! So much fun to match names and faces and see all of your
wonderful stamps. The exchange frenzy was a finely choreographed hoot!

I added 17 Fs to my puny pre-event total and 23 Xs, which is way
amazing since, where I live, I never thought I would get ANY of those.
[Puppypaws and Kittylady, I seem to have missed your stamps!:-( ] The
Moab/Y/Es crew have hidden such a plethora of Lbs for the rest of us
to seek. And now there are even more thanks to the forsight of recent
visitors. Thanks to those who played along with my attempt at the
personal traveler game; I cherish your participation. I got one first
find, Y's To the End of the World with its most incredible view (and
climb). Woo hoo!

While it is true that the hunts and rewards were splendid, the real
treasures were the nutty people whom I count as new friends. It was
such fun running into you on the trails: Ramdelt and Stubby at the
beginning of Searching (fortuitous because I didn't get their stamps
at the exchange); A-Bear, CW Sun Seeker, Cadenza and Alex at the top
of Searching and Blackvelvetrav after her dash for coffee; Aljan and
company when I went to re-stamp at CO River on Monday.

I certainly am relieved to learn that all the ID and CO people made it
safely home. CWSS, please check in soon.

Now having met a bunch of you, I believe I can claim the title of
OLDEST in the age war, which is ironic because I am also probably the
NEWEST in this group to succomb to the Lbing fever. Surrounding myself
with younger folks (younger either in years or spirit) has long been
my stategy in the war against the clock. Thanks for sharing your
letterboxing ideas and techniques with a newbie.

Many, many thanks and praise go to Kat, Y-nought, Kennamtn and
Rivergeek for your efforts and hospitality. Aspen, I loved the
flowers! I still have many more magnificent canyons to hike and boxes
to find around Moab. There will be return trips, for certain.

And now, who/what/when/where will the gathering on the eastern side of
the mountains be?

RE: Box on the Rocks Moab 2005

From: cw_sunseeker (cw_sunseeker@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-16 04:57:22 UTC

Whew! I just got back from my Moab/Colorado trip last night.
Wow - so fun to catch up on all the posts and look at the pictures!
Yes, it was a blast to meet my UT, CO & ID friends! Great boxin'
there in Moab - thanks to all the placers. Thanks to Kat, Kennamtn,
Rivergeek and Y-nought for great planning! The event stamp and boxes
were fantastic! And good pie. . . I loved the chocolate one!

Now I miss the nice cool weather there...it's 98 degrees here in
Phoenix today. It was hard to miss not getting ALL of the Moab boxes,
and now I have an excuse to go back! I picked up a few along the way
in Arizona and Colorado too - Grand Junction and Ft. Collins.

Each time A-Bear got out the carving stuff at midnight, it was
irresistible not to join in! Then when Cadenza joined in the second
night, it was just too fun. Blackvelvetrav and Lizman had the right
idea . . . sleep! Jasmine says "woof" to Lizman. She likes her new
buddy and thanks him for the walks!

It's great fun to search for boxes with friends who share an
appreciation of this hobby.

It was really exciting to run into our fellow boxers on the trail
(Dixie Peach & Cappy, Ramdelt & Stubby, BoxerLover and Cadenza) and
in the restaurant (RavenWolf clan & Aljan Sundance clan)! Thanks to Y-
Y-nought & boys for going with us on Portal trail. And it was fun to
have Kat along on one of the hikes, too! I haven't gotten organized
enough yet to come up with an actual count yet of box finds.

After going back to Ticaboo and Searching on Mon. with A-Bear,
Cadenza, Lizman, Blackvelvetrav, I was lucky enough to keep on boxin'
Monday. with Cadenza and Boxerlover - what fun!

Then I headed on to Colorado found: Commuting - Those Were the Days!,
Big Fish (in the dark), Johnny Carino's (in the dark), Grand Valley,
No Use, Big Thompson Flood, Fish Tales, Joseph Mason (in the dark),
William Stover, and Auntie Stone. I attempted Grizzly Creek - in the
DARK, and I think I was in the right area, but got spooked while
poking around.

Puppy Paws: let me know if you make it to Phoenix! :)

Oh, and will Stubby the Toothslayer keep on boxin'? I think he will -
it seemed natural for him - he'll have to keep us posted!

I'll need more sleep before writing up the clues to my hidden boxes!
Thank you all again.
CW Sun Seeker
