Okay, fine, fine. I'm Eliza B, formerly of Seattle, now out on the
plains, lurker on this list for a few weeks now. (The
husband-type-person does a lot of business in Denver and kind of wants
to relocate that direction. We're there a *lot* when the snow isn't
flying.)

Oh, and I have pie. Made it this morning, in fact. It's just
garden-variety apple, but I can make a *mean* cherry-peach, when I'm
in the mood. :)

Someone asked how we found letterboxing: Back in 2001, I was on a
Carving Consortium mailing list, and one of the members had a link on
her website (there weren't a lot of "blogs", per se, back then.) to
LBNA and it was *all* downhill from there. I'd been carving my own
stamps for a long time at that point, so I made a signature stamp and
found Amanda of Seattle's Schmidt Park boxes and was hooked. One
might possibly say obsessed. (One would be right.)

There's been a three year lapse in boxing for me, since there aren't
many of 'em out where I live, but theyr'e starting to pick up out
here, and I've evangelized so much that I've got a couple friends into
it now, too. So it's looking up. :) We're headed out to the Denver
area over Father's Day weekend and plan to 'box for all we're worth
when we're not oogling sheep.
