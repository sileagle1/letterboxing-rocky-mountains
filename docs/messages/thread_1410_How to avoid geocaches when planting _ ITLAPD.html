How to avoid geocaches when planting & ITLAPD

From: claudey (erin@erinterry.com) | Date: 2007-09-18 22:44:01 UTC
Hi all!
We all know how frustrating it can be to carefully plant a box, and
then find out it wound up too close to a geocache we didn't know
about, and have our box fill up with trinkets when cachers find our
boxes by mistake (even though they're marked NOT A GEOCACHE!). Or
worse, as Jely told me about, have your stamp and log book disappear
because someone thought it was a trade item! I'm posting a geocache
browser kml file in the files section, which if you download it &
double-click on it, it will open up in Google Earth (if you have that
already installed). It will show you every cache ever hidden. Of
course, not all caches will show up as there are mystery ones where
the coordinates posted are bogus and the cacher must solve a puzzle to
get the real coords. Also, stages of a multicache won't show up,
either, just the first stage. But, at least it will give us a good
general idea of where NOT to plant a box. The icons of the caches tend
to jump around a bit so it's not always the exact location, but like I
said, it will show you the general location and you'll know not to
hide one nearby. You can click on the icon, then click on the link
that appears by the cache to see where it is exactly, but you have to
be a geocaching.com member & logged in to do that. Here's an
explanation of the icons
(though it's pretty self-explanatory): Question mark: mystery cache
(no cache actually thereit's within 2 mi of the fake coords, but some
cachers go there anyway if they're new). Green
cache icon: traditional cache. Yellow cache icon: multicachefirst
stage starts there unless it's also a mystery. Ghost icon: virtual
cacheno physical container, but cachers will come by that location to
get the answer to a question. Envelope icon: letterbox hybridyes,
some cachers do also letterbox! It's just like it sounds, a combo of a
cache & a letterbox. Stamp/logbook + trade items.

I hope I haven't thoroughly confused everyone now! :) Any questions,
let me know.

Last but not least, happy International Talk Like a Pirate Day
tomorrow (9/19)! Arrggh, mateys!

claudey
