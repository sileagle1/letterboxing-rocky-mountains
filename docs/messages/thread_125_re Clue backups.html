Hiya! I know this is an old subject, but I'm just catching up...

I think it's a great idea to have a backup system for clues, and that
Autumn has done a nice job of respecting the sensitivity of the
authors. It's also true that clues change alot, so it may be an
impossible task to do by hand using Word documents, especially as the
Rocky Mountain letterboxing community expands. That's the reason LbNA
went to the "new" website. The manual work was overwhelming. For
years, people posted their actual clues to the yahoogroup and then
the webmasters cut-and-pasted them into LbNA files. Poof! Archive on
yahoogroup. I still occaisionally search the group archives for old
clues. Now with the "announce" group, less and less people post their
clues to the big list. Too bad, really...

There is an unofficial experiement on an alternative similar to
Autumn's idea over at the big list (letterbox-usa at yahoogroups dot
com). The link is below. It's a database that gives only the
yahoogroup message number, not the whole clue. So you could post your
box in a message to the big list the old fashioned way and then enter
a database item to make it easier to find. If you edited the clues,
you would just re-post a message to the group and then change the
database link.

Not to say that the yahoogroups archives will last forever, either,
but it's probably better than a big file of paper clues.

I'm not trying to sound ominous or anything, LbNA is a long term
project with committed people behind it, but you never know. Nothing
wrong with being prepared!
