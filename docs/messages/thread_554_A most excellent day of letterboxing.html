A most excellent day of letterboxing

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-08 15:24:04 UTC

I finally managed to spend another day in Moab on the great
letterbox hunt.

After 8 letterboxes and a hitchhiker, the sun was setting and I had
to call it a day. I do NOT know how those east coasters do 30 boxes
in one day!!! Even if they were stacked on top of each other how do
you do the whole stamping, stamping, writing, rebagging, yada yada
thing for 30 boxes?! But I digress. The weather was just perfect..
couldn't have asked for better.

I started the morning at Fisher Towers searching for Alien Gothic.
Oh. My. Heck. Aside from the fact that this is a wonderfully
written and made letterbox, I can still picture the scene layed out
before me as I stood on a ridge staring in awe at the unreal
landscape just glowing in the early morning light as far as you can
see. I stopped many, many times and said, 'oh my gosh!' out loud
more times than I can count. If you make it out to the Moab area?
You're gonna want to find this box. I'm just sayin'. :)

I worked my way down Highway 128 and found Colorado River, Paddle
Hard, Searching (and, yes, that IS an Ent up that canyon!), and
Bighorn Sheep. At Bighorn I was standing at the plaque decyphering
the clues when an older couple (even older than me) pulled up to
look at the river. I just kept decyphering, said, 'Good morning,
I'm decyphering a clue!', when she looked over my shoulder. The man
just thought I was nuts but the wife went, 'Oooooh!', like, 'now I
don't think you're crazy anymore!' hehehe. A few miles down the
road I actually saw 4 real life bighorn sheep!

On into Moab to finally.. FINALLY... capture Left Hand box (you
might have seen the fireworks go off). All the bushes in that
canyon are starting to bud out. Gorgeous. Plus, I didn't even need
a clue sheet anymore.. after four attempts it's like being in my own
backyard. heee. Arrtoy Kpra was next and I attempted to get the
Birthday Box but wasn't able to find it. I also picked up a
hitchhiker and a bonus box along the way.. I was so busy hiking I
forgot to stop for pie!

I highly recommend the Moab area to anyone thinking about a road
trip. The local boxers and y-nought have created a most excellent
letterboxing spot.

Es, who forgot to take her journal with her but is still smiling
from a really lovely day
