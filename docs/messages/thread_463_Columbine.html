I'm going to agree agree that you probably shouldn't do it yet, but I
think some could do it tastefully, if very, very, very careful. But I
think there are many that could be offended regardless of your tact.
If you felt strongly about doing something. I think you could have
some kind of very nice tribute somewhere a little more removed than at
Columbine--oh, and I probably wouldn't discuss it on the big list. I
can see someone turning it into an ugly flame.

This is much more removed than our immediate history, but I found one
letterbox that was at a very important yet unknown part of US and
Native American history. It is part of our country's forgotten
history, and it is a shame that others haven't known about it. I
don't think I can say more without giving clues away.

I can think of many that would feel this was a desecration of a sacred
place for many cultures. The planter put her heart and soul into it
though and her nicest carving to that date. She hid it in a larger
box and included an in-depth reading of the history of that place and
its culture and why it was important for all of us to understand it
better and honor the people who had gone before. The box clue was
hidden with a name that was different than what one might expect, so
it wasn't flaunting "Oh, here, I put a box at a sacred place. Isn't it
cool!" I haven't seen her push the box. She has been very unassuming
about it.

I learned so much from doing this letterbox and was grateful for it.
I'm not going to tell you which it is though and ruin it. Sorry! And
I have letterboxed all over the western US and through many other
places, so it could be anywhere. I think she struck a nice balance
that not many could have done.

Well, this has been a nice diversion from work for a few. Now back to
the grindstone! Hope you don't mind my two cents (actually, I'm not
good at two cent writing. I do more like a quarter :)

Que les valle bien y pasen un buen ano nuevo! (Dang, I need
international characters on the internet!)
Cadenza
