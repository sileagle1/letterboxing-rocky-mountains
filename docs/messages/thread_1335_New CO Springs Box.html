From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2007-05-29 18:57:35 UTC
According to Schrodinger, absolutely! Until anyone actually checks
on it, the cat could be said to be neither dead nor alive, or,
simultaneously both dead and alive. It will not definitely be either
until you examine it, giving new meaning to the phrase "y-nought's
curiosity killed the cat." Or maybe I should say y-0.

BTW, for those of you who don't know, the whole discussion about
Schrodinger came about when team JeLy was planning a trip to take the
candy wagon down to Pagosa. We wanted to meet up with y-0 and La
Paloma, but there was a great deal of uncertainty about that trip
which led to a silly email exchange about the Heisenberg uncertainty
principle and Schrodinger's Cat (yes, I'm a science geek), which
eventually spawned the idea for a letterbox where the stamp is
unknown until you open it and y-0 coining the phrase "Quantum Catbox."

Ironically, during that trip, I planted the Piedra River hot springs
box, where y-0 eventually ended up proposing to La Paloma, which
inspired the creation of yet another letterbox.

You see, it's all related. Perhaps I need another letterbox, this
one dedicated to Jung's principle of Synchonicity. Perhaps I could
work that Police song into it somehow. I'm digging bands more like
Coldplay these days, but the Police were great in their day.

JeLyBean
