Utah's great finds

From: (scott_katytalbot@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-07-09 04:05:36 UTC
For those of you who did not know, my wife, kids, and I were in Utah for the past week letterboxing like crazy! It was amazing how streaky letterboxing can be! The first 2 days we found nearly every box we went after. July 4th and 5th were near busts, only finding a couple of boxes each day (most were MIA). But ended well on Thursday and Friday finding nearly all attempted again. In all, we found 37 of 52 attempted. I know that some people do not like others posting that their box is missing so I will contact those people individually. Those found were:

The Stone House
Farewell Prohibition
Snowbird Letterbox
Take Flight
Bees and Beehive (GFCS #1)
The Highland Ram
The View
Blue Ribbon
Ladybug
UTSS #3: Cherries (met up with CWSunseeker here)
Utah History Mystery #2
Utah History Mystery #3
On The Farm
Wetlands #2 Fowl Play (needs a log book)
Utah's State Animal (GFCS #4)
Bingham Copper Mine Letterbox
UTSS #4: California Gull
Ski Utah
Moose
Mountain Trails Foundation
The Swaner Box
Summit County Library (Indoor Box)

Silver Lake, Brighton UT

Thanks to all planters and to CWSS for meeting us for Cherries!

Oh, I forgot to mention that when I went to find Cadenza's Rocky Mountain Elk in Copperton, I was shoed away by security. I was told that they were setting up for a film there about a couple of baseball players during World War 2. I think that they said it was called something like "American Pastime". They had the track covered with a stage and baseball setup and didn't want some crazy letterboxer trouncing around the set. Can you believe those Hollywood producers???!!!
