From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-25 03:25:23 UTC

Hello folks-
My little town is having it's annual winter festival called
Snowdown. As part of the festivities we have the "Search for the
Silver Bullet" which is a treasure hunt put on by the local radio
station and the Coors Plant. They give out five clues a day over the
radio, spaced three hours apart, and recap all previous clues with
the evening clue. This lasts for five days. The clues lead to some
treasure that you turn in for $250 cash plus other prizes which makes
this thing hugely popular.

Two years ago the clues were hard, but not hard enough, and
someone found it on the third day. This loses a lot of advertising
potential so last year they made the clues ridiculously hard and on
the sixth day they announced the 26th clue which was it's exact
location. Pandemonium ensued as hundreds of people raced for The
Bullet. I just got the first five clues today for this years' hunt. I
should say that I've never even come close to ever finding it. But I
have considered offering my services as clue writer.

Anyway, it's a LOT of fun and maybe I'll crack it early enough
this year. Who knows?

y-nought
