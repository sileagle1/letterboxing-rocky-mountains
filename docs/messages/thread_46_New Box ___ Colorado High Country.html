The Dragon's Lair
Placed by: Autumn's Folly
Placement date: Aug 10 2003
State: Colorado
County: Park
Nearest city: Bailey
Number of boxes: 1

Clues
~~ Dragons tell your ancient stories
Dragons sing your ancient songs
Dragons tell your ancient stories
So your wisdom can live on!
(from "Old Ones" by Edward Earl Bates)

Directions:
Take Hwy 285 West toward Bailey. Turn left onto Park County Road 68
(toward Wellington Lake). Follow this road about 9 miles to the
trailhead parking area. There is a sign for Rolling Creek #663 and
Colorado Trail #1776. Park here.

To the Letterbox:
Cross the street to the eastern trail marker for Colorado Trail
#1776. Take a 5 minute or so leisurely walk. On your right you will
find a 50' fallen tree. 10 paces at 230 degrees from the top of the
tree you will locate another fallen log. The dragon's treasure is
buried behind this log under a pile of rocks.

Walking Distance: Not far at all
Incline: About 3-400' right at the beginning
Kid friendly: Sure, though strollers will be hard
Shoes: I did it in sandals
Time: 15 min or so

Other Facts:
If you have not been further up this road, I recommend the drive.
About 2 miles up and past the Jefferson Co Outdoor Lab School is
Wellington Lake. This is one of the most beautiful small lakes in
Colorado. Shadowed by Castle Mountain, this serene and secluded spot
is just exactly the terrain in which dragons must have lived in their
day.
