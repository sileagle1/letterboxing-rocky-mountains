Re: [RMLetterboxing] the cnn thing

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2004-09-03 12:06:11 UTC-04:00
I wonder how much publicity is too much publicity. CNN definitely has a
mass audience. It is not The Smithsonian. Undoubtedly, press can
provide the letterboxing community with some wonderful, new contributors,
but it can also unintentionally cause a certain amount of damage to or
even theft of existing boxes. I suspect that inadvertent damage by
sincere, but careless (possibly novice) letterboxers is the bigger threat
... not being discreet in locating or rehiding the box, letterboxing on a
rainy day, etc. It's hard for me to imagine someone seeking out boxes
with the intent of stealing them. I can more easily imagine someone
stumbling across one and then justify taking it by using that old
finders/keepers mentality. And, Es, I suspect your wonderful, handmade
journals would be rather tempting for anyone lacking descent moral
bearings.

How do people find out about letterboxing? In our case, our "art
oriented" librarian gave our son a copy of The Letterboxing Companion
about a year ago. We read the whole thing cover to cover a couple of
times before we ever went searching for our first box. After seeing lots
of good box examples, we're working on some of our own boxes. We've
introduced letterboxing to 2 or 3 individuals we thought would really
appreciate the art of it. Otherwise, it's really more of a family
secret. I just assume the whole letterboxing thing grow slowly and stay
manageable in size.

~The Catamount Carvers
