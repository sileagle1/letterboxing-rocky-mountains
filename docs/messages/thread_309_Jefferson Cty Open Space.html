Jefferson County Open Space has seen a large increase in geocaching and
letterboxing. Unfortunately, some of the sites are in sensitive areas
or in areas that have seasonal closures. While we do allow placement of
caches and letterboxes, site owners must apply for and receive a special
activity permit prior to placing anything on Open Space property. Your
website lists several letterboxes that do not have permits. Is there a
way for you to contact the placers and ask them to contact Jefferson
County Open Space to get a permit? While most the sites would be
approved, all letterboxes without permits will have to be removed. The
following is a list of the sites without permits...

Zeke's Letterbox
Soar Feet
Rain Cloud
Wheel of the Year - Litha
Deer Creek
Stick Deer
Homesteader Trail
White Ranch Park

Your assistance in contacting the placers is appreciated.

Visitor & Resource Protection Supervisor
Jefferson County Open Space

Re: Jefferson Cty Open Space

From: Eoghan (eoghan@sharawadgi.com) | Date: 2004-08-24 23:10:29 UTC
D'oh! Here comes the red tape. ;-)
It's nice to have more people l'boxing in that you have more boxes,
but here comes the downside - increase in oversight by various land
management agencies.
I'm glad that JeffCo has been so good about allowing the hobbies to go
on with just a little bit of a check up to make sure boxes aren't
causing any damage. They're really a model of friendly and appropriate
response. Other counties (Boulder, for example) have just jumped the
gun with thoughtless prohibitions or threats to prosecute people for
'littering'. I've been considering going legit with my boxes in JeffCo
for awhile but guess I don't have much choice now. Coleen didn't list
some of my other boxes that are in the OS parks but I plan on becoming
respectable for all the ones I've hidden in those parks.

It'd be nice if one permit could handle everyone, but I think that the
real point is for JeffCo to have a chance to review each box one at a
time to make sure they are responsibly hidden.

Another thought is that we invite her to join the RMLB list so she
knows we are friendly and willing to cooperate with JeffCoOS policies.
I personaly think it important that regardless of the similarities
letterboxing has with geocaching, that we, as a smaller group, work to
differentiate ourselves from the geocachers, if only because we are
smaller, more local, and less under the thumb of a single big .com
company. (I'm not bashing geocaching, I've been doing it for a long
time too.) It just seems that because MORE people geocache than
letterbox, we should try to emphasize that our 'boxes' get less
traffic and that because of the stamp our boxes are typically more
thoughtfully crafted and hidden than much of the 'geotrash' hidden out
there.

Just my dos centavos,
-Eoghan
