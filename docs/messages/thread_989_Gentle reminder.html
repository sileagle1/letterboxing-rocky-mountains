Gentle reminder

From: autumnfq (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2006-01-22 21:24:02 UTC
Hey folks,
I've been reading the posts, and (admittedly) laughing a lot.
However, I wanted to remind everyone that some of our group members
are Geocachers as well as Letterboxers.

Jely, that article was hilarious - but folks, let's just remember
that we're all in this together, and I dont want posts that may
insult some of our other members. This isn't a geocaching message
board, but I wouldn't want anyone who participated in both hobbies to
feel unwelcome.

I think the posts asking for cache trinkets to be removed from boxes
were appropriate, and I will add my two cents that if anyone finds
trinkets in my boxes, please take them out, and do whatever you need
to to ensure that cachers dont mistake my box for one of theirs.

Happy Trails,
Autumn
(who sometimes has to do that board moderator stuff)







Re: Gentle reminder

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2006-01-22 22:26:19 UTC
Oh, I know, Autumn, and I wasn't trying to offend anyone or make
them feel unwelcome. If I did, I publicly apologize, and will
punish myself with a week of no pie. I know that any geocachers who
read this board are sensitive to both geocaching and letterboxing,
and wouldn't take the stamp out of a letterbox. It is frustrating,
though, when one of your boxes is put permanently out of commission
by a geocacher less careful than the good folks who read this
board. I would call upon those who enjoy both hobbies to try to
spread the word about letterboxing within the geocaching community,
in hopes that future geocachers will be more careful with
letterboxes if they stumble upon them.

JeLyBean
