Happy 2nd Anniversary RMLetterboxing!

From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2005-07-07 17:54:48 UTC
Gosh, it doesn't seem like two years - but this board was created on
the 4th of July, 2003 and members started joining around the 10th. We
started out so small, with so few boxers (and boxes) in the Rocky
Mountain area.

It's great to see how this list has grown, and how the hobby has taken
off in Colorado, Utah, Idaho, and New Mexico! I'm hoping Wyoming will
take off soon, too! I still remember fondly our first "semi-official"
gathering at Roxborough Park that summer. *hugs to all who were there*

We have such a great bunch of boxers - pirates - pie lovers - carvers -
scavengers - hikers - trailblazers - and friends. I'm proud to be a
part of it.

Best wishes to everyone, and may there always be a Find at the end of
your trail!

Autumn
RMLetterboxing founder -
who is much, much too quiet on this list
but absolutely loves pie ...*grins*

What fun to remember! I can't believe it was two years ago that I joined
this crazy bunch. I remember thinking that I probably wouldn't really have a
place here since you were all in Colorado, but I decided to come to the
group since I am in the Rockies region and it was the closest I could come
to having a regional group with only a few boxers in my state. I knew most
of the focus of the group would be there, but look how we've grown. I know
most of the focus is still in Colorado, but I still love reading your posts
and sharing with you. It only took a moment of watching in Moab to know that
we have been a group of friends and it didn't matter where we were. I'm so
glad to be associated with you scurvy pirate pie lovers! You are a fine
bunch. Thanks for being my LB friend over these last couple of years.

Yep, this group sure has seen a lot of fun letterboxing in two years.
And the Moab gathering was a good example of how widespread, but
tightly knit it is.

Thinking of the Anniversary makes me think back to when I joined. The
kindness and help that I recieved was just phenomenal. Unlike our
newbie Kris, I hunted for an obscure letterbox near me for about a
month without any luck. If I wasn't having so much fun reading the
posts here, I don't think I would have kept at it. The kindness I
felt from Silver Eagle, Autumn, Cadenza, and also from Jay Drew
really shaped the way I participate in letterboxing. And in the years
since, I have formed some friendships in this group that have become
the real reward of this pastime.

Thanks Autumn, and happy Anniversary.
