From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-07-05 21:00:22 UTC
I am definitely *not* an expert on this, but I did recently read a
note on the main talk list about a geo-cacher recently finding a
letterbox and 'trading' for the hand-carved stamp. The poster said
that there was a geo-cache somewhere very close by and the cachers
had stumbled onto the letterbox first and thought it was their
target.

I have no idea if there is a geo-cache near the Bear Creek box...but
I do remember seeing a note in there saying something about that box
being someone's first geo-cache find. Did you happen to go through
the journal and see that? It was raining so I didn't stop to look
too close...just sort of scanned them, stamped, rehid it and ran!

It was well-hidden when we found it, but who knows if it was when the
cacher found it. And I certainly can't say they were the ones who
took the stamp - that was just a possibility! Maybe someone spotted
someone else at the box and found it after they left? I've been kind
of shocked at how poorly re-hidden a few boxes we've found have been.
We found one in Oregon that was literally just sitting at the foot of
a tree right next to the trail. Fortunately we were there about 20
minutes after the person who 're-hid' it in plain sight so all was
well.

Esmerelda

From: Kristen Fulton (kfulton11@adelphia.net) | Date: 2004-07-05 15:11:28 UTC-06:00
I did see that in the log book, and also saw a couple entries from people
who said they stumbled on it by accident. It was well hidden when I found
it, but I had a bit of a hard time putting it back and getting it hidden
again, so maybe some people are just not patient enough to make sure it's
well hidden. If the geocacher took the stamp, she would have left something
in return, right? Who knows? :-) I found my first hitchhiker in that box
today, so that made me feel better about the stamp being missing! I have a
box that I am going to plant soon... I think I am going to use a Sharpie to
write on the back of the stamp "This is not a geocache, please do not take
this stamp." I would hate for all my hard work to be taken because someone
mistook it for a prize.
- Kristen
