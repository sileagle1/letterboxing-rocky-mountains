From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2005-05-31 03:07:18 UTC
Don't go letterboxing in the rain.

Just dont.

The first box was not horrible. Took my umbrella, and managed to
only get somewhat dirty while rummaging underneath the overgrown
junipers. I spent the drive to the next box pulling juniper twigs
out of my hair, and bemoaning my wet boots.

I should have stopped there, but hell .... I'd driven about 75 miles
so one box was just not gonna cut it. My next stop was at a wetlands
preserve, wildlife area. Beautiful, simply stunning scenery. I must
remember to go back when it's nicer out. The lightning started about
then, and I worried as I walked with my umbrella - backpack in tow.

My boots were thoroughly soaked by this point, right through to my
socks which were making squicky squicky noises. But I pressed
forward, determined not to let the day end with only one box. I got
to the destination, and could see the hiding place of the box - down
a slight embankment toward the pond. Cant do that with my umbrella.
So, I set my bag down, put the umbrella over it, and proceeded to
pick my way carefully down the embankment - it wasn't long.

But as expected on a day like this, the ground gave out beneath me
and I fell onto my ass, sliding down the embankment and giving a
liberal coating of mud to my backside in the process. I got the
box. Washed my hands in the pond, stamped in, replaced the box (not
falling this time).

By the time I got back to my car, I dont think there was an inch of
dry skin on my body. My backpack was covered in mud, as was my
umbrella, and most of my clothing. My logbook is wet, my hair has
some funny twig/seed/leafy things in it, my boots have stopped making
the squicky noise and have just started to oooze mud.

And I have a 75 mile drive home.

Ahhhhh letterboxing. Gotta love it.

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-05 14:35:26 UTC
Not to self....Don't laugh at other people's 'boxing in the rain
stories. Just don't.

It was really nice in Grand Junction when my kids found Big Fish, but
it started to sprinkle over at Johnny Carino's. The storm lifted by
the time we got to Palisade so we started down the trail for There
Were These Two Guys.... After the first two boxes set up the joke it
started to rain again, but like any obsessed father I talked my kids
into pressing on. I had the box with the punchline in my hand when
Dark Side of the Moon shouted through the deluge that we couldn't
open up the box in the downpour without ruining the contents. "Maybe
we could find a tree to sit under", I spit, hopefully. DSOTM just
waved his arm towards the stream of water running down the trunk of
the biggest tree there. Elros stopped shivering and started running
back as I rehid the box. Then we spent a fun night listening to the
rain on the tent and trying to guess the punchline to the joke. We
couldn't.

We laughed hard when we got the punchline yesterday morning and then
headed up onto Grand Mesa. It hadn't rained up there at all so we had
fun looking for letterboxes in the four inches of FRESH snow with
another storm hitting about every twenty minutes. We found two out of
the four up there and then headed back home, soaked, but quite
content to have found some truly amazing letterboxes.


y-nought
