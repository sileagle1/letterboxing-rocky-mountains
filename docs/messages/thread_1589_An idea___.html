Hello everyone -

On the heels of that last post, I have a question for y'all.

Aljan and I were commenting the other day on how folks ask us how we do
what we do. What are our techniques? How do we come up with ideas? What
materials do we use? And there are plenty of you out there that I would
like to ask those very same questions.

Is anyone interested in a workshop? Don't ask me when, this is a very
preliminary idea, but if we all got together, brought our carving,
stamping, book making stuff, and we can do demos, and answer each
others questions?

I never mind answering questions about how I do what I do. I know we
are all striving to do bigger, better things with our art, and if I can
help foster anyone's ideas, I'm all for it!

~RavenWolf

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-31 18:31:29 UTC
For Denver Libraries it's necessary to be either an actual non-profit
(401 (c) 3 to get free rooms and/or be part of a Registered
Neighborhood Organization. In the first case then the event would have
to be free and open to the public where all can participate fully.
Otherwise the fee is $10/hour.

Here's the link for more DPL info
http://denverlibrary.org/about/meetings/index.html

I've also had the idea for something like this for a while, and also
think mid-winter when we can't box seems best. Personally, I'd also
love it if there were a way to incorporate folks who might already be
interested in the creative and outdoors aspects of 'boxing and get some
new folks involved, in addition to tutorial and workshops type sessions
for folks already involved. One place that's come to mind is to see if
Meiningers (high-end art store) here in Denver might be willing to
host. They already have a calendar of events program that might be fun
to collaborate with??

In any case, I'm also interested and would be willing to help put
something together. Lemme know how/if I can help.

I wonder if these aren't two distinct ideas. A workshop intended
for people who already have some experience with letterboxing but
want to improve their carving/logbook making/etc would probably

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2008-09-16 20:47:01 UTC
Sorry for the lag-time in responding. Samurai, Amaya and I went to
the Live and Breathe event in OR. I will give a full report with
picts as soon as I am able!


> 1) I've noticed some medium that seems to be twice the thickness of
> other medium...what is it?

Is it white? Probably MasterCarve. You used to be able to find it at
Hobby Lobby.

> 3) Log book making...some are absolutely fantastic, just
beautiful.
> Ideas would be great for us less than creative folk.

Absolutely. Lots of questions about that one.

> 5) Why won't the magnetic tape I bought work worth squat...do you
> need special sized magnets to hold an altoid box in place?

Magnets come in different 'degrees' if you will. The magnetic tape I
find doesn't 'hold' very tightly. You can find magnets that hold
together so tight, you almost can't get them apart. Check the local
craft stores for magnets with a higher 'rating'.


> 6) Has anyone tried making home made paper for a logbook?

Yes. Here is the problem with handmade paper: While it is very, very
pretty, it is also very fiberous. Ink tends to bleed into the paper
badly, and it leaves a poor image. Cardstock is great. It is thick
enough that you can stamp on both sides without bleedthrough, and
it's not too thick to work with. If you want to know more about
homeade paper, I would be happy to share! =3


> 7) Technique...I've notice that many carvers make thicker lines on
> their stamps...I'm having to recarve some of my stamps that are
> tearing.

Hmmm... Tearing how? Do you back your stamps onto anything? Can you
post a pict?

> 8) Reverse negative images? Say what?

Did you ever exchange with Autumn when she had her first siggy stamp?
That was a reverse or negative image. It is where the actual image
doesn't stamp. You cut it out of the material, leaving most of the
material behind. Ack. Does that make sense? I'll see if I can post a
couple of images...

> 9) Do's and don'ts for letterboxing etiquette, e.g. what not to
talk about at an event and other formal tie affairs.

Probably a matter of opinion, but certainly open for discussion!

> 10) Stamp backing, what works, what doesn't

Also a matter of opinion. What have you tried?

Hope that answers at least some of your questions!

~RavenWolf
