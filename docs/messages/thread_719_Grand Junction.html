How funny! My boys and I are headed for Grand Junction tomorrow morning
and we hope to find a KOA-Style campground for tomorrow night.
Originally, we had hoped to camp up on Grand Mesa at one of the many
Forest Service campgrounds. But Esmerelda just tipped me off that the
campgrounds are still covered with snow up there.

I have camped up at the Colorado National Monumaent campground and I
just loved it. It is a lot like Moab, but less crowded, and the view of
Grand Valley is awesome. We would probably camp there, but we want to
get all of the GJ/Palisade boxes tomorrow evening and get an early
start up to Grand Mesa Saturday for the ones in the snow.(There are two
very new ones there, and two almost a year old).

I'll have to get back to you on the campgrounds and the newer boxes,
right now I'll recommend Big Fish. And I was lucky enough to see some
wild horses on the way back from Mustang, which was cool. But any of
Esmerelda's letterboxes are a real treat.

y-nought

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-06-03 15:53:31 UTC
Ooo! Ooo! I know someone who lives in Grand Junction! ::jumps up and
down with arm in air:: woot!

The Koa is indeed in Clifton... very near the I-70 exit and right in
between Grand Junction and Palisade, so it's a nice, central location.

Highline Lake is waaaaaay in the heck out in the boonies at the west
end of the valley. My experience is that it's mostly used by the
people who head out to the Lake for boating. I probably would suggest
one of the others before this.

Island Acres is also right off I-70 and a nice spot.. it isn't all that
central, but it's such an easy place to get to that it's not bad. It's
near the exit to Grand Mesa and a few miles from Palisade.

The National Monument is in another corner of the valley, but it really
is beautiful up there. Like Y said, very much like Moab. A little
further from everything, but if you're not in a hurry, no worries.

We're working on getting some more boxes placed soon!

If you need any phone numbers or information or whatever, give me a
shout!

Es, excited for visitors
