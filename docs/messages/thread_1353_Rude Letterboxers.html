From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2007-06-28 15:41:05 UTC
Hey folks, you ever run into this? Someone sends you an email telling
you they had a bad time looking for one of your boxes, couldn't find
it, your clues were horrible, etc?

I have met so many nice folks through this hobby, I am astounded when I
get a note like this. Fortunately, it happens very infrequently. I
have found about 250 boxes or so, and not all of them have been stellar
experiences, but I would never dream of writing an impolite note to
someone telling them their box, their stamp or their clues were bad.
People spend so much time, money and effort placing boxes for my
enjoyment, how could I possibly ever criticize someone, even if I
didn't enjoy it? It's like getting a birthday gift and telling the
person, "Oh I would never wear this," or having your kid give you a
picture they painted for you and telling them, "this is the worst
artwork I've ever seen."

Honestly, I don't know what's wrong with some people. Makes me want to
pull in all of my public boxes.

JeLyBean, just blowing off steam...

From: cadenza74 (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-07-11 08:36:41 UTC
hi folks! Cadenza is still alive and ticking here in Wales!!

I just checked in to approve a first post and thought I'd see what was
going on when this post caught my eye. I was remembering when a very
active letterboxer came to visit the Yellowstone area. They stopped
in Idaho Falls and apparently looked for one of my box series. I got
this terse email from them saying that they thought I had planted the
boxes too close to the water and they had all gone missing in the
Snake River because they had looked everywhere and couldn't find them
(this was in the middle of a drought, by the way-nowhere near the
water). When I wrote back encouraging them to keep looking and saying
that the series was most definitely there they were nice enough to
tell me that I just wrote them badly then. They were quite snippety
about it and my feelings were somewhat hurt. I'd had tons of people
look for those letterboxes and never had any problems with my clues,
and this was a letterboxer with enough experience that they shouldn't
have had a problem.

About a month later there were some questions on the big list or AQ or
somewhere about trips up to Yellowstone and this person got on telling
all about how there were great letterboxes to be found in the area and
they sang the praises of every letterbox but mine. Now, not to boast
or anything, but I have some of my best ever stamps in that area. I
mean, I have some fun letterboxes around there. I couldn't help but
feel that the choice to exclude me from the list of good letterboxers
was a deliberate one, since I was the only local Yellowstone-area
letterboxer. I had only tried to be helpful, and I couldn't help but
feel that the tone of everything they did was bratty. I can respect
an email saying that they didn't find a box. I appreciate someone
letting me know there might be a problem, but this was very different.
And then choosing to very publicly ignore me? I mean, how high-school
is that?
