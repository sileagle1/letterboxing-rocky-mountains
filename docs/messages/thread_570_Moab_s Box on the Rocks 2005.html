The camping area on highway 313 is NOT the most centrally located for
letterboxing. We just like it because you can get some privacy, and
there are usually some spots open. It is "primitive" in that there
are no restrooms or dumpsters.

My suggestions would be, (and Kat would know better so she might jump
in here):

Highway 279 heading west between Arches NP and Moab there are a
couple of campgrounds. Jaycees is pretty nice and private but usually
full, and there is one further down by the river that is wide open
camping on a sandy beach by the River with few trees. From this road
you can drive-by Moab Man and take a short hike to Ticaboo and Lulu's
Day out. The boxes on the Portal Trail are on this road too with a
bit of a steep hike.

To acces the MOST boxes without long hikes I would recommend one of
the several campgrounds on highway 128 that heads east from just
north of Moab. There are LOTS of campsites on this road, and any will
do, I've stayed at Big Bend and I liked it. You can Drive By Desert
Bighorn, Paddle Hard, Colorado River, and The Westerns. Take a short
hike to Searching...., a medium hike to Alien Gothic, and a little
longer hike to Negro Bill. Rumor has it that another new box is going
in on the trail up Negro Bill Canyon just that week.

If any of you folks are driving around campgrounds and see an old red
Isuzu Trooper with a bumper Sticker that says "Get A Clue- Go
Letterboxing", that will be us and we would be happy to make room for
you around our fire pit.

I hope this helps,

y-nought

Re: Moab's Box on the Rocks 2005

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2005-03-22 22:29:57 UTC

I think Y covered the camping area pretty well! The camp sites along
the river are wonderful, but can be noisy with the road right next
to it. His site along 313 is a great site (there are some fun
petroglyphs at the canyon mouth of 7 mile canyon), quiet and less
traveled. It is along the road leading to Dead Horse Point &
Canyonlands NP. There are a few spectacular sites at Dead Horse
Point and in Arches NP, but harder to get. The Arches sites have a
few walk-in signups everyday, but you must be at the visitor center
at 7:30 am to get one! Fisher Towers has a few sites (gorgeous) and
is located very near the fabulous Alien Gothic & The Westerns Boxes,
but is a longer drive (30-40 minutes) to town. Kane Creek Canyon
(turn right at McDonald's and follow the rock rim till you're out
along the river) has some nice spots with less traffic noise, but is
a party area (I always see a lot of trash and bottles left around )
and really south of town there isn't a lot. Behind the Rocks has
some sites, but I don't think they're real nice, plus you get a lot
of off roaders tearing around. Sand Flats Rec. area has some
beautiful spots. It is up the hill from Rotary Park just past the
Slickrock Bike Trail. It has been quite a party spot in the past,
but volunteers and BLM have really worked hard building sites and
blocking off old roads and old illegal sites. This is the only time
of the year that this area is really beautiful for camping (really
HOT in the summer). I'll let you know if I think of anywhere else!

Kat (ready to throw in her opinion if needed!)
