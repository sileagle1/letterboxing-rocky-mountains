From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2008-07-23 00:45:15 UTC
Don't get me wrong. I think the LTC's are a wonderful art form and I plan to add mine to
this artform. But I don't think they are so involved with letterboxing itself. To me, they are
two completely different things, and as an artist and an outdoor enthusiast, they are both
terrific and can be appreciated as such.

As to the comment about people putting new boxes out there, if new boxes were
constantly appearing everywhere, you would have quantity vs. quality and it would be just
like in Connecticut where boxers are running into each other on the trails. To me, that
sounds like a nightmare. I love the fact that boxes are an unexpected treat when they are
planted, whether it is a Monet or an 'oops I tried'. There is not enough time in this fun
lifetime to do all the things there are to do, and letterboxing is just one of so many things.
And remember, nobody was born carving, it takes some practice and everyone I know has
gotten better and better at it. My original stamps are probably a disappointment to some
people, but it is fun to watch the progression of letterboxers and their styles.

And by the way, an awful lot of the boxes I have found in other parts of the country
contain storebought stamps and journals.

KAT
