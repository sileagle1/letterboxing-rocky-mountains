What a wonderful trip and the perfect time of the year for it!

In Durango I highly recommend 'Campus in the Sky'...you have to
solve clues (not as hard as the onion) and it's so fun when you see
the really clever places y-nought has stashed stuff. :) But it's
not so difficult that it will take you an entire day.

I'd also suggest a run up to Animas Overlook.. the view from there
is splendiferous, it's not extremely difficult to get to, and you
can do a series of boxes all by different boxers. Y-nought's
new 'Celtic Tree' box is on the way up, too. I haven't made it to
that box yet, but I'm confident it's worth the stop.

Rio-Grande is pretty much a drive by, so an easy one if you're in
the area near it.

Are you driving over the pass into Ouray? What a drive! I love it!
Engineer Mountain is a fabulous hike, but it does take some time.
The trail is just breathtaking and well worth it if you have the
time. Along that road is 'Wandering Aengus' which you can do easily
and in a short time.. I love that one. Further along is 'Wings of
Eagles'.. right on the top of the pass and not terribly far from the
road. Down the mountain and into Ouray.. several boxes there but
I've only been to the 'Box Canyon' box which is easy to get to and
find.

Are you just zooming through Grand Junction or will you have any
boxing time? They have good pie here. Mmmmm... pie.

Es, foofing up the valley for guests

I checked on Celtic Tree today because I heard they had to bring in
some equipment to save some trailers from the flooding on Junction
Creek. Happily, it's high and dry, although I did have to wade
through some refreshingly frigid water to get to it.

Here are some of my favorite boxes on your route that Esmerelda
didn't mention:

There is one in Del Norte and one by Wolf Creek Pass that are
driveby's.

There is a rather long but flattish hike to a Drewclan box near
Pagosa Springs. Those waterfalls are probably thundering now. Bigtime.

Between Delta and Grand Junction is a cool letterbox in Escalante
Canyon by Esmerelda.

And then Big Fish in Grand Junction is one of my alltime favorites. I
haven't found There Were These Two Guys.... yet, but I want to,
baaaad.
