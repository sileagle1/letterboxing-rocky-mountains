Hello Group

Glad to find some folks talking letterboxing in areas that I might
actually get to. I was down on the front range earlier this summer
but didn't have time for LBing - darn. Sounds like you've got some
fun ones going down there. I did make a trip to SLC & was able to
do some Utah & Idaho boxes then. And I see there are now some in
the Moab area that I'll look forward to nabbing when I head down
there to bike next spring. Not much here in western Montana but
we're trying to change that.

I'd be interested in a discussion on creating clues. So far the
boxes I've planted have been a mixture - a couple straightforward
drive-bys for those just passing through; one long hike in an area
I enjoy but that I seriously doubt others will ever bother with; a
couple mystery boxes - one dead easy the other I think tougher
though I haven't had feedback on it yet; and several that are sort
of intermediate with clues gained by exploring signs in the area &
that sort of thing.

How are people inspired to come up with creative clues?

From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-08-23 14:40:30 UTC-06:00
Welcome to the group! It's nice to know there is another planter out
there! =] We are in Littleton, CO and have made it a mission to plant
kid friendly boxes in the area. most are 1/2 mile walks or shorter...
our longest was the one we did this weekend that is .67 miles each way.

As for clues... I started out with basic straight forward directions in
local areas that I knew very well. Recently, I added a story clue...
It's a stamp of a dog that my mother-in-law suggested to place in a dog
park close by. And, this weekend, I did my very first mystrery clue (I
make no promises on how good it is). When we started boxing, I found
that I love to carve! So, I ended up doing the 12 zodiacs... however,
we have found little time to get out and place them, so only about half
are out there... when I finish planting those, I plan to start creating
stamps that are specific to the place I will put them. Clues are hard
for me, as I am terrible at rhyming or coming up with stories, etc.
However, I love Esmerelda's clues that are in the form of a story. I
also enjoy clues that tell you about the area where you will be boxing.

Hope that gives you some ideas! =]


Debbi

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-23 22:56:33 UTC
Woohoo! Greetings and welcome to Rocky Mountain Letterboxing!

I did a series of boxes based on an old mountain man (Zebediah Gump)
and had lots of fun writing stories that included him in some real
history (remember Forest Gump?). Once I research the
incident/person/location it's pretty easy to just create a story
about it that eventually has Zeb lead us to the spot. I've had some
really nice feedback from people about it, which makes it all the
more fun for me. Zeb may have some more chapters show up here one of
these days.

I've also based one on a movie and put it in a location that fits
into the plot line...then written a story which included the movie's
characters with a 'new' adventure for them. (Big Fish)

Y-nought does a wonderful job of using clues, poems, cyphering and
stories. I'm not smart enough to write ones with puzzles, but they
sure are fun to solve! Or try to solve! (Cursed onion!!)

Speaking for myself, I just love when the clues have something
besides the very basic directions. I don't really care so much if
they're by the side of the road or in a valley you have to climb to
so much as I do about learning or seeing something new and
having a great adventure.

Esmerelda

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-08-24 20:15:57 UTC
Welcome mt walker,

I have done a mixture of styles, much like you have. My first was a
longish hike on one of my favorite trails.
Then I did one in town on a level path.

Then a harder coded clue, mostly for people who live here (Durango
Colorado) and have the time to work it out. No newbies have surfaced
for that one yet, although it has been solved by a visiting pirate.

After that I did a coded clue in Utah and thought I was done with
planting boxes. But, alas, I see other boxes or clues or a perfect
spot and I feel compelled to make another one. One thing I do
differently now (I have only been doing this a little over a year) is
I take a little longer to plant them. I look at them for a while and
get to know them before setting them free. And I will often change
them a few times before they go. It beats running out there to put in
a new logbook or stamp after they have been planted.

Oops.... Now I'm rambling......
