From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-02-21 11:19:03 UTC-07:00
Since you asked . . .

Carving with a needle is an interesting thing. In some ways it was
great and in some ways it was a pain.

I just went to one of the many local farm supply stores out here and
asked for the needles. There were a bunch of different sizes so I
bought about four sizes. I've found it isn't worth the effort to use
the bigger ones. I can get the same results from my normal tools with
a more comfortable hand.

The needles are for veterinary use. You should have seen the look on
the sales person's face when I explained that I wanted them for
carving rubber stamps. She told me that she thought she had heard
every possible strange and bizarre use for the needles (not all legal
either), but that this was definitely the most unusual. One nice
thing was the needles only cost 15-25 cents each!

You don't use the tube of the needle, like you might think. In fact,
you won't come near the tube. The sharp part at the bottom can be
used like a very small X-acto. It works nicely for very fine detail.
The needles are small though and weren't made to be held that way. I
tried buying a syringe that fit them, but it was too cumbersome to
use. I found that if I carved for a long time with the needles, my
hands cramped up more than usual, so I only used them for very fine
detail. I did like the results though. I really don't use them tons
since I have got pretty good at using the speedball for even fine
detail, but sometimes you just need a knife and the exacto feels huge
on very tiny detail. Give it a try! I've known some that loved the
results and others that hated carving with them. I think it is a
personal choice. I tend to get pretty picky with some of the quality
of my own carving, so I liked them.

Hope this helps--let me know if you have other questions,
Cadenza
