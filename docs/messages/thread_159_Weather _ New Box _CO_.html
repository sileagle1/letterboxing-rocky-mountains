SoC#2: Hairstreak Butterfly
Placed by: JeLyBean (contact the placer)
Placement date: Feb 14 2004
State: Colorado
County: El Paso
Nearest city: Colorado Springs
Number of boxes: 1

Clues
Symbols of Colorado #2: Hairstreak Butterfly. The state insect of
Colorado, the Hairstreak is a native insect found here and in other
areas of the Southwest. The caterpillars emerge in spring and
develop on oak leaves, particularly Gambel Oak (Quercus gambelii).
The adults appear beginning in June and can be seen throughout
summer. They are easily identified by the characteristic
slender "tail" protruding from the hind wing and by their beautiful
coloration: upper sides of the wings are purple with a wide dark
border and orange spots on the outer edge of each wing. The
Hairstreak may typically be found amongst oak thickets.

Terrain: Easy. Less than two miles round trip over mostly level
paths. Some easy climbing to the box.
Clues: Fairly easy.

Directions: Reach Ute Valley Park by taking the Woodman or
Rockrimmon Exits off I-25. Follow Rockrimmon to the intersection of
Vindicator and head west. The parking lot for Ute Valley Park will
be on your left past the school.

Clues: Hit the boards, all right? Wind around the hill and take the
only trail just for hikers. Walk for a while. When you get to the
appropriate spot, make like a troll. Lean against the right side and
take a compass reading of 94*. Up through the evergreen branches,
you may see a place that's just a hole in the wall. Of the several
choices, the upper is preferred by butterflies. Still missing it?
