68 boxes in CO and then some ...

From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2004-05-01 07:39:49 UTC
Holy smokes, this passtime is growing! :) Just over a year ago,
when I started boxing, we had fewer than 20 boxes in Colorado, and
most were planted at such altitudes or such difficult trail lengths
as to be impossible for the average weekend boxer.

Thanks to everyone who is planting and sharing in the hobby!

68 boxes in CO
23 boxes in WY
119 boxes in ID (jeez!)
46 boxes in AZ
30 boxes in NM
40 boxes in UT

326 boxes in the Rocky Mountain Area! *happyhikingbootdance*

Re: 68 boxes in CO and then some ...

From: Rachel (rachelincolorado@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-02 03:15:15 UTC
I must agree in the excitement with everyone! Today we found THREE
boxes on such an incredible day - luckily the snow melted from two
days ago - AGAIN!!! Anyway, we were just talking about how we have
found all of the 'local' boxes in our area. We've yet to find all
the ones in Colorado Springs plus others in the high country - that
will be a power boxing weekend! Plus we are working on hiding
several more very soon - stay tuned!

Today was excellent! Even though we started boxing in Texas ( in
2000) - wow that was four years ago - we are becoming more active in
Colorado now!

Found today:
Woody the Beaver - we were the first to stamp in!
Rock Crawler - second attempt - wow, that was hidden very well!!!!
The Miner (Gold Rush) - great condition - excellent stamp! Allowing
us to drive through Clear Creek Canyon - we hadn't been there yet -
and three tunnels - awesome day for this drive! Thanks!

Keep on hiding, keep on boxing!
Cheers,
Pumpkin Princess
