Helen Hunt Falls box rescued/relocated

From: Eoghan (eoghan@sharawadgi.com) | Date: 2004-01-14 20:31:56 UTC
Jelybean,
Pumpkin Princess and I were going for a few hikes in Cheyenne Canon
this past weekend and accidentally ended up letterboxing! We hadn't
planned on it so we didn't print out any clues. While walking up the
trail to the upper falls we found the Helen Hunt Falls letterbox bag
stuck on a branch hanging over the trail. Wow. We've found some easy
boxes before, but never had one materialize in front of us without
even looking for it! Apparently it had washed out of its hiding place
or someone had found it by accident and not replaced it. To keep it
from being stolen or thrown away we rehid the box. Unfortunately we
didn't know where it belonged so we had to hide it somewhere else.
Hopefully it will remain safe until it can be moved to its proper
home. We hid it in a pile of logs to the right of the trail about 20'
before the small wooden bridge near the top of the trail. Let me know
if you need a better description of the site. Hope it stays put.
Thanks for hiding the box.
-Eoghan

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2004-01-15 00:22:47 UTC
Curious! However, that's not my box. It was placed by some parties
named piano&penguins who are still listed as the active owners on
the LbNA site. You should probably contact them through the site.
Goddess and I found that box back on Jan 1, and the place was VERY
busy. We had to sit around for quite some time before a break in
the traffic allowed us to stash it again. As it is, the box was in
a location not very far off the trail, with what I consider to be
inadequate cover. We hid it better than we found it, but I would
not be surprised if it was accidentally discovered and plundered.
The box itself was very large and obvious in its hiding location.

JeLyBean
