Missing Boxes

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2004-09-04 15:34:08 UTC
You know, after my first box went missing, I was truly upset for the
lost work, lost journal, lost fun for other, and many more reasons.
After a few of these happenings, I had come to accept it. Kinda
like the five stages of loss/trauma, you know, anger, bargaining,
acceptance...

Anyway, I've since changed my philosophy about my letterboxes. Once
I put a letterbox out there, I assume it's gone for good and really
no longer mine. If I happen to be hiking in the same spot later,
I'll stop in and see if I was able to provide others with a little
fun. If it's gone, there will be others, I intend to never stop
placing new boxes.

I have had the improper hiding thing happen many times. It must be
people who let their kids re-assemble and rehide the box. I have a
hard time believing an adult letterboxer would be so careless. I
recently found my butterfly box with the contents just dumped in and
the baggies wadded up and stuffed in there. I mean, if you're
putting everything back in, how hard is it to put them in the bags
first? Had to be kids. Right?

What bothered me the most about Letchworth was the blatant theft.
In the past, I had the luxury of not knowing why it went missing, so
I could assume maybe some nature lover decided to clean up
some "litter" or an animal had dragged it away, or some such
fantasy. But to have someone tell me specifically that a person had
not only stolen it but had shown it around to their co-workers,
proud of their act, that's just evil. And now this person knows of
the LbNA site and could potentially hunt for and steal other boxes.
It bothers me that we can really do nothing to stop a person like
this.

I wonder if it'd be worthwhile or effective to start placing some
kind of note in the box that says something like, "This box is
property of Letterboxing North America. Deliberate removal or
destruction of this box may be subject to penalties." Has little
teeth, I know, but might serve as a mild deterrant...

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-04 16:42:51 UTC

In rock climbing there is a warning that is passed around to deter
theft. Extra climbing gear is often left at the bottom of a climb and
scoundrels can take it in plain view of the owner, but since they are
a few hundred feet up nothing can be done. So everyone says that
stolen gear will not catch you if you fall- even if you bought it
from the thief. Superstitious, I know, but some people just don't buy
used gear, and if they do they check out the karmic history.

So, writing a curse into the box may deter some.
