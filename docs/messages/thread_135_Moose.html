I've seen some beautiful elk and deer - but nothing of the moose variety.

Here's a question for the list:
What critters have YOU run into on the trails while boxing?

While in AZ on a looooong hike for the Southwestern Trio I came this (hold
fingers about an inch apart) close to stepping on a snake that had come
out to sun itself on the trail. Yikes!

Also - the Plains Conservation Center boxes have a plethora of wildlife -
prairie dog town, coyote, burrowing owls, and a really nice small herd of
white tailed deer crossed my path on the way to one of the boxes there.

And out in California - had an interesting encounter with a homeless
person while boxing with California Bear. Not an animal, really - he was
quite nice! :)

I have been fortunate enough to see many critters while hiking
outside of Texas, a lot of them in the Rocky Mt. area. I have
seen coyote, elk, moose, gizzly bear (in Alaska), black bear,
polar bear (Canada), buffalo, pronghorn, mountain goats,
big horn sheep, fox, marmot & pika.
The one critter I still have not seen is a mountain lion.
Baby Bear and I once got chased out of our campsite by a black
bear while backpacking in Minnesota, and a black bear once
chased me away from my dinner at a campsite in California.
Scared me so bad I laid awake most of the night in my tent!
I envy all of you that live so close to such beauty & wildlife.

From: (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-12-14 03:03:06 UTC-07:00
While I've seen others, I'll say the ones I've seen while letterboxing since
I began last spring. (Another reason to love letterboxing, it allows you to
get out and enjoy stuff like this!)

On the LB trail I have seen the aforementioned bull moose as well as elk,
coyote, bison, a rattlesnake (ooh, don't get me started on that one. I saw
it as I was just about to step on it!! Luckily it was asleep or something
because I moved quickly enough to avoid it and it didn't move), of course
squirrels and chipmunks, marmuts, a variety of trout (in the same spot I
have seen a salmon run before I discovered letterboxing, it's right by my
Big Springs Series now), trumpeter swans, golden and bald eagles, osprey,
peregrine falcons, blue herons, pelicans, canadian geese, and many dams from
beavers. The beavers themselves stayed hidden.

Oh, and my friend's black lab puppy that we took along once for fun :-) he
made a great diversion while we sent others hunting for a box.

Outside of LBing the other ones I've seen on the trail have been things like
deer, a porcupine (that was a fun one), a grizzly bear and it's two cubs,
antelope, oh, and I think about the whole of my family often counts as
wildlife :-)

Those are the main critters I can think of. Dang, I love where I live!
Isn't life near the mountains the best! I don't think I could give that up.

Cadenza
