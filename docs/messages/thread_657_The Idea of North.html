Island Park Idaho is a bit farther for me. But it would also fulfill
a lifelong dream. I would not be able to go to the Tetons without
climbing a peak, so for me it would be a weekend of boxing and a week

So, just throwing my two cents into the ring, I'd love to gather
around Yellowstone. The best times for me (and for climbing) would be
May-June or September. But I would try to make it anytime there was a
group of letterboxers getting together.

And a pirate told me once that there is an awesome Museum in Cody. If
you can believe pirates.

Re: [RMLetterboxing] The Idea of North

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-04-21 09:18:24 UTC-06:00
there are amazing museums in Cody. It is an interesting town. Outside of the
hotel Buffalo Bill Cody built for his daughter, Irma, they have shootouts
each evenening for the tourists. It's pretty funny. There are also a bunch
of letterboxes, some by Don and Gwen, but most are by a small group of
school kids in Wapiti. There are a lot of manufactured stamps, but this is
the only time I didn't give a flip about that. It was so cute to know that a
group of school kids, in what is almost a one room school house from the
outside, decided that letterboxing would be a fun way to teach themselves
and others about the history of their area. It made my day to find most of
their boxes.
I wasn't really thinking this year would work. It sounds as if there are
already enough plans in the works and a third gathering in a few months may
be a bit much. I was thinking more along the lines of next summer. that
would give us time to catch our breath a bit and some time to psych
ourselves up for an excuse to make another long drive to a fun new place.
It's funny how many have never been to Yellowstone. I'm embarrased to admit
that I didn't go until I moved here four years ago (and I went to jr.
college in this area for two years). I just thought it was so fun to get out
and explore Moab and all it's fun and doing this area might be too. If we
do, I will definitely get working on planting this summer.
Cadenza
