conserving photo space and still seeing all!

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-04-12 19:46:19 UTC-06:00
Okay, I have an idea to conserve space and add as many photos as we want so
that we can all share!!!
I have set up a new account with Yahoo under the username lbadventures. I
created a folder in there that can have public access. I can post as many
photos as I want with no limitation. I can only put up to 300 in a folder,
but I can keep making folders.
This means I could take photos from the site and add them all to the photos
page and post the link on the list. I think it will be a great idea and
provide no limitations on how many photos we share.
I can think of a couple of down sides though. For one, I would have to be
the one to post things on there, so you could put photos on the list (or
email them to me) and I can put them in the photos page, but others can't
add or take them away. If someone wants one in or out. I can do it. I am
happy to do this though, and if anyone has concerns about a certain photo, I
could remove it immediately. Another possible concern is that in order for
us to let anyone on the list see them, I would have to leave it open for
public access. Much of Yahoo is like this anyway already and I don't have a
problem with this since I don't think we would put anything in there that
could be used maliciously, but I understand that some might have a problem
with it. The only other options are totally private (which defeats the
point) or me inviting certain email addresses (which could take forever and
change constantly.)
Anyway, I think this might be a nice option. I've seen some do it already.
Ryan's old website had links to his yahoo photos page so other letterboxers
could look at his photos if they wanted to. I thought it worked well in that
situation.
If you want to see how it works, I already added a couple pictures into the
"links" section of the list. I can take it off easily, but I thought I'd
give you a couple of pictures to let you see how it works. You can still
download pictures, print them, order photos or gifts with those photos
without my permission. I think it will even let you have 10 free
professional prints. Of course there is no charge to save or print it
yourself. Let me know what you think. If I feel you all like it, I will
start transferring photos over!
Cadenza

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-04-13 09:02:25 UTC-06:00
Okay, I was completely ready to sign us up for a free account without even
asking the group. I love some of the features, especially the ability to tag
the photos in a way that you can run your cursor over the photo and have
something pop up telling you who that person is or putting a comment there.
As I dug a bit more though, I'm not sure that we would be happy with just
the free account. There are strong limits to how much can be uploaded--only
10 MB/month. This is about what we already have in the photos section of the
list. So we would only be able to upload that much and we would have to wait
another month to do any more. My other concern is that the free accounts
won't allow saving the original photo size, only a smaller one. So if we
want to print photos at home, we aren't going to get the same quality.
I just did some digging in Yahoo photos as well. It saves three sizes of
prints and you can get a slightly larger one, but it appears the full size
original is only available if you are purchasing prints (isn't that tricky
of them?) This is annoying. I want to access the full size from both sites,
but at least Yahoo saves the full size one. I wish there was a free site
that didn't have the space limitations! Anyway, I better get back to work.
Cadenza

Compare that to what you get with a Free Account:

- 3 photosets

- Storage of smaller (resized) images
