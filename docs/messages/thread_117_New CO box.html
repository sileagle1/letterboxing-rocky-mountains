> Littleton, Arapahoe County, CO
> Placed by: RavenWolf
> Box webpage located at:

> Clues:
> Placed by RavenWolf and The Samurai Stamper.
>
> Easy hike - about 1.5 miles both ways, no real elevation change to speak
of. It should take you between 35 and 45 minutes from start to finish
depending on how fast you walk. The path is dog friendly.
>
> >From I-70, or I-25 take C-470 to the SantaFe exit. Go North on Santa Fe
to Mineral (about 2 miles). Head West (Left) onto Mineral, then go right at
the 1st stop light. Go past the RTD parking to the 1st real road and turn
left. This should lead you to the South Platte Nature Center - Park here.
>
> If you have time, check out the nature center. It tells you about the
wildlife you might encounter, and about the general area you'll be walking
in.
>
> Go down to the paved path just behind the nature center. Head north along
the path. Walk for about 5-10 minutes and on your left hand side you should
see a fence with a small gap. In the gap there is a pole proclaiming that
only hikers and people with dogs are allowed to pass - no horses or
bicycles. Head through the fence, past the pole onto the dirt path.
>
> At the first fork, head left to the brown arrow. (Your bearing should be
around 330.) Follow along the path past the second arrow toward the river.
The path parallels the river for a bit, then curves to the right and then
back to the left. As the path curves to the left, you should come out of
some trees into a more open area. Take note of the large tree at about 60
degrees 50-60 paces up the trail.
>
> The right portion of this tree has been broken, probably as a result of
the large snowfall Colorado had this spring. On the broken portion of the
tree, there are a few steps left behind from a tree house that children once
occupied.
>
> Go under the broken 'archway' of the tree and set your compass to 110. You
should see the reminants of a dead tree. You're going to need to do a small
amount of bushwacking here. Head along bearing 110, 35 paces to the base of
the tree. Then turn to a bearing of 190 and head another 15 paces. In front
of you should be another dead tree that looks as though it has been bent in
half. The legend of the Alchemist's Lizard is hidden underneath.
>
> Please rehide well and take extra caution in retreiving and rehiding this
box. The platte river trail is frequented by MANY people especially in the
summer months.
>
> After you are done stamping in, you can head back the way you came, or you
can continue a little further up the path. Past a small bridge and up some
stairs is a fork in the road, head right (120 degrees). Continue down the
path to the next fork and head left. Head through the fence and over another
wooden bridge and this will put you back onto the paved path. This little
detour should only take you another 5 minutes or so. Head south along the
paved path to get back to your car, or continue north along the river if you
would like to walk a bit further.
