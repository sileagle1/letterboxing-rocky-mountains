From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2003-08-02 12:12:36 UTC-06:00
Unfortunately, I don't think so. Some of the abandoned boxes out here are
rather remote. They'd require a day long hike at altitude. I've been
reading over the clues, and other than one or two of them that are a few
hours away, I can't even imagine hiking for them. Perhaps I will look
again. There's one in Dillon that might be doable.

For those not on the big message board:
Anyone interested in adopting an abandoned letterbox should visit the LBNA
main website at www.letterboxing.org - click where it says "search for
boxes" and then search by "placer" where the placer is abandoned box.
If you can adopt a box, it just means you'd need check to see its status,
does it need a new ink pad or logbook? Then email Wes (the webmaster of the
main website) and let him know you'd like to adopt the box. We can get you
Wes' email address if you need it and can't locate it on the board.

Let's keep those boxes in CO active! :)
Autumn
