JeffCo: Another Approach?

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2004-09-01 15:30:28 UTC
I'm doing some thinking about this permit things and there seem to
be a lot of restrictions. We often carry around letterboxes waiting
to be placed in case we find a cool spot that speaks to us. Just as
with out-of-towners, the permit makes it difficult to make these
kind of placements. I'm wondering if there is another way.

What do people think about maybe setting up a meeting with the
JeffCo people to try to find an alternate solution? As an example,
perhaps we could get a sort of blanket permission as long as certain
criteria are met, such as, has to be on trail, can't be in an
endangered species habitat or environmentally sensitive area, can't
be in the rattlesnake pit, etc. If the JeffCo people could identify
off limit areas, perhaps they'd allow us to place anywhere else as
long as we sent them an email pinpointing the spot so they could
track overboxed areas or whatever. Another example of an alternate
solution, perhaps the JeffCo people would allow us to place them on
the fly and publish them, as long as the permit was filed within a
certain number of days afterward, and then if disapproved, we'd
promise to have someone go out an pull the box. I'm just thinking
out loud here. Do you think such a dialog with the JeffCo people
would be worthwhile?

JeLyBean

you a weekend to get your box back).

I think this may be a good thing to try and accomplish. I am in.
