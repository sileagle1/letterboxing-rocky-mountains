Denver 'boxing

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2006-04-01 00:19:48 UTC
I had a wonderful time earlier this week in the fair city of Denver.
Did you know I used to live there? Aurora actually, from 1979-1984. I
am surprised by the growth and increased traffic every time I go back!
And even having lived there for 5 years, every place I went
'box-hunting was somewhere I had never been - so THANKS for that!
First I met with Silent Wings' sister and nephew for a stamp exchange.
Very cool stamps!

Then I found:
Lunch Stop by Fork Finders - first finder for Fork Finder's first
hide! I got in and out before the prescribed burn. Talked to some nice
firemen.
The Gold Miner by TheCarvingClan who lived on THIS side of the Rockies.
Triceratops by Front Range Hiker - educational trail and I LOVED the
worm's eye view of the fossils. Good source for your stamp design.
Coors Beer Microbox by Silver Eagle - busy, busy spot. Like so many
locals, I enjoyed lunch here watching the geese.
Onion II by y-nought - I LOVED this 'box from clues to stamp to journal!
Stegosaurus by JelyBean - I have finally bagged a JelyBean 'box. Great
clues: short and sweet; and nice carving, too.
Buffalo Bill Cody by Esmerelda - I believe I found the right spot, but
Bill was under about 2 feet of snow. I probably could have dug him
out, but re-hiding would have been a problem and I'm sure I would have
torn the snow up and possibly attracted the uninitiated. So I'll have
to catch this one on a return trip.
Catnip by Front Range Hiker - I loved the journal and Genesee Park is
beautiful.
Fodor Glava by Aljan - extraordinary story and an extraordinary
letterbox. Does Aljan ever disappoint? I think not.
Eagle Eyes by preboxed - I enjoyed this pair and even though I didn't
see an eagle, the other critters in the air and on the ground were
very entertaining.

You all will be happy to know that the Jeffco Open Space folks have
checked on almost all the Jeffco boxes I found within the past couple
of months.

I have hidden a new box within Denver city limits and will have the
clues up soon.

Thanks to everyone for the great letterboxes. I barely made a dent and
look forward to my next trip to Denver to bag some more!

Boxer Lover
