From: Cattail (MLK) (mkoski@lamar.colostate.edu) | Date: 2004-07-27 23:10:05 UTC
Hi Debbi - well, let me tell you about the Rocky Mountain Wedding
box - there are three "Crosier Mountain" Trails (or access to that
trail) in the Glen Haven area. Not knowing this, my friend and I
went out a couple weeks ago to get the box, and we were apparently
on the WRONG trail!!! It was a very steep climb, and we had been
hiking for about 2 1/2 miles before we even got to a fork (which was
supposed to be a mile). After looking around and being very puzzled
by the lack of any landmarks mentioned in the clues, we gave up and
headed back down the trail. We ran into someone who informed us
that there are three trails, and we deduced we were on the wrong
one. So, I THINK the correct trail is the one that actually starts
in the town of Glen Haven (by the horse stables), instead of the one
about 2 miles east of the town (and there's another one even further
east). At least that's what it looked like on the topo map. Lesson
learned: these are *clues*, not so much directions - always check
the topo map!!! We'll be trying again in the next couple weeks.

As for kids, it might be a rough hike. It was GORGEOUS up there -
great views, wildflowers and rocks, but it was a little steep (hey,
it's a mountain after all). If you go, bring lots of water!

By the way, I'll be planting a box really soon at Horsetooth
Mountain Park in Fort Collins - I've got the stamp carved and I just
need to get the box together.

Cheers,
Cattail :)
