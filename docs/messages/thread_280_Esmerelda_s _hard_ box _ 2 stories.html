From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-07-29 16:56:15 UTC-06:00
The box I think we can't do is:
Zebediah Gump - Doc Holiday. At the bottom when you talk about the
terrain and such (PS - I LOVE that you do this!!!!), it says:

Esmerelda says: This trail isn't suitable for wheelchairs and you'll
need some strong legs and healthy lungs to get a stroller up there. It's
not a long hike (about 45 minutes round trip), but quite steep and the
trail is fairly rocky in spots. It is, however, dog friendly, and
there's a wonderful view from the top.

My kids can't do steep and rocky... the little one tends to lose his
footing.

Speaking of losing his footing, I have to tell you all about this... I
went out to Deer Creek Canyon Park today to plant a stamp (no, the clues
aren't posted yet, and won't be till at least Friday afternoon). The
trail we took was downhill and NARROW. Ok, it narrow for a 2 & 3 year
old. You might have been able to get 2 hikers side by side, but it was
much more confortable to go single file. Anyway, I was determined to
place this stamp (Jessica - 13 - carved this stamp back in May!). I
tell you, there were a few times I was sure Matthew or Maggie were going
to go tumbling down the cliff. Thankfully, they didn't and actually, in
all reality, they did VERY well. But, I think it is one of the "hardest
trails" we have taken them on.

Oh, oh, and then we were off to another place to plant a stamp... some
of the trails follow a river... WELL, while I am planting the stamp, I
turn around to see Maggie and Matthew staning extremely close to the
edge of the cliff looking down at the water!

Now, keep in mind, I am very paranoid, so they probably were a good 2
feet from the edge, but it was too close for me!

Needless to say, I am not so sure we will be picking places like that to
plant stamps when it is just the 3 of us again.

Debbi
