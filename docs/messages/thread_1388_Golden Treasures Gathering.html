Golden Treasures Gathering

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2007-08-27 07:19:53 UTC-06:00
Thanks to Allemeyn, Front Range Hiker and Preboxed for putting
yesterday's gathering together. They went all out and did a fabulous
job! We greeted old and new friends, found boxes, exchanged stamps, did
a poker run (we collected cards from select letterboxes), had a treasure
box pinata for young and old alike that when finally broken spewed out
lots of cool stuff ... little handmade logbooks and stamp pads and gold
chocolate coins, etc. Oh, and we ate pie. By the way, Tai Chi makes a
killer pecan pie ... mmm! Es, I raised a fork towards the sky in your
honor ... just in case you were flying by.

There was an absolutely insane epidemic of cooties. They were turning up
all over the place. For a while, I was feeling like I'd contracted
measles, shingles, and chicken pox all at the same time.

I will hopefully post a few photos later today. I'll admit now that I
didn't take too many photographs, because I was pretty much immersed in
ink most of the day with cooties, exchanges, personal travelers, regular
boxes, surprise boxes, event stamps, hitchhikers from the visiting Big
Bison Hitchhiker Hostel. Whew!

Thanks again to the organizers for all their hard work and for providing
such a fun and memorable time for us!

~Silent Wings and the rest of the Catamount Carvers

meeting Tai Chi and Chai Tea. We had followed them to so many boxes
around the Front Range. They are the sweetest people!

her first stamp-carving. She will soon venture out with her own
letterboxing identity/trail name; this is destined to be a lifelong
addiction (I mean, hobby).

the 3 little kids (with big sister's help) enjoyed making their own
logbooks with the supplies donated by the organizers. These logbooks
are already being used and are officially finding a home in my LB
backpack. Thanks again for encouraging this activity!

Looking forward to the next event :-) We plan to enjoy our CO
letterboxing days to the fullest until the Air Force decides to move
us on.
