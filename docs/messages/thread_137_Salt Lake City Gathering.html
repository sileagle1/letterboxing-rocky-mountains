From: (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2003-12-21 17:57:24 UTC-07:00

I am forwarding this email in case it entices any of you even more to make
it out to our gathering in Salt Lake City. We have had a slight change in
schedule due to an airline scheduling conflict. I will send the info in
case any are able to make it after all. We would love to have any who can
make it. This should be a lot of fun-a chance to meet other letterboxers,
and a chance to plant/find a slew of new letterboxes. If you know of any
others who might be interested, please let us (and them) know. I know there
are some letterboxers in Utah that go out boxing, but don't really plant
boxes or participate on the list. We'd love to have any and all who can
make it.

It looks as if we will be meeting on January 3rd now. This should be a good
day to get out and enjoy some letterboxing. Butterfly and I haven't decided
on an exact time yet. We are still feeling things out a bit with her
family. If you have strong feelings about what time we should meet, let me
know.

As for locations, we were thinking about meeting in Memory Grove (in
downtown SLC) since there are two letterboxes hidden there already (A Walk
in the Park Series). We can meet there and decide where we want to go from
there. Some of that will depend on the weather and what we want to plant
after that. I imagine that we might stop somewhere for some lunch along the
way. That might be more warm than a picnic style thing. If you don't know
how to find Memory Grove, I can give you more specific directions or you can
find them in the clue to the letterbox series. If the weather gets bad, we
can skip over to the state capitol or a restauraunt to take shelter.

Let me know if you can make it. I will be emailing later as we know more
about what we are doing.

Cadenza

Hi U2
Things are moving along here in Ct and you can not begin to believe how
excited my letterboxing friends are that they have this opportunity to
share our joy with others. I have a shopping bag full of boxes to
bring. They have nice greetings to you all also. There are several that
will be easy to take out of Utah and plant , but most have the Utah
theme and so will really have to be planted somewhere there. One is a
Rocky Mt. Stag , so he can go elsewhere. I have two Event Stamps, one a
snowman carved by Leader of the Pack . She also carved a Sea Gull
flying from Ct to Utah. ~ Chuck of Chuck and Molly Fame, carved the
Rocky Mt Stag and The Utah State Animal , Our fun loving Needeeps is
sending two also, one a Connecticut or Bust , cute, and a Map Forever
United -really nice-- Lobsta lady [our Maine gal.] did a great history
lesson on Ephraim and nice stamp also a H.H. from her a Boomerang ,
Ct.>> Ut.>> . Bluebird designed The Bridge connecting Ct. and Ut.
then our Slo Camel [a teacher] did a great job on a Bee Hive with a
lesson about that too. Steve and Heidi and Maddras [a dog] carved
CT. state map with a Butterfly in the middle !! and Music Woman send a
lovely carving of a Ct. Light House. So, those are the boxes and
there are also some H.H. Do you think we will be busy ?? And I only
have one day. Of course I am going to ask the children to drive me
around to sight see --with a few boxes in my backpack--just in case. :-D
Now,, I need to know how I will be able to get in touch with you . Also
I will find out the address of the house where I will be staying in
American Fork.
Can't wait.
Butterfly in CT.
