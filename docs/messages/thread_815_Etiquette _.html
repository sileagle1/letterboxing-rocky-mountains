From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-08 03:57:28 UTC
It's just my opinion, but I think it's fine to ask for this kind of
information. Most of the time it is common knowledge, and I don't
think anyone wants to lure someone to a box that isn't their cup of
tea.

But I'm sure you agree that this info should be kept in private
discussions. I would hate to see any info posted here that would
discourage people from visiting a box based on the originality of the
stamp. (Or anything else, for that matter).

y-nought, whose stamps are all hand-carved, for better or for worse.

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-08-09 12:37:56 UTC-06:00
Isn't it funny how that can be such a sensitive issue? Pretty much all of
the ones I have found in UT and ID are hand carved. There was one in SLC
that wasn't, but it was taken out in a snowstorm that took down half the
trees a couple years ago. It has since been replaced I heard. I haven't
found all of them in MT but the ones I have found were carved. In WY, most
are as well, except for a bunch of ones around Cody/Wapiti, but those were
done as a part of a school project and it was so fun for me to picture these
school kids from this tiny school getting into teaching others about their
town through letterboxing that I just loved those boxes still. My trip to
Cody was the only time I wasn't fairly disappointed to find a store bought
stamp. I haven't had the feeling it would be too sensitive with this group
to ask about the stamps, but I think some on the main list would not like
it. I think it would depend entirely on how you phrased the question.
Have fun!
Cadenza
