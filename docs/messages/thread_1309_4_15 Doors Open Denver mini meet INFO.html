4/15 Doors Open Denver mini meet INFO

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2007-04-06 19:11:23 UTC
Ok everyone, as promised, here's the info for our mini DOD informal
gathering and urban exploring opportunity on Sunday, April 15th

Timing is everything! TODAY April 6th in BOTH the Denver Post
AND Rocky Mountain News you'll find a copy of the pull out guide for
Doors Open Denver. Run out and get your copy NOW!

This will give you a chance to read up and plan which buildings you'd
like to explore/work in with nearby letterboxes/and so on. Then,
after everyone arrives, we do some exchanges, chat, etc we can
(hopefully) arrange ourselves into groups with people who want to see
similar DOD places/parts of town/'boxes.

I'll try and stop by and get extra copies of the guide this week to
hand out on the 15th, but please do try and get your own copy TODAY!

Most DOD locations are only open until about 4 pm, so in order to
allow plenty of time to do exchanges, letterbox, and still have most
of the afternoon for DOD exploring, here's the plan:

We'll meet:
Sunday, April 15th - 10:30 am
Indoor meeting place. You never know about the weather!
Common Grounds Coffeehouse
1601 17th St, Denver, 80202
303) 296-9248
Some more info: http://tinyurl.com/22h6oy

I'll be the blonde (female) wearing the explorer's hat.

I'll try to get there about 10 a.m. and attempt to stake out a table
large enough for our group. Based on posts here and previous emails
I'm thinking we may have 10-15 people and would like to confirm
this...sooooo

CAN WE GET A HEAD COUNT? Please post here not later than 8 pm FRIDAY,
April 13th with the following info:

1)Yes, we're still planning on coming!

2)X number of people will be in our party and our trail names
are ??????

Can anyone say: Personal Travelers? I of course will be
there!

Our meeting location puts us only a block or so from Union Station,
DOD HQ this year, where more info and volunteers will be available to
answer questions, and it's also only a few blocks from the FREE 16th
Mall Shuttle which will make getting around downtown proper a bit
easier for the day.

If anyone is into urban bicycling, the 16th Street Mall allows bikes
on Sundays only and this would be a great way to speed up the
distances between DOD and letterbox locations without dealing with
cars.

Speaking of cars, since I just know you out-of-towners are going to
ask:

What about parking?

Street parking downtown "should" be OK-ish on a Sunday morning and
for the most part free. I will note that parking WEST of Union
Station (say near my, Denver Skatepark and D.O.D. boxes, plus quite a
few other boxes that could be walkable along the Platte River) is
restricted to TWO hours only, even on Sundays! Check the signage in
this area and maybe make sure you have a supply of coins for the day!

Another alternative is to park near the Central Library and Denver
Art Museum (another bunch of boxes in this area) If you can't find
street parking, garage rates are reasonable by downtown standards
here:
http://www.denverartmuseum.org/visit_us/plan_your_visit/parking

This would also put you in the area of quite a few DOD sites up on
Capitol Hill, and you could then take the 16th Street Mall Shuttle to
the meeting place at the caf.

Think that's it other than to say, don't be at all surprised if
some more letterbox clues make their appearances prior to our DOD
event. I for one, will also commit to having such clues up no later
than 8 p.m. on Friday the 13th, so remember to look before you head
out to Denver.

Looking forward to meeting you all for some Denver exploring!

preboxed
Denver

Oh yeah, and for anyone who missed my initial posting a month or so
back and is now saying to themselves:

"What the heck is DOD?"

Doors Open Denver is a FREE two-day event that allows you to go behind
the scenes at nearly 80 of the city's significant architectural gems
and lesser known treasures. Spend the day on a self-guided tour of
both historic and modern buildings, take an expert-led tour or embark
on an Urban Adventure, a self-guided tour based on a theme.

Official website:

http://www.denvergov.org/Default.aspx?
alias=www.denvergov.org/doorsopendenver
