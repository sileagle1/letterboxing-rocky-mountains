My kids are really getting into this

From: Kristina Sandoval (krislsandoval@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-08 15:38:06 UTC-07:00
My 8 year old son made a hitchhiker for kids. He made the log book from card stock and taped the pages together, then made a stamp. he wrote Hitchhiker for kids on the front and insode he wrote .. this hitchhiker was created by an 8 year old letterboxer from Co Springs. He put his email address and asked that the finders email the locations they find it.

We will go out and plant it on our next journey!

Mow my 15 Yo daughter and I keep discussing possible box sites here in the Springs. There are a ton of places, we just have to pick and then figure out what to do from there.

So my question to all of you experienced pie eater and box owners... do you hide the box first then figure out the clues or do you start the clue hunt first and then find somewhere to hide it.
In other words how do you get started??

Kris
Newly obsessed!

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-07-09 04:20:51 UTC
I have a general idea where it's going.. like Buffalo Bill near his
grave...but don't write any clues until the box is in it's new
home. Because I never know exactly where it's going to be until I
place it! Then I write the clues backwards.. from the end spot to
the starting point. If that makes sense.

Es, who sometimes is sense challenged

Re: [RMLetterboxing] Re: My kids are really getting into this

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-08 23:09:11 UTC-07:00
I do exactly as Es said. If I write it backwards I don't have to guess where
I will end up and then I just put it in the right order as I write it. I
usually start with a general idea of location though like a trail or a
park--well sometimes even a city and then I go from there when I find a spot
that works. If I am determined to hide in a predetermined spot, I may hide a
box in a riskier location b/c I didn't consider all my options as I should.
Cadenza
