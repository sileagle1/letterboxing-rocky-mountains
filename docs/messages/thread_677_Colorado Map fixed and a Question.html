Re: Colorado Map fixed and a Question

From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2005-05-15 02:01:39 UTC
When the LbNA map for Colorado was first sectioned out - I worked
with the guy at the time who was managing it. We only had a whopping
20 or so boxes in the entire state, so divisions of more detail just
seemed to make things more complicated. *laughs*

Since letterboxing has grown so much in Colorado (YAY!) I think it
totally makes sense to re-divide the map into something that makes a
bit more sense. What each section is CALLED is rather irrelevant,
(to me) since people looking at the map will have a general idea
where they're headed.

If you LIVE in Colorado - the terms "Front Range, High Country, and
Western Slope" make total sense. However, if you're travelling into
the state, it might not make sense at all.

My suggestion would be to go with the division that was mentioned
below. Western Slope, SW Colorado, High Country, Front Range, and
Eastern Plains.

Those from within the state will understand the areas, and those
coming to visit us will be looking at a map anyway. But this type of
division is much clearer and will make planning boxing trips ever so
much easier.

Just my two cents.
Also -- Ed (y-nought)-- will you contact me offlist when you get this
message? *smooch*

Autumn

and damned proud of it!
