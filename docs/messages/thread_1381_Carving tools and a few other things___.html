From: claudey (erin@erinterry.com) | Date: 2007-08-05 03:21:56 UTC
Before I get to my question, I just wanted to also add my sorrow about
hearing about the careless treatment that the Pirate King has gotten!
It was such a beautiful box with all its lovely contents. I truly
hated to hear that it was damaged. At least most of the contents are
intact, but I agree that the journal is the most important part! I
hope that the rest of the series is ok, as I have only completed the
first 2 boxes! A pox on those who were so careless!!

I also hate to hear about others' journals being mistreated, and I
wholeheartedly agree that great care should be taken when stamping
in!! I personally never let my kids mess with journals for fear that
they would even just mess up our stamp in it! :)

So now on to my question... I was wondering what everyone's carving
tool(s) of choice is/are. I am looking for something for fine lines in
particular. I had bought a Speedball set awhile back that came with a
handle and 5 blades, but I find that the #1 is still too big, and is
not nearly sharp enough! It seems to tear rather than cut. I find
myself going back to my faithful Xacto knife for all cutting/carving.
But, I still would like to find something better for those detail
areas. The knife cuts too deep sometimes and can weaken my lines. I
have read about using a hollow needle, like vets use on horses & such,
but I don't know where to get one. Any ideas from anyone?

Thanks-
claudey

PS-I finished my carving of Gandalf the Grey last night!! Now I just
need to make a journal & he'll be on his way! :)

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-08-05 12:16:12 UTC
I use the gouge with the red handle. Whichever one that is!

I took a few of the number 1 nibs, put them on the stove until they
were uber hot, then squished them with pliers to make them smaller.
I also use a diamond sharpening stone which keeps them reeeeally
sharp. They go through the Mastercarve like buttah. The stone is
way easy to use... got it at a sporting goods store for a few
dollars.
