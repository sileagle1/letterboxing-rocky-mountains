Yo Ho Yo Ho a Boxing We Did Go

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-17 01:33:48 UTC
Skeetch and Es hit the high seas today and returned with some new
stamps and some new clues.
It was a gorgeous, perfecto, pirate lovin' day in Western Colorado!
We stopped at Silt for PRE's Ruler of the Schoolyard. I played with
the box while Skeetch metal detected across the street (he found
three cents!). Then on up to Glenwood Canyon to visit PRE's No Name
and check on Grizzly Creek. So fun to read the entries! Backtrack
and head up to Marble.
We passed Redstone and stopped for Fairy Moon by Ravenwolf. I
clambered up the slope with my best pirate clambering skills..
immediately spied the box, snagged it and sat down to proceed. As I
opened it up, my first thought was surprise that Ravenwolf had used a
lined wire notebook....instantly after which I thought, 'what's all
this JUNK doing in here?!' and as my light bulb flickered on I
hollered to Skeetch, 'This is a GEOCACHE!'. Well! How RUDE! I
flipped through the book, but didn't leave a note.. no sense in them
knowing the letterbox is nearby! But.. how could they miss.. because
it's 18 inches away!!! Oh my heck! And Fairy Moon was totally
uncovered.. just tossed into it's hole. I figure the geocachers have
seen it, grabbed it, read the 'letterbox' title on the outside and
just tossed it back into it's hole. One of the cachers had stamped
in. I LOVE your journal, Ravenwolf! Beautifimous! And I rehid it
reeeeally well. No way is a cacher going to see it before they see
the cache. The cache is way obvious. I also wrote on the outside of
the bag, 'This is not a geocache!'.
Then on down the road to Marble where Zebediah Gump: The Crystal
found a new home. We hadn't been up there since we originally
planted it which was just a month short of two years ago. Oh my
HECK.. the marble operation is in full swing and the parking area is
stacked way high with 37,000 pound blocks of marble! What a sight
that was! We were looking for a spot and spied a Marmot sunning on a
rock.. I grabbed Skeetch and said we were going the other way!! NO
marmots!
Then a lovely drive home and.. life.. she is good!

Es, adding three finds to her journal and singing a happy
letterboxing tune

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2006-04-17 04:00:49 UTC
And.... a boxing we did go today also!!!!! Kenna finally came home
yesterday from the coast and was able to go and play this afternoon,
yay! We first went out and hiked up to PRE's "BMAO in Moab" and had
a wonderful time stamping in. The trail was very busy because of all
the jeep safari visitors this weekend. The directions were very fun
to follow and went past a huge brilliant red patch of flowering
cactus, gorgeous! People were looking at us funny as we hiked past
them on the way up, and then hiked back by them while following the
directions back down the hill. Wonderful spot and box.

Then we went back to town for drinks and picked up Aspen for the
trek north to "Thompson Canyon" and beyond to the clever "Just Say
Go" boxes. It was super busy up those canyons and people were
camping nearby the Just Say Go box. We chatted with them for a while
and found out they were from Es's neck of the woods. While we were
up the trail stamping in, they hid some eggs for Aspen and we had a
fun egg hunt when we came back down. They were the nicest people!

All in all, a fantastic day to be out and about! Awww, sigh......

Kat

From: Kenna (kennamtn@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-17 14:14:36 UTC
Yes, PRE, I have to say THANKS, THANKS THANKS!!! While in CA, I had
a fistful of clues, and all the excitement in the world to find new
boxes (especially ones that Kat doesn't have!! He, he!). I ventured
out, dragged my parents along, and found exactly. . . NONE! That's
right! Not ONE box was there. I should've had at least 7 new
stamps to come home with, and came home with zero. I was so sad.
It's not that I didn't try. I went to every spot, looked under
every rock, bush, tree, people's feet, I could find. I came home
and told Kat my sad story, and she cheered me up when she told me
PRE had left 3. . .count 'em, THREE. . .new boxes out there within
arm's reach! YAY!
We had to rush out and find them. (Good thing the FF's were still
there. With Jeep Safari in town, Kat was worried other LBers came
and got them before we did. She was kind enough to wait until I got
home, but told me that if the FF's were gone, she's going without me
next time! Yikes!)
So, I'm glad you had such a great trip, and I'm glad you were able
to plant some. Sounds like we're going to have to make a trip out
to CO sometime soon, though. There's a treasure trove of boxes just
waiting for us!
Kennamtn
