Colorado Visit Update...

From: blackvelvetrav (blackvelvetrav@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-04-01 01:07:41 UTC
Tales from Coloradoas many of the great Colorado boxers know my
family and I are visiting from Idaho this week. And are heading back
to Idaho tomorrow.

Even though I am traveling with half a carload of non-boxers we did
make an attempt on my birthday to get out and box a little. We had
really bad luck attempting to locate Buffalo Bill as the weather up
on the hill changed so drastically on our way up that by the time we
reached the parking lot the winds were at least 40 MPH and it was
snowing. Down below it was beautiful and sunny not even 10 minutes
before the sever weather set in. After only 5 minutes out of the car
my non-boxing husband gave me the "you must be joking" look and I
must confess my ears were already hurting and we had to leave Buffalo
Bill up on the hill without a find in my book. As a conciliation he
was willing to stop by the Coor's box at the park on the way back to
my sisters house. The winds were still awful but at least I found
one box on our trip.

Our luck during our Colorado visit turned even darker on Wednesday.
Lizman (my son Alex) and I were able to plant 4 new boxes in Summit
County called "March of the Penguins." I am on a computer tonight
that is dial up but if I can manage to not go crazy with the slow
pace I hope to get the new boxes posted. If not tonight I should
have them up and ready to be found on Monday after returning to
Boise. The turn to the dark side came later that afternoon. We went
dog sledding while our non-boxing counterparts went up to Keystone to
ski for the day. We had originally skied at Copper Mountain on
Monday and Tuesday. Anyway we received a call on our cell phone
after finishing up with the dogs and low and behold my husband had a
bad fall in the Terrain Park at Keystone and was taken to the
hospital. He now has 8 broken ribs and his left collarbone is broken
in three places. He just spent the last two nights at the New Saint
Anthony's Summit Medical Center. You can imagine the 12-hour car
ride home will not be a comfortable one for him.

Just an update from us Idaho bound folks. Colorado is definitely
wild and wolly country!

Blackvelvetrav
