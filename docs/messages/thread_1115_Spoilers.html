Hello all-

I would just like to say that I am against posting photographs of
someone else's letterbox location, logbook, or stamp on the internet
without permission.

Trip reports, with photos of the outing, can be done with discretion
and are a lot of fun to read. But any images that give away locations
or show stamp images can basically ruin the letterboxing experience for
the finder.

If I see any image or description on the internet of one of my
letterboxes I'll retrieve them. And if I see stamp images of other
letterboxes, that is "Game Over" for me and I will search elsewhere for
an adventure that I don't already know the ending to.

y-nought, not meaning to sound grouchy, but spoiling MY fun is a very
serious matter......

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-06-11 20:49:15 UTC
For myself, I'm not really talking about anything specific.I have
just noticed a trend where stamp images are showing up on personal
letterboxing websites along with some pretty detailed descriptions of
the hiding place, which spoils the game for many seekers.

It has become such a problem in some areas that many of the
letterboxers no longer publish clues on any of the websites and you
have to "know" someone to get clues.

I would hate to see that happen here. I think that one of the great
things about letterboxing is that most clues are accessible to anyone.

But in the end all I can do is state my opinion, which I did, hoping
to start a discussion in case there are some folks in this group who
are considering this.

It's just my opinion, though, and the future of letterboxing may
feature more published stamp images and less published clues.

y-nought
