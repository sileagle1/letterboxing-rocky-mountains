What are your thoughts???

From: Scott (scott_katytalbot@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-17 19:42:04 UTC
I was out boxing today and attempted to find the "Butterfly Trail"
box in Niwot that was noted as missing a while back with a note that
it would be replaced in a few days. This was to no avail as the
stash was still missing. This made me think of something that I
wanted to run by everyone in this group. What if we were to set up
a list of boxes that were currently missing and was to keep it
current so that we had one place to look before hitting the trail?
I would certainly volunteer to maintain the list if everyone thinks
it would be a good idea. I know that some placers don't like others
to post the status of their boxes, however I know that this is a
good, honest group of folks here (pirates and all). If there is
some contention to this maybe we can stipulate that the owner has to
authorize the posting? Please let me know your thoughts and I will
start it up if it's a go.

In other news...boxing last weekend in Wyoming was great! We found
all of the boxes in the Cheyenne and Larimie areas minus CW
Sunseeker's missing Cowboy. We did find one box that now holds a
little extra surprise that is perfect for a summer picnic. Can you
guess what it is?

Another recent trip led us to the honeywine plunder. No Y-nought, I
was not the first finder and therefore those dreaded pirates in
Grand Lake plundered the stash before I could get there. I think
there may be an inside conspiracy since Catamount Carvers were in
Grand Lake to get all of the loot up there near the same time.
Seeing this empty chest, I felt bad and left my breakfast inside for
the next finder. It was sent all the way from the Lock Wench out
east so I hope it doesn't go bad before someone else finds it.

Ramdelt

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-07-18 09:01:00 UTC-06:00
Personally, I have strong objections to it. I think the intent is really
good, but I can't keep track of the amount of times I have been told that a
box of mine was missing only to find that the seeker was looking in the
wrong place. In fact this happened to me just this week. I heard about a
year ago that a box of mine that is difficult to get out to was missing but
I haven't been able to get out there to check. I waited to give status
reports b/c of a couple of things that she said that left me curious as to
her accuracy. This week AZ Roadie was traveling through from AZ and reported
finding the box exactly where I described it to be. If someone had somewhere
to report a box missing, others would have been disuaded from looking for a
good letterbox.
This is a topic that has been debated until people are blue in the face on
the main list, and I feel it is for good reasons. I just don't trust the
accuracy of reports that don't come from the planter and I trust them to
update clues if something is missing. If they don't, I just take that as one
of the risks of letterboxing. If you do something like this list, you should
probably not attach it to the group. It would be subject to angry trollers
like the attempted database on the main list that died in huge glory as
angry letterboxers destroyed it. I personally wouldn't do anything malicious
like many of them did, but I'm not happy when my boxes are discouraged by
someone that may not have been looking in the right place in the first
place. I know other letterboxers that are grossly offended at attempts to
have their letterboxes listed in databases.
I hope this doesn't come out too strong sounding or offensive. I like and
respect you as a letterboxer. I just have had strong feelings for years
about databases. I feel the harm they can do outweighs the good they can do
and the attempts to have them by others have had really bad results. Plus,
even if a box is missing, I might replace it, not knowing it was listed on a
database as missing, and then others wouldn't go to find it b/c it was
"missing." Does that make sense? I just worry about all the results of a
database (or list, or whatever it may be called.)
Cadenza

Re: [RMLetterboxing] What are your thoughts???

From: (scott_katytalbot@comcast.net) | Date: 2005-07-18 16:46:50 UTC
Unfortunately I had a nice reply for this typed up and my computer crashed so I'll try to get all of the information down again.

Cadenza, I understand where you are coming from. Did you happen to see the part I had written about the placer being required to authorize the posting? I think that letterboxing in Idaho and Colorado are slightly different in the number of boxes and the number of letterboxers as well. If I had a box posted as missing, it would give me incentive to check it, replace it or retire it sooner. If I had driven hundreds of miles and hiked in to find a box that was missing and later found that the placer knew it was missing, I'd be upset and would think twice about searching for another of their boxes in risk of the same result. What about those boxes that were on websites that are no longer active yet the boxes are still out there (Eogan's Stashes)? What if a letterboxer goes unheard from and their boxes never are maintained? How many times do we hear from someone that wants to travel to an area and is wondering if the boxes are still active? I personally would want to be conscientious to my letterboxing friends efforts and time. I guess one of the main reasons for my thought was the recent list of placers that came out on this talk group and said that their boxes were missing with or without a replacement plan. If a placer is willing to come out and say that their box is missing, wouldn't a list be a good thing? Then when it's replaced it would be removed? This issue really isn't that big of a deal to me, I just think we need to be more open minded and think more about others time and efforts. Whether this is the solution, I'm not sure, but communication and new ideas are what will make Letterboxing better for everyone.

Ramdelt
