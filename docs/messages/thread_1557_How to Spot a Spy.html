How to Spot a Spy

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2008-07-15 05:11:29 UTC
Ya know, spies are a lot like letterboxers. They live in a secretive
world. They have false identities. They deal with codes, ciphers and
methods of concealment. They are seeking a kind of treasure. They are
trying to learn something most people don't know. So, how could you
tell if someone you know is only a letterboxer, or a real KGB spy?

I have come up with a patented failsafe JeLyBean method for spotting a
spy. Here's how it works. You walk up to someone who is seemingly
only a letterboxer and say some improbable phrase. I recommend, "It is
lovely this time of year in Minsk." If the person looks at you like
your medication has worn off, they are probably just a plain
letterboxer. But, if they reply with an equally improbable phrase and
hand you a strange object, they are probably a KGB spy. Brilliant, huh?

JeLyBean, just trying to do my part to keep the world safe for
democracy.

PS, Just in case you are wondering, I am NOT a KGB spy.
