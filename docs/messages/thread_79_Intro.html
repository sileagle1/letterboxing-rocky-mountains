From: Lisa Cook (lisadcook@comcast.net) | Date: 2003-09-01 19:26:49 UTC-06:00
Hi Debbi,
Welcome. I am new to this also (started less than 2 weeks ago.) Ive only done a few so far. The 2 in Chatfield were my first and are good for kids....1 year old if in backpack/no strollers. But the walk is flat and not too long (I think it says less than a mile for both boxes).
They are Beltane and Ostara.
The Fountain Valley one in Roxborough is not as flat but not too far if you go there and back (I dont know about the whole loop as I didnt take it). There is some uphill but not too much/no strollers either.

Also both will be close to youin Littleton.
I am in Highlands Ranch.

Lisa aka TDG

Hi all! I wanted to introduce myself to the group... I've been lurking
on the group for a few months now (mostly out of laziness) and feel

on the Front Range. I have "met" a few of you thru email already. Been

Happy boxing!

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2006-11-07 22:54:37 UTC
You have it all wrong, PRE - there's no clique with the Big Brain
Box. There are 10 (or maybe 9) mini CDs floating around out there
in other people's letterboxes, kind of like a HH or Cuckoo Clues.
If you happen to find one, you find one. If not - it's probably
just as well, given the grief those disks seem to have brought to
the finders so far. Once people find them, they can do what they
want with them, put them back into another box, give them to a
friend, smash them with a hammer, throw them into an incinerator,
etc.

Now, I will admit to a little preferential treatment... that being
my preference for myself. :o) I do sometimes do favors for people
who do favors for me. I think I have been pretty open about that.
I will always trade extra hints, hitchhikers, unpublished clues,
etc. for those who will do the same for me. As Matron Mama Morton
says, when you're good to JeLy, JeLy's good to you.

JeLyBean
