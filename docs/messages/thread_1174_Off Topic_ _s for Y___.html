From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-09-08 20:21:20 UTC
Aljan-

Well, I'm a total cheapskate, so I haven't ever stayed in a hotel in
Telluride. I do know that they go from pricey to outrageous during
any of the festivals. But I think the last one is the Blues and
Brews Festival next weekend and the prices go down after that.

As far as camping, my favorite is the Matterhorn Campground up
towards Lizard Head Pass...very picturesque. But the Ilium Creek
Campground is closer to town. Both of these are Forest Service
Campgrounds without showers. I've heard that there is a campground
in town by the park with showers, but you might as well be in a
hotel for the fee they charge.

This person might also consider staying at the Mountain Village.
There is a free Gondola that runs between downtown and Mountain
Village and is kinda fun (even if you aren't staying up there).

There is only one letterbox I know of: Fall and Falls in Telluride
by Kat. This is a great box after a nice hike to a special place
with awesome views....highly recommended. Lots of other good hiking
trails start basically in town. There is a visitor's center on the
right as you enter town that has brochures about hikes and other fun
stuff. In a couple of weeks the Aspen might even be changing.

Es or Kat or someone else might have a better idea of the hotels or
places to go.

Would I know this mysterious traveler?
