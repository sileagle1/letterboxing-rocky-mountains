From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-05-04 02:07:25 UTC
I posted a few pictures in the photo section.....

Linus is an Australian Code-Cracking Dog, also known as a Blue
Stamper.

We found two letterboxes at Indian Creek (Great stamps and hidden in
the coolest spots!) And then we headed into the National Park.....

It has been 24 years since I've had a dog and I haven't really
noticed all the rules and regulations until now. But I found out that
dogs aren't allowed on trails even if they are leashed (Duh!). So we
headed over to a campsite over by South Six Shooter to do a short
impression of Curly Stooge and Marcel Marceau setting up a tent in
the wind.

After we had our fill of that comedy we decided that Moab would be
more fun..........and it was! Linus proved to be a mellow tent-mate
and the canyons blocked the wind. We didn't have any Moab clues with
us, so I decided to show La Paloma a few letterboxes on a dog-
friendly trail. Then we explored quite a ways beyond the letterboxes
and kept saying, "can you believe this? I never would have known this
existed if it weren't for Letterboxing!"

Woof!
