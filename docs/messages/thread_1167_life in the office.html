I know the feeling, Pre. Somehow knowing that I am leaving my job has made
things that were just mildly difficult feel intolerable. I'm lucky that I
do have a light at the end of the tunnel, but when I think about that I get
overwhelmed too. There is so much I need to do between now and October!!
The wedding thing is tough enough, but then you add in another reception in
another country, immigration and visa hassles, getting licensure transferred
to another country, and packing up everything and trying to move into a
one-bedroom flat, which means I need to sell or donate nearly everything I
own, well, I'm feeling the stress!

Sorry to go on an off-topic rant, but I'm feeling a bit frazzled too. I'd
like to say that I'm doing some letterboxing to get away from it all, but I
haven't had a moment. I haven't even looked for a box this year. I want
to, but haven't found the time. I'm hoping that things will calm down a bit
so I can. I know I need to at least get a few done because I want to do the
Dr. Who ones for Andrew when he comes out here. I know he won't respond to
Dartmoor boxing. I think it's more fun to box in the US to tell you the
truth, and he doesn't really like the moors. He definitely doesn't like the
rain that hits the UK at any given time, and storms blow up unexpectedly at
Darmoor on a regular basis. I figure if I can hook him here on a beautiful
mountain trail with a clever clue, he might not fight as hard when I want to
head down to England.

I also need to clean up a few boxes. I have boxes that have done well over
the years, but suddenly I'm getting emails from all over the place that my
boxes are missing. I have one in Wyoming that is only the second box I ever
planted. It had done well in it's little spot for three years, a geocache
got planted near it recently and suddenly the box has been moved three times
and the stamp went missing. Luckily Daelphinus (sp?) of dvn2r ckr was nice
enough to do a quick recarve and move it back to where it was supposed to
be. Now, I just know someone will lambast me for blaming geocaching, but it
seems rather funny that it didn't move for three years, but the moment a
geocache came along, it has gone all over the place! Anyway, I want to redo
a few key letterboxes in the area. Some will just have to be retired, but
there is only one active letterboxer in this area besides me who plants
boxes, and she isn't super active. I have a few that are key ones for
helping connect people to the process (decent carvings, easy finds, in nice
family friendly locations) that I want to replant. We'll see if I can
manage to find the time, but I'm not holding my breath.

Ah, the joy! Okay, I've avoided work long enough. I better get back on
task. Y'all have a great day, okay! Until later.
