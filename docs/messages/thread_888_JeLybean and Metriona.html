Re: JeLybean and Metriona

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2005-09-28 00:23:44 UTC
You've got questions, I've got answers. Sort of.

First, if I told you what I do for a living it might reveal my
superhero secret identity, and the world would suffer as a result.
Suffice to say, I do many things these days, but my college degree
is in rocket science. No kidding.

And come on... aren't you being a little unfair labeling my clues
intimidating? I consider myself an equal opportunity clue-giver;
some may be harder than others, but there's fun for all brains!
Some of my favorites, like Pulpit Rock and Ute Pass don't really
require any solving at all, they simply provide directions to the
box. The hardest part about boxes like those is the hike.

Second, Metriona: yes, others have figured it out. In fact, many
millions of people have potentially solved Metriona since it was
first solved around 1843. A few brilliant minds, such as y-nought
and apparently your daughter's boyfriend, have solved it from
scratch, which impresses me. This however is not necessary, since,
as the LbNA web page (http://www.letterboxing.org/BoxView.php?
boxnum=15129&boxname=Metriona_Bicolor) states, the clues have
already been solved for you and you just have to talk to the right
person for the key. But you know, that y-nought is always trying to
impress people. And he succeeds.

No, I am not a linguist nor do I have any major interest in
linguistics. But then again, I am not the developer of the Metriona
clues; that was a mind far greater than mine.

And finally, yes, you ARE smart enough to play letterboxing with
me! None of my clues really require intuitive leaps, flashes of
genius, or deep thought. They're just puzzles or plays on words,
and even the harder of them typically employ something like a well-
known cypher technique and only require doing a little research and
recognizing a pattern. Now Mapsurfer's boxes... I wonder if ANYone
is smart enough to play letterboxing with him.

JeLyBean, taking a break from "A Bard's Tale" to write this...
