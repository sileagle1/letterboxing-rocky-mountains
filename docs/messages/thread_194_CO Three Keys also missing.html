CO Three Keys also missing

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-05-16 23:10:23 UTC
I found Three Keys savaged by vandals today. They had broken the
stamp in two and used the journal to express themselves intimately
and at length. Mostly about how much they enjoyed the hobby of pot
smoking and how much women loved them in their imaginary world. I was
surprised to see that one get accidentally found. It was hidden
really well and I know they didn't solve the clues; they are pretty
difficult and would require a few more synapses to fire than these
folks had available judging from their prose. I think they were
looking for a place to hide something and came upon the letterbox in
it's nook. Strangely, we share a desire for secret places with some
people that are hiding for very different reasons. I remember a
journal entry in a geocache I found once. It was "hiding" behind a
rock in an obvious little hideout clearing in some willows. The entry
went "Came to blast a doobie. Left nothing. Took the Where's George
Dollar".
I's not that big of a deal. I can repair or replace the stamp. I
will have to re-work the clues and find another spot. It is unlikely
that anyone is searching for it now anyway and both stamps in it were
my own. But if there had been some finders' stamps in the log I would
be a lot more upset. I know I have heard it on this list before, but
it's the log with everyone's stamp that is most valuable.
y-nought

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2004-05-17 13:23:30 UTC
Sorry to hear one of your boxes got vandalized. It is
easier for me to accept when somebody finds mine and
throws it away thinking it is trash, but to just destroy
something confounds me. I, too am surprised when some
of my boxes are accidentally found, especially when they
are hidden really well. Sometimes it is because the last
finder did not rehide it well, but sometimes it is because
an animal found it and left it in the open for someone to see.
Either way, losing a box is hard, especially if it is far
away and I can't replace it easily. And like you say, the
log is lost forever. Glad you will be replacing it since
I hope to look for your boxes next time I get to your area.

Silver Eagle
