A FULL LBing weekend

From: gonefromnj (gonefromnj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-09-26 16:23:27 UTC
The H-A's spent an outstanding weekend in Grand Lake and recommended
it as a letterboxer's destination of choice. Here is the final
tally:

3 days - Friday, Saturday and Sunday
13 clues printed out and taken with
12 boxes found (15 if you count 3 double boxes by Aljan Sundance)
1 Hitchhiker left behind
1 Hitchhiker picked up
2 exchanges - one planned and one surprise meeting
1 first find - our first, missed a second one by only a day
29 new stamps in our logbook

Highest Stamp: WOTY-Yule beat Moffat Road by about 200 feet; both
are over 11,000 feet.

Most Difficult Boxes to Retrieve and Return: Two Track and
Commissioner Shooting due to a wedding at Point Park

Toughest Drive: Moffat Road, followed closely by None Other Beeste.

Box Requiring Most Determination: Grand Lake Lodge due to the lodge
being closed for the season and the entrance blockaded.

Best Food Served While Stamping In: Jenny's in Empire while
stamping into Yes.

I am also posting a photo of our surprise meeting with Front Range
Hiker and her crew at the Spirit Lake Letterbox. Hopefully this
will encourage her to stop lurking and start posting. We also met
up with Aljan, but didn't get a picture together.

It was a great weekend, the weather was unbeatable and the aspen
were blazing. If you haven't gotten up there yet, get moving!

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2005-09-27 00:19:25 UTC
Based on input from Jrsy Boy of The H-A's, I have modified
the clues to my Grand Lake letterbox to include a way to
hike up from town if the Lodge is closed. You can also
try parking on the road where it is blocked and hiking to
the main parking lot. The Lodge is closed between Labor
Day and Memorial Day, but the box is accessible as long
as the trail is clear of snow. It is so beatiful up
there I wish I could visit more often. I envy ya'll!

Silver Eagle
(who is still sweating in 100 degree heat down here in TX,
but happy to have avoided Rita)
