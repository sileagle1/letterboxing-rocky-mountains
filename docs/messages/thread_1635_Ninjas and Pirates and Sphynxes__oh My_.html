Wow! I was doing a little Hibernating and I thought I heard someone
say Fandango.

Pre....I don't know what your schedule looks like, but I'm sure you
would have fun if you came to Durango DURING the Silver Bullet
Search. It's Jan 28 through Feb 1. Four clues a day and the whole
town looking for a hidden treasure worth a few hundred dollars in
coupons and merchandise. And the clues are cryptic and challenging
enough to keep the amatuers confused. (And most of us veterans too).

Plus...there is a crazy parade and about fifty other events to take
in betweeen flashes of inspiration.

This year the theme is "Durango in Da Nile", so an eqyptian outfit
and makeup can really boost the fun level. Here's the link to this
year's shindig: http://www.snowdown.org/ ......but they don't have
the schedule posted yet.

My job involves plowing snow, so the weather has a lot to do with how
much I get to participate. Last year I didn't even get a chance to
look for the Silver Bullet. I was, however, able to run over and
enter the Cat-A-Pult contest after a ten-hour shift and take last
place in that event. It was great fun tossing stuffed kitties with
trebuchets while the crowd picketed against us with ASPCA signs that
the event organizers handed out.

Anyway....just thought I would toss that idea out there.....

Oh!...and also I should say that the letterboxing is not so lonely
around here anymore. There is an active 'boxer in Bayfield and a few
in Farmington, New Mexico. Woot!

y-nought
