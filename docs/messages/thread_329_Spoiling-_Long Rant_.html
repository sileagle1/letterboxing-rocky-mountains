Spoiling-(Long Rant)

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-04 01:59:17 UTC
I may be a little more evil than average, but the thought of someone
gathering up letterboxes as trophys ocurred to me a long time ago. I
never wanted to even talk about it. But now that someone has stolen
one of JeLyBeans boxes and refused to return it I am forced to look
at that possibility.

I don't know if something like this has happened in geocacheing. It
seems more likely there than in in letterboxing. Easily found with a
GPS and many more caches. But it seems like letterboxes are so much
more dear to the owners. Cherished stamp images rather than trinkets.
When someone found Three Keys and broke the stamp it felt like they
did it because they knew it would hurt me to see it broken.

Frankly, I don't know what we could do if someone decided to gather
up all the boxes they could find in a particular area.

One of the magical things about this pastime is that it even works at
all. We have to depend on the basic goodness of not only other
letterboxers, but everyone. I am glad that for the most part we are
left alone to have our fun and connect with each other through these
hidden treasures. But with more people finding out about this all the
time, the risk goes up that there will be some people that want to
spoil it.
Just Ranting,
y-nought
