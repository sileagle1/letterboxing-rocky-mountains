From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2004-04-26 16:18:10 UTC-06:00
Well - I just got back from a business trip to Washington DC. While there, my hubby and I had time to get in ONE letterbox (yeah, I know ... in Maryland, and only ONE box?) but the one we found was called Radio Days, and was most definitely in the city.

It was a microbox - two black film canisters taped end to end. Stamp in one side, log rolled into the other side. The whole thing was velcro'd to a branch in a bush. A bit hard to find, and so - will probably last a bit for an Urban box.

Also - we didn't have time to do this one, but - there's also one INSIDE the National Zoo - check the clues for those boxes for some great ideas on city dweller boxes! :)


Best wishes,
Autumn

From: (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-04-26 22:57:52 UTC-06:00
Look for good sized parks or river walks. I have hidden some along a river
bank under rocks and I've found them in good parks. Keep in mind that they
have to be large enough parks that every inch is not groomed. Those boxes
never last, but sometimes there is a patch of trees and rocks around trails
or creeks that works nicely and won't be found.

Cadenza
