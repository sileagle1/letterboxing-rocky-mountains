3. Add 5

If you haven't, add 1755.














6. Now subtract the four digit year that you were born.










You should have a three digit number










The first digit of this was your original number
(i.e., how many times you want to have chocolate each week).

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-03-17 07:59:22 UTC
Chocolate pie.. nectar of the.. pirates...GARRR!

Pre...what route are you taking? Are you following I-70 all the way
to Moab?
If so....Zeb Gump Grizzly Creek may be open. Zeb Gump Doc Holliday..
depends on the snow in Glenwood. You could decide after you see what
it's like when you get into the area. I know that Zeb Gump Mustang
and 16 ton are clear (although make sure the road to Mustang isn't
muddy!).
Jimmy Buffet and Talk Like a Pirate (same park), all the Napolean
Dynamites (very easy to get all three in short time.. Lucky!!), Raft
the River is a drive by, Pie Eating Contest and There Were These Two
Guys (same park), Big Fish, Rocky Mountain Retro Candy (a little bit
of a walk to do the series but not a difficult one), Grand Valley,
Johnny Carino's, Commuting, Those Were the Days.. all of those are
clear as of right now.

None of the ones that have the city of Mesa or Grand Mesa as the
closest are free of snow yet.

Y-nought's No Use should be clear... it may be too far in the wrong
direction for you depending on your itinerary and route. You might
look at a map to get an idea.

If you take the 'back' road into Moab you'll hit quite a few boxes
along that road. Rather than go all the way down the highway to the
regular Moab exit, you take the small exit before it that says Cisco
and head for the river. The first box (The Westerns)is at the Dewey
Bridge.. which is immediately after you hit the river...and then
there are bunches all along the road all the way to Moab! Don't miss
Y-Nought's Alien Gothic.. it's one of my favorite boxes ever. And
then there are like a BAJILLION in Moab! Woohoo!

If I missed any someone shout it out. Oh, I know.. there's the
airport one which doesn't have posted clues. If you want to try it
let me know and I'll direct you via email.

If you want suggestions as to what order to do the Grand Junction
boxes in to get to them in a sort of geographical order let me know.
And if you aren't coming through Grand Junction have a wonderful time
anyway!

Es, singing a little song about chocolate pie
