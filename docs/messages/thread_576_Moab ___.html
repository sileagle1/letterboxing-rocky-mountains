From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-03-25 02:59:03 UTC

Hiya Jodie-
The campgrounds that Kat and I were talking about a few posts
back are BLM campgrounds, and they don't take reservations. My
experience is that you can usually find a spot if you go looking in
the afternoon. Towards evening they start filling right up. These
campsites usually cost 10 bucks a night if I remember right.

There is another possibility-- the KOA style campgrounds where you
pay 20-25 bucks for a tent site and you get to have a few amenities,
like a shower. They usually have game rooms so your kids can learn to
hustle pool while you are enraptured on your chaise lounge. The
brochure I got from the Visitor Center in Moab lists 16 such
campgrounds around Moab. So, more money, but you KNOW where you are
going to stay. They of course have RV sites too, and many of them
have one and two room cabins. Most of these allow pets, but they
charge a little extra.

I don't really know about the hotels.
