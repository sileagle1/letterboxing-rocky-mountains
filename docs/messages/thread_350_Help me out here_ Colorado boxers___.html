From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2004-09-21 17:33:52 UTC-04:00
I'm leery about publicity. The Fort Collins area boxes are definitely
receiving some attention.

I got a post through a school e-mail just yesterday that says: "Check out
the following website for something called Letter Boxing. There are 10
in Larimer/Weld County. http://www.letterboxing.org/"
I do think someone with lots of experience should talk to him and really
stress etiquette, etc. I also think that there might be some fallout
with local publicity. I've seen all of Esmerelda's Fort Collins' boxes.
They are really gorgeous and very accessible (once you've got the clues).
Cattail's lovely Horsetooth box requires a good hike. Other than
Esmerelda and Cattail, there are two newer box placers in that area. I
would really hope they wouldn't discuss specific boxes! Without some
care and thought, letterboxes are easily damaged.
We've got some boxes on the verge of going out, but we're going to wait a
bit before placing clues.
~The Catamount Carvers

Help me out here, Colorado boxers...

From: splendidbuccaneer (splendidbuccaneer@yahoo.com) | Date: 2004-09-21 22:48:02 UTC

I just got this email (did anyone else?):

I'm the Xplore writer for the Fort Collins Coloradoan newspaper and
wish to do an article on letterboxing. I would like to do an
interview and maybe shoot some pictures of you searching, hiding or
makingup a letterbox. I would like to do this this week. Please let
me know if you would be interested in this? (he included his phone
number but I don't think I should post it on the internet )

Thanks,
Miles
-----

Of course I'm way far away and couldn't do it this week. And, well,
I have mixed feelings about the publicity. But, as was discussed on
the main board recently, this guy is going to do an article whether
we help or not. So it kind of makes sense to help to try and make
sure the whole 'these are the rules of letterboxing' issue gets
covered. (I know they aren't called rules..but guidelines doesn't
sound right). A devoted boxer can hopefully guide the outcome to
some extent. I think that if he wants to actually go to a letterbox
and take pictures, we'd need to hide one just for that purpose. I
suspect that, like me, other boxers would rather not have their
boxes plastered on the paper for anyone to see and say, 'hey.. I
know that tree...'

So.. do any of you want me to give him your name? Any suggestions?
I want to get back in touch with him soon so he doesn't just go out
and start photographing boxes on his own.

Es
