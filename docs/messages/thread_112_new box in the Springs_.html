> New letterbox: Helen Hunt Falls / Silver Cascade Falls
> Colorado Springs, El Paso County, CO
> Placed by: piano&penguins
> Box webpage located at:
>
> Clues:
> The trail starts at the Helen Hunt Falls Visitor Center in North Cheyenne
Caon Park.
>
> Difficulty: Easy [placed by two 6-year old children :-)]
> Distance: Approximately 0.6 miles round trip
> Elevation Gain: 7225' to 7400'
>
> Directions: To get to the Helen Hunt Falls Visitor Center from Hwy 24 and
21st St, go south on 21st St. (which becomes Cresta along the way)
approximately 3 miles until you come to Cheyenne Blvd. Turn right (west)
onto Cheyenne Blvd. (which becomes Cheyenne Rd. along the way) and go
approximately 1 mile until you come to the Starsmore Discovery Center (2120
W. Cheyenne Rd.) at the entrance to North Cheyenne Caon Rd. (By the way,
this Starsmore Discovery nature center is definitely worth visiting if you
have the time and inclination!) Turn right onto North Cheyenne Caon Rd. and
go approximately 2 miles up the canyon until you see a large parking lot on
the right with porta-potties, and several parking spaces in front of a small
log building by a waterfall. This is the Helen Hunt Falls Visitor Center.
Park here.
>
> Walk past the visitor center on compass bearing 304' and follow the trail
along the rock wall up to the bridge over Helen Hunt Falls. You can look
right down on the falls from this bridge. This is also a great photo
opportunity when your picture is taken from below. Now the fun climbing and
counting starts!
>
> Cross the bridge and climb up 34 stairs to the first flat spot. Then
climb 15 mores stairs and cross another small bridge. Now count 18 stairs,
and then 19 loosely-defined, well-spaced stairs. Next, you walk over some
big rocks. Then take 30 more stairs, then climb with very few defined
stairs until you come to a rock wall. You can explore around here to your
left. You will find a spring and a great lookout point for Silver Cascade
Falls above.
>
> When finished exploring, go back to the main trail (officially called
Silver Cascade Trail), and climb 6 more stairs, then walk over some
half-buried tree roots. Next take 3 more rock stairs. Walk between some
big boulders until you come to the first ponderosa pine tree in the middle
of the trail. From here, go to the second ponderosa pine in the trail. Put
your back to this second pine and take a compass bearing of 220' to another
ponderosa pine. Behind this third pine tree is a tiny trail. You are
almost there! (If you went to the bridge that crosses the creek on the main
trail, then you went too far and missed the turnoff trail.)
>
> Take this small side trail up several yards to a small boulder with an
overhang that faces the creek and main trail. The letterbox is under the
overhang of this small boulder, behind a small log. Please take the
letterbox back to the main trail and continue over the bridge and up the
main trail a very short distance to the turn-around point. Here the trail
ends and you get a nice view of Silver Cascade Falls and of some of Colorado
Springs to the east. After stamping the journal, etc., please put the
letterbox back exactly as you found it. The small log is needed to hide the
box from the view of casual hikers on the main trail.
>
> Now enjoy the hike down, but be careful because it can be very slippery.
PLEASE STAY ON THE MAIN TRAILS ONLY. Silver Cascade Falls area is one of
the most dangerous in the whole canyon! It is said that people have
accidentally slipped down and over the falls when wandering off the trail
trying to get to the falls. Be very cautious, and Happy Hiking!
