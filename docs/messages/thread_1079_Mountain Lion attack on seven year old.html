Have you all heard about this incident? I realize these attacks are
rare, but I always need an occasional reminder that we do indeed live and
play in a landscape with real predators. We consider ourselves pretty
good at watching out for our kids when hiking ... no running way out in
the lead or dragging in the rear. We want a visual on our kids at all
times. This particular lion was extremely bold, and my family's "common
sense safety measures" would have been sorely lacking. This child lucked
out that he was actually holding his dad's hand at the moment of attack!

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2006-04-18 10:47:05 UTC-06:00
Es,
Were you actually thinking "aware" or "neurotic." Ever wonder why this
family of bird and canine lovers picked the Catamount Carvers for our
family sig? We actually do live in the midst of prime lion habitat. The
elusive lion has been spied by Silent Wings on one occasion, spied by
Yoque on four occasions and we see signs on a regular basis (tracks, scat
and kills). A number of our neighbors have lost animals to them here and
there. We had a lion use our rooftop to ambush an unsuspecting deer in
the middle of the night a few summers ago. That was pretty hair raising!
"Habituated lion" is a term we take pretty seriously.
~Silent Wings, wondering what the big cats think about us bipeds caching
inedible things in their territory
