Hi you all - I plan to join and watch this list for the next two weeks
or so - will be flying to Colorado Springs to visit my daughter in
Canon City on the 21st, saying six days or so. We will not have a lot
of time to letterbox, but I have a box ready to plant and leave for
you, and I would like to find at least one or two for my log book. We
will probably spend our time between Canon City and Colorado Springs,
so I would appreciate having the names of any boxes with these
attributes: (1) not much more than a mile hike, having some hip
problems and can't go further right now; (2) would like to know of at
least one that is large enough for a darling hitchhiker that is 2 1/4"
x 3 3/4 ", about 3/4" thick, which I am bringing with me for a friend
(please e-mail me privately so it won't be too obvious where I plan to
leave it). I know about Red Canyon Park and Cabin on Shelf, but the
placers are from Oregon, and I would really like some images from you
folks in Colorado as well. So anything between Coal Creek and
Colorado Springs that isn't a real strenuous hike or covered in deep
snow would be great to know about. I've checked out all of the boxes
listed at LBNA for Colorado Springs and printed clues for Little Feet,
Greenback Cutthroat Trout, Bear Creek, Pikes Peak, and Fountain Creek
- are any of those close together by car and/or big enough for my
hitchhiking friend? Any information will be so appreciated. My
daughter is not into letterboxing but will go with me after a couple
I'm sure. If I can't find a home for the hitchhiker I can leave it in
the box I plant, so that isn't really important, but I would rather
leave it in someone else's box if I can. Hope to see one of you on
the trail! Calli-K from Oregon.

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2005-12-13 03:49:02 UTC
... are any of those close together by car and/or big enough for my
hitchhiking friend?

- Little Feet: no, it's a film cannister microbox
- Greenback Cutthroat Trout: probably not, it's a small box about
2.5" square (most of mine are this size)
- Bear Creek: maybe - did this one some time ago and don't remember
it very well. It's on a nature center trail and requires some
moderate uphill walks.
- Pikes Peak, the box is probably big enough, but there's no stamp
and I would think twice about driving up the mountain this time of
year
- Fountain Creek: maybe - did this one some time ago and don't
remember it very well. It's pretty flat if I recall, on nature
trails through some marshland. Can't remember how big the box is.

You might also consider Chantress, which is a fairly short and level
walk in a nice setting, although in the northern part of the
Springs, and both Red Rock Canyon and CONTEMPLATIVE TRAIL/GARDEN of
the GODDESSES, which are a little longer but pretty nice, near
Garden of the Gods. Red Rock Canyon will require some climbing some
stairs in an old quarry.

Email me off list if you have any questions: jelybean(at)adelphia
(dot)net.

'Box Happy,
JeLyBean
