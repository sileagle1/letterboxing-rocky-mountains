Many patch companies will create a sample patch based on a design. The
company I used for the Great Lakes patch is

http://ezembroideredpatch.com/

You need to submit a final design with the colors you want. For example,
all you would need to do is email them a .jpg of what you want, similar to
the RockyMountainLetterboxing.jpg that Silent Doug posted. They would then
create a sample and email you a scanned image of the patch. If it met with
your approval, they would then create the quantity you requested and mail
them to you.

I have had two orders of 200 patches each. The cost is very reasonable.
Here is the sample patch they created for our Great Lakes design:
http://letterboxing.homestead.com/greatlakespatch.html

From: (kotlarek@wi.rr.com) | Date: 2008-04-17 16:25:52 UTC-05:00
Yes, I charged the orders to my personal credit card, then collected money as I sold the patches.

Here are a few more details re: the process:

We used the design for our Yahoo group as our patch design. The art was created by SpringChick.

I sent a .jpg file of the design to the patch company.

They sent me a .jpg image of the sample patch they created.

I posted the sample image to our Yahoo group (Photos section) so everyone could see it and comment. After receiving comments, I asked the patch company to modify some colors slightly. They then created another sample and sent another image.

I created a database in our Yahoo group so people could add their name and the number of patches they would want. If you haven't used the database feature before, I highly recommend it! It keeps all the info in one place, rather than a whole bunch of separate email messages. Anyone can create a database and you can define it to allow anyone to add data to it.

When I had a feel for how many patches total we wanted, I placed an order with the patch company. I ordered more than the initial requests so I would have a stock for future orders. I don't remember, but I think the minimum order is 50 patches. Of course the higher the quantity, the lower the price. You'll want to get the full quote from the patch company before you advertise an exact price, since the order will also include some shipping and handling charges.

I never promoted the patch on any other website other than our regional Yahoo group, but when Mark Pepe created a "Patches" page on his blog, I sent him a link so it could be included. If you want to generate a bigger demand to lower the price, you may want to promote the patch on a few other sites.

Whoever the individual is that ends up in charge of selling the patches, I highly recommend that you ask people to send you self-addressed stamped envelopes. That makes it very easy to fulfill the orders.

For reference, here is the link to our Great Lakes patch, so you can see how I distributed the final info about ordering.

http://letterboxing.homestead.com/greatlakespatch.html

You could put similar info in the Files section of your group, or a blog or wherever. I'm totally out of stock of our patches after selling 200 of our original design and then another 200 of a revised design after Minnesota was included in our group. I'll probably place another order after I get a backlog of requests.

I hope your patch becomes a reality so I can add it to my pack!

Wisconsin Hiker
