Oldest Letterbox in Colorado

From: Kat (Autumn) (autumnkat@gmail.com) | Date: 2008-08-05 15:36:09 UTC-06:00
At the recent Mt. Doom gathering - someone asked me which box was the first
one planted in our fair state. I had an old archive of the LBNA site before
the change-over in 2004. Sadly, when I clicked on that link, the site has
gone away. Boo! The dates in the LBNA basement for most of those old boxes
is incorrect.

mapsurfer's "The Bard's Tale" was planted in August 2000.

A trip to The Drew Clan's website showed a half dozen boxes in Colorado in
the summer of 2000, and didn't list planting dates. So, I emailed Jay Drew
to ask and I got the reply below. An awesome bit of trivia - our very first
RMLetterboxing "event" was held at the location of one of Jay's boxes -
Fountain Valley - in Roxborough Park which was pulled by park rangers in the
ensuing years.

----------------------------------------
Autumn,
How nice to hear from you! We miss Colorado so very much, and were hoping to
visit Carbondale for my brother's wedding over Labor Day but it isn't meant
to be. Our first letterbox there was our Loveland Letterbox on the Lewanee
Trail, placed July 13, 2000. Another early box was placed that same summer
by our old friend The Jolly G-Man also in Roxoborough State Park. Wow, what
an amazing place!
It's great to hear from you again, Jay & Margaret
------------------------------------

So, I'll be checking with Jolly G-Man to see if one might have been planted
earlier. He has long since left letterboxing, so I'm not certain I will
find his contact information. In the meantime, we can unofficially call the
Loveland Letterbox the oldest. :) And if anyone has any old archives they
can check, that would be spiff!

Cheers,
Autumn

From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2008-08-06 01:36:48 UTC
Here's my take on this:

In 2004 I was puzzled by the fact that the Andrews Lake letterbox had
a "planted on" date of 7/13/2000 AND Mapsurfer listed the Loveland Pass
letterbox by Jay Drew as being the first letterbox in Colorado planted
on EXACTLY the same day.

I also knew that Jay Drew had planted a letterbopx down here in Pagosa
Springs called Adoped Country on 7/16/2000, just three days later. I
knew this date was true because I found the box and it had the "planted
on" date in the logbook. So.....I wrote to Jay Drew in March of 2004
and asked him about this. Here is his reply:

------------------------------------------------------------

Hiya! I was surprised to see Loveland listed as Colorado's first
letterbox myself. At that time the letterboxing community was trying
to get
"all 50 by Y2K," but we didn't quite make it. I was psyched to get a
first. But it was the Fountain Valley box in Roxbury that we placed
first.
In any case, we just spent a week in Keystone that year, so all the
boxes we planted were pretty close together time-wise. And, to
complicate
matters, I'm not very consistent about keeping track of when boxes
were planted. I guess the logbooks will tell: we were hoping to get
back
to Colorado this summer but have decided on a California trip instead.
Maybe next year we can check the logbooks! I'll go through our old
notebook and see if I happened to record the dates, just in case.
Thanks for
asking, Jay in CT

-------------------------------------------------------

That guy is my hero!

Anyway, I posted on this list about that back in 2004. It was post #413.

My personal view is that the Drewclan placed the first box in Colorado
in Roxbury (with Jolly G-Man?)sometime around 7/12, 2000 Because it's
hard for me to imagine him planting all those up north and traveling
down here to plant Adopted Country by the 16th.

But that is just my guess. We don't have a logbook to verify this
because Roxbury is missing.

But I do know that Jay Drew told me that Mapsurfer (and now Mark Pepe)
had the Loveland Pass letterbox listed there incorrectly.

y-nought

Re: Oldest Letterbox in Colorado

From: Silver Eagle (sileagle@alltel.net) | Date: 2008-08-06 22:12:00 UTC
Howdy everyone, long time no post for me, but this topic is of great
interest to me since I, too love to hear about the "old" days. I did
some searching using the Wayback Internet Machine and came up with
LBNA site as of October, 2000:

http://web.archive.org/web/20001008074451/http://letterboxing.org/

It lists all the boxes mentioned here plus a few more. Based on Jay
Drew's claim that Loveland was placed on 7/13/2000, then it appears
to be a tie between it and Andrews Lake, which was also placed on
that date by Brooks/Devere. Another interesting fact is that the
Loveland letterbox is actually 2 boxes, so I guess there could be 3
boxes placed on the same date! It also looks like the boxes placed
by Jolly G-Man were in 2001, so he is not in contention.

On my first letterboxing trip to Colorado back in 2003 I had the
privilege of finding many of these boxes, but unfortunately not the
Loveland ones. In fact, I found almost all boxes that were placed in
Colorado at that time, and added my Grand Lake box as well. It is
amazing to see how much y'all have grown in 5 years and I need to get
back that way again.

Take care,
Silver Eagle (from TX)
