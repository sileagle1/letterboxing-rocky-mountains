From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-01-12 20:13:33 UTC

It's a Colorado thing. It's called the Colorado Outdoor Recreation
Search and Rescue Card and it costs three bucks a year.

http://www.state.co.us/searchandrescue/SARcard.htm

If you buy a hunting, fishing, or snowmobile license it is
automatically added on. This basically pays for SAR teams to be
staffed and supplied throughout the state. When someone is injured in
the backcountry the rescue is free to anyone with the card. Even
helicopter service. So, like PF said, it makes no sense to be on a
trail without one.

I don't know about Idaho, but I did talk to a SAR person in Utah once
that said they are funded through recreation tax dollars, so there is
no need to buy the card, rescues are still free. Each state has a
different way of funding these services and I think some states just
charge the person who is rescued.
