From: autumn_boxing (autumnkat@gmail.com) | Date: 2010-09-08 00:12:16 UTC
Unfortunately, I have to work this Saturday (and no getting out of it). Please keep us posted on the memorial times. Holly's mother also said she wanted to hold a mass in Seattle (am I right about the location, Peg?).

When I spoke to Holly on Wednesday, as Dick & Peg will attest, she was still her same self - full of "piss and vinegar" as my folks would have said. Though her body was failing, her mind was not. She was still funny and we shared a laugh, we both agreed that her dying "sucks", and then we shared a cry. If you were among the many who did not know she was ill, it's because she was just that way - very private, PRE-ferring to hop online when she felt good, and put a witty quip on Atlas Quest. You'd never know she'd been battling this for the last year and then some.

She did ask for a mini-meet in her honor, especially something that would pull the community together. Dick & Peg will be coordinating that, which is awesome. She's asked that her letterboxes be adopted and her letterboxing things be shared "with those who will USE them". Hah! I'm sure all of those details will be worked out later. I think a box carved for the meet and planted for her would be just a grand thing - thanks for the offer and suggestion RW.

Though I dont know her favorite charity to suggest a donation, Holly did have breast cancer, so a donation to Susan G. Komen would not be amiss.

I want to give a very special thank you to Dick & Peg - TaiChi & ChaiTea for their countless hours helping Holly. You two were a literal godsend for her, and for her family. Without TC&CT, Holly's final days would have been much more challenging. I am deeply, deeply grateful.

Kat
Autumnsfolly
