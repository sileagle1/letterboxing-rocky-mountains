From: edwebbe (edwebbe@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-10-07 20:29:45 UTC
Thanks to lionsmane, Astro D and Rubberpeace (and all the other folks
who helped) for this awesome event. Our camera was suffering from
operator error so my report will be verbal.

First, the setting was unbelievably beautiful. A perfect spot for the
gathering surrounded by colorful leaves dipping into crystal water
that skitters down the rocks.......paradise.

Then there was this constant flow of....event stamps that were
inspiring, delicious food, exchanges and travellers, yummy food, a
haunted wood full of bats, spiders, witches and a horseman (sans
head), more food, some stories and more exchanges and a brownie.

And we left thinking about what a great bunch of people we had just
seen. Old friends that we saw in person for the first time......faces
we could put with the stamp images we've been seeing for a
while....and brand new friends that share the same appreciation for a
good clue, the outdoors and folk art.

The only thing that was lacking for me was more time to spend talking
or heading down the road for a few more 'boxes. Seriously, for every
letterbox stamp I got yesterday there are at least five more in that
area on my wish list. And hunting letterboxes with new friends is
such a kick!

Oh well...I guess I'll just have to go back....

Thanks again,

y-nought

P.S. As the darkness settled in last night La Paloma was introduced
to La Paloma.....priceless!

Y-Nought,

I am nodding my head with everything you said. I enjoy sharing
letterboxing with my Twinville Trekker family, but they are not as 'die-
hard' as I can be and usually tolerate letterboxing for up to a 5-box
limit......and Hey! At that point, I'm just getting started! hehehe

So, at this past week's event joining a small group of fellow
letterboxers, discussing and working out clues, while hiking and
enjoying nature and chatting about letterboxing was such a blast! I had
no idea it could be so fun.

I was telling my huband later that night when I got home, that it was
such an odd feeling afterwards remembering how I felt so welcomed and
part of a tightknit community, as if noone, no matter that some of us
had never met before, were strangers. I was a little nervous showing up
by myself, not knowing anyone, except by trail names we've seen in
logbooks and online. But it was like joining a letterboxing family or a
tribe. Too cool.

I can't wait to do this again.
And if anyone is planning to visit Santa Fe or Albuquerque, please let
us know. We will be happy to join you for some letterboxing or some
chatting over coffee/tea.
