Letterbox Etiquette

From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-09-21 14:02:22 UTC-06:00
Would it be out of Etiquette to make a LB to honor someone that would
require a $5 (or more) donation?

Let me explain.

My neighbor's son (he is 2) has a very rare disease that has left him
completely disabled. Horse therapy (I can't remember the correct name)
is a wonderful thing for disabled kids. Thomas was able to go for 5
sessions this summer. However, their insurance company changed their
mind about covering this therapy and they were left with a $500 bill.
Thomas LOVED this therapy, and they saw a wonderful change in his motor
skills. But, having 4 children (10 yr old twins, a 5 year old and
Thomas who is 2) and only one income, they can't afford to continue
sending Thomas.

My thought would be to put a box at the center and ask for a mandatory
donation of at least $5.00 in order to stamp in. ALL of the money would
go towards the $100 sessions for Thomas.

Would you visit the box even if it meant making the donation?

Thank you all in advance for your input.

From: Debbi Scott (dscott5377@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-09-21 16:43:03 UTC-06:00
Lisa / Esmerelda:

I am planning to leave the box in the Pegasus Office. You would have to
ask the office staff for the box. You would give them your money before
you got the box. The money will go in an envelope that I will go by and
collect 2-3 times a month. My daughter volunteers there, so we are
there at least that often. I don't want to burden the staff (it is very
small) with having to set up an account for Thomas (I am not even sure
they can do that). So, each time I have enough funds for 1 therapy
visit ($100), I would ask for a gift certificate that I will present to
Thomas' family.

Also - as each log book is full, I will be giving that to them as well -
like a card =]

PS - I will also make a HUGE note in the clues stating that a donation
is required, so there are no surprises when you get to the box.


Debbi
