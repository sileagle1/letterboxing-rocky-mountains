Gathering

From: Kathy Castleberry (kathycastleberry@msn.com) | Date: 2005-04-23 21:55:54 UTC

I am finally back home. After the gathering we hung around in Moab
several days then went to Phoenix for a week. I sure enjoyed meeting
all of you at the gathering and wish I could have stayed a little
longer. I thought my family was pretty nice to give me 2 hours, but I
could have used 4. (never happy) My husband got a real good picture
of the stamp page that we all stamped on and a few pictures of the
group. I am still trying to get names with faces and wish I had just
lined everyone up and took a group shot. Did anyone do that? I found
several boxes in Moab and hid a little HH in one. I am disappointed
that I did not find the box in Negro Bill's canyon....looked and
looked and I am wondering if it is missing. Also did not find Ticaboo
but found Lulu and we sure had fun in that place - it was a beautiful
spot. Is Ticaboo missing, Kat? Or were four of us just too dumb to
find it??? Maybe you don't want to answer that!! Anyway, now I will
catch up on all my email and LB emails - I have 175 emails to
read!!! Puppypaws (ready for a gathering in Colorado)

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2005-04-25 04:19:38 UTC

Welcome home, Puppypaws! wow, what a fun trip you must have had! Did
you find any boxes down in Phoenix? It was wonderful meeting you and
your wonderfully understanding friends at the gathering. I missed
out on getting your stamp for my journal, but that wonderful little
dog will be in some of my box journals, I'm sure!

Ticaboo is safe and cozy up under her rock. For some reason, almost
everyone has had trouble finding this box. Only a handful have come
through with flying colors. It seems that most people haven't gone
up the wash far enough to get to the top of the second pourover,
maybe 1/2 mile from the tunnel. You'll have to visit the spot
again!!! LOL!

We just got home last night from a short weekend in St. George (team
dance for Aspen)and had fun finding two of CW Sunseeker's boxes on
the drive over. Yay!

I'm so sorry to hear all the woes of our little group, poor Stubby!
And I cannot believe someone urinated on a box. Why are there such
sickos? Geez!!

Kenna and I had great fun the other evening hunting in near dark
with a big ol moon overhead. We happily stamped in at "art on the
rocks", one of the wonderful new boxes in Moab.

Kat (also in the rain today, carving away)
