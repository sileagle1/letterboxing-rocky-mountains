I sharpen mine sometimes using a very fine whetstone. It doesn't
replace getting new ones every now and then, but it helps. The
problem is that the sharness of the ones straight from the stores
isn't always consistent.

My real pet peave though is that I can't seem to find nibs that have
good cuts (coming from the store). Does anyone know what I mean? It
seems like the edge of all the ones in the store isn't straight, but
has either an uneven bend or part of the nib that sticks out
different than the rest. I love my carving tools. They are much
better than just the x-acto, but I wish there were some better
quality standards. The other brands I find online are way to
expensive to make it worth switching.
