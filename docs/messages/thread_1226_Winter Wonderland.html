From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2006-12-22 13:18:37 UTC-07:00
The Catamount Carvers checking in with all of you ... at last. Our roads
stayed nicely plowed, but it took us quite some time to just dig a foot
path from the house to the road. It is quite a distance. The car is
still buried, but Yoque smartly parked the truck right up near the road
and it is now dug out. We await for Quest to restore our phone service,
which has been out for days now. DSL works only in the middle of the
day. We're just staying home with colds, shoveling, house training a
puppy ... oh my, and trudging through deep snow to take care of a few
different neighbors' animals and plants, while they are off in places
like HAWAII. I believe that this kind of snow depth is NORMAL for Aljan
and it just keeps building and doesn't melt off until spring.

As for the Blizzard of '83, I remember that one! I was a college student
on Christmas break and working snow removal for a grounds maintenance
company. I hand shoveled a ton of snow in the midst of and following the
blizzard. Now, I generally make my kids shovel before they have sledding
rights. With this type of snow, it is a family affair and my back is
talking to me today. Add me to the list of folks wanting a new snow
shovel for Christmas or a twenty-something back ....

Happy Holidays!

~Silent Wings
