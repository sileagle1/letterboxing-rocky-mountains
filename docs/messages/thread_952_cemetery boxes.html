Re: cemetery boxes

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2005-12-17 16:34:48 UTC
I've been 'boxing all over the US and have found many cemetary
letterboxes (and planted a few myself). I have seen both those
within the actual cemetary and those placed outside, nearby, or in
the surrounding fence or wall. My general observations:
- most boxes are NOT placed within the cemetary, however, most also
require you to ENTER the cemetary to look for a headstone or other
landmark
- the object within the cemetary is often used in the clues (eg, a
date might be compass heading, or you may have to line up some
statues to get a bearing, etc. - I had one where you had to look
through a hole in the headstone to sight the box location)
- boxes placed WITHIN the cemetary are more likely to go missing
- whether within or without, cemetary boxes are cool
(ok, that last one was an opinion, not an observation, but you get
the idea)

'Box Happy,
JeLyBean

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2005-12-17 16:17:59 UTC-07:00
I have planted a cemetery box that I just loved. It was outside of this
wonderful old ghost town cemetery in my Land of the Yankee Fork series. The
whole series is one of my favorites. I have heard a lot of debate about
appropriateness, so I think sensitivity is good. I hid mine just outside of
the fence, even though it is not still an active cemetery (it also made it
easier to find a non-public hiding spot.)

I also found one box in Texas last summer. It was in a more modern
cemetery, but the box was hidden near a special memorial fountain and not by
individuals' graves. I thought this was still tasteful and okay.

--just my thoughts,
Cadenza

This doesn't apply to everyone, but I would love it if people visited
my grave to get a compass bearing in search of some folk art. On an
Autumn day and it is a family trying for their very third letterbox and
they have driven a little ways. One of the kids has become sidetracked
at a statue of a horse and the rest of them are reading the clues again
under a big Catalpa tree. They are having fun, but they are a little
bit worried that they won't find it after going so far out of their
way, and they did tell their Aunt that they would be at her house in an
hour. And then the youngest one sees it and says, "Here it is!" And
they walk over to the modest headstone with the Y and the Zero engraved
in the bottom. Who knew that kid would be so good at this?

That would be cool.

But I might just be cremated and have my ashes placed inside two
ziplocks in a tupperware hidden beside a trail somewhere and become a
WOM gravesite.
