BMAO in Moab April Fools Trip

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-04-16 02:48:22 UTC
Let's just say I exceeded my expectations in the boxing department and
had a GREAT time and would like to thank all the placers for their
boxes out that way. Sorry it's a long post, but as some of you have
figured out by now, it was a VERY full trip!

The Trip West:

I needed a stop and found myself at the Grizzly Creek rest area. Got
out of car to poke around. Had a very, very vague sense there was a
box in this area, but for now I was just PREconnoitering for possible
placing on the way back. Poked around for maybe 10-15 minutes. Went
to this tree and looked upstream. Hey I thought that would be a good
place for a ..

Wait a minute! What's this? Sure enough I'd ended up right on top of
Es's box here w/o ever having read the clues! Not even once. My copy
of them was in my car! As I was busy printing out clues the week
before I left I couldn't help wondering: I wonder if anyone's ever
found a box when they weren't really looking for one? So, I can
answer yes! A nice giggly surprise for the first box of the trip!

Also, some perve was hanging out at the picnic table near this box. I
can also now confirm in the affirmative an opinion I've always had.
When I returned to the parking lot the only vehicle there was a REALLY
big oversized truck which had to belong to pervert guy. Let's just
say, in this case it IS true what they say about guys with big trucks
and the relative size of their "other equipment" . Anyway, I
digress...ahem!

Made one more quick stop before Moab for Boxer Lover's Raft the
River. Loved how this stamp captured what it's all about here and how
it inks up in color.

Day # 1 BMAO in Moab:

First stop Es's Westerns box. Just what you'd expect in a Es box.
Great history, spot, log book and stamp. I was still drinking coffee
and waking up while sitting on a rock by the Dewey Bridge while
stamping up. Great way to start the first full day of boxing!

Next on to Kennamtn's Inidgo box and a FF :-). wahooo

On to hike the rest of this trail. Poked around to see if I might
find the right place to begin's Y's Alien Gothic box, but never got
going the right way, I guess. Fisher Tower and this 4.5 mile r/t
hike was well worth hiking even (gasp) w/o boxes to find!

Now kinda hot, tired, and hiked out went for a lovely drive along
highway 128 along the Coloroda River - one of my fave roads in the
whole country and a big part of the trip destination. Nabbed
rivergeek's Colorado River and Paddle Hard boxes. Watched the sun go
down and realized in BBD (Before Boxing Days) I'd floated by or driven
by these boxes many times. Then headed back to camp - sunburned,
tired (in a good way) and ready for some campfire time and....

pie! Sorta....The next day being my actual bday I'd picked up some
cheese cake for the trip. Cheese cake is like a "3-fer": It's like
pie, it's like cake, and it's got cheese.....mmmmm cheese. Made me
think about more ideas for that Homage to Fromage box.

Day # 2 The Big, Big Day - Fools Day

Oh I just get happy all over again thinking about this day :-)

Spent the firt part of the day "hanging out with" Kat and her Desert
Bighorn box (still working on coffee here...all that counting sure
woke me up!), then Portal Trail #1, Stopped at Ye Ol' Climbing spot on
the way home for Kat's Moab Man - what a cutey,
then tagged along on with Lulu and Ticaboo on their big Day Out.
Delightful little canyon and felt in some way I was "there" with all
you who went to the Box on the Rocks meet the PREvious year (almost
exactly). Had a giggle at Blackvelvtrav's Easy Rider Kokopelli (koko
being a spring-oriented trickster and a lot like us April Fools) and
literally laughed out loud at A & J Bear's Don't Bust the Crust box
(cute little box and sneaky hiding spot too and after reading this
line in SO many local boxes...Ha!)

I love all the wonderful colors and images in these two pages of my
book. So southwest, and the graphics are really my aesthetic style!

Headed back to the car for some lunch across the road at the river
before the plan was to hit Kat's Layers box. Tried to start the car
afterwards, even though I'd just moved it for a picnic and the stoopid
thing wouldn't start. Yikes! A nice couple from Carbondale, CO
helped me out with a jump to the battery, but I was paranoid now about
driving into the back of beyond, shutting off the car and being
stranded. Drove into town and was determined not to shut off the car
until I at mechanic - not easy to do on a late Saturday afternoon.
Once there, got some ice and supplies then held my breath as I started
the car. Bloddy thing started just fine! Drove around town turning
the car off and on, and the little green machine that clicked 90K on
the way over was just fine - as it always has been sinc the day I
bought it - huge relief!

Sheeze, I still got plenty of daylight left. What's this dourown
panel box, I thought as I sat in my car delaying restarting it and
looking for something to read. Haven't been out that way from town
yet. I had food, I had beer in the car along with some blankets. So
what if I get stranded (thankfully no more auto issues for the trip.)

What an AMAZING canyon to be exploring right in that rosy tangerine
glow of the sunset hours! Then there's this box itself. Wow, wow,
wow what a terrific way to end a really great birthday! I was
grinning the whole way back to camp :-)..for more campfire and
cheesecake (and red wine) ....

Day #3

The only truly sunny, lovely day of the trip. Having been so inspired
by all the really awesome spots and boxes, I took the morning off to
hang out at Hittle Bottom (love that name - it's a place not a body
part) on the CO River in my lawn chair. Sipping more coffee (have I
mentioned I'm NOT a morning person and it takes me awhile to get
vrooooming each day?) I dug through the few box-making supplies I'd
brought with me to put something together to leave behind in Moab. (I
DID leave behind this box. Coincidently it's called BMAO in Moab on
LbNA.) I also helped some kids make a sand castle on the beach that
morning and, I realized too late, had also done a pretty good job on
the beginnings of a sunburn.

Some hikers on the trail back from Kat's Layers box that same day said
my knees were about the same color as the surrounding rocks. Ooops!
It was SO worth it, though! Loved this series of boxes (I did
geography in college) and the unbelievable hike, again in the rosy
glow of sunset. I just sat on a rock for an hour and watched the
world.

Day #4 - Last Day in Moab

Packing it in this last day! Sheeze I only just realized in looking at
my book how all over the place I was that day!

Back to the canyon where dourown is to see some awesome rock art stamp
art in the form of Aljan's Phoenix Rising box and an extra special
treat I'd only heard tales of on this list. Art on the Rocks by the
Bears went hopping by my book, Snoeshoe Koko (Kat) - great view!),
Fish Out out of Water (by 10 year old Lizman) on my way up to Dead
Horse Point State Park.

Ok this place is amazing! And again I arrived in time for Magic
Hours. Had the park practically to myself for an hour or so as I
hiked around. Then when I finally did get around to looking for boxes -
holy crap, they kept popping up everywhere! Then an off duty ranger
showed up and walked by where I was with a LOT of boxing loot!
(I'd recognized her from paying the park fee - it's SO worth the cash
to see this park!) on her way to the vista point with her yoga mat.
Oh dear god, I have all these boxes, its getting late and I'll never
be able to get them all back unobserved! Do I still have food, beer
and blankets in my car? I'm gonna be here all NIGHT!

Oh right - check! Heck what do I care? It's all good! (hehehe
there's something to be said for basically living out of one's car for
a week.) After getting a few boxes back OK I went and peeked at the
vista point...what the?? Dunno WHERE she went (I suspect she went
over the edge to a private spot) but I was able to get the rest of the
boxes back tucked away safely. The last evening in Moab turned out to
be QUITE the boxing extravaganza! Some talented placers in a
phenomenal spot. Great way to end the last day. Ya'll rock!

Day #5 The Way East

Stopped off on my way back to I-70 to place Thompson Canyon and Just
Say Go boxes. I'd discovered this place years ago and did up a few
boxes in advance to leave behind. I hadn't even had a chance to email
the Moab crew privately about their boxes, and to mention these clues
were up before Kat piped up via LbNA totally excited. Hehehe - nice
to leave Happy Campers (who had made me equally happy during my
trip) "in my wake" on the eastbound trip.

I'd run out of daylight on my trip to Moab to stop by Commuting Those
Were the Days (Kat) so made the stop en route back. Great little stop
off, history and image...and dinosaur exploring...

Here beginnith the day of playing "tag" (in reverse) with arttrekker
(who you might remember had posted on this list about her upcoming
trip from Calif via Avon to NM. I kinda role my eyes now too when I
think about her. She stamped into EVERY box between here and Glenwood
on MY birthday....)Sheeze she doesn't mess around! And snagged a HH
who'd been sitting in this box for a bit I would have had! if I'd been
able to stop on my way out. She also managed to snag my FFs on two
boxes I left in western CO (Ruler of the Schoolyard and No Name) less
than 24 hours after I got home and got the clues up. We some good
laughs back and forth in email after I go home.

And finally, that Napolean Dynamite guy sure is busy in Palisade. I
got to see him, like 3 times, dudes and dudettes....right after, you
guesse it, artrekker visited a few days before.

Nothing like rolling into town at 11pm, having to unload the car, take
your first shower in 4 days and get up in 7 hours later to go back to
work the next day to begin to ruin a really, really good boxing buzz!

Least it was only a 3 day week and I was able to get back out there
both days last weekend here in the Denver area and got to meet Aljan
in person.

Yep, if life were any better these days I think it would be down right
illegal!

preboxed
