From: Autumn (Autumn@shadowslight.com) | Date: 2005-07-18 10:29:17 UTC-06:00
Scott,
I found Butterfly Trail not long ago - mid-late June. If you (or the
placer) want to contact me offlist, I can tell you the particulars. We
found that one clue was not there, and the box was not where I expected it
to be, once we found the correct location.

Also - I like your idea of a missing box list. A twist on the idea - you
could create a "database" right here on the RML message board - list the box
name, date reported missing, status from the placer, etc. Would be easy to
maintain. I can help get it set up if you like. Would need strict rules
on "no spoilers" for the box, and we would NOT have the status of boxes that
are "alive and well" - so hopefully this will discourage folks from the
types of databases that have been problematic in the past. Just a simple
list of which boxes are down for maintenance, missing, or potentially
missing and placer contacted.

If there's much discussion about this, I can create a poll to get votes from
the group?

Autumn

Re: Butterfly Trail - and missing box database

From: dixssps (Dixieh7@msn.com) | Date: 2005-07-18 17:06:11 UTC
I thought I'd jump in with my thoughts on this. I agree with
Ramdelt that there isn't anything worse than traveling to find a
letterbox and it's gone missing. Of course, the boxer could be
misreading the clues and it isn't missing at all. BUT, it really
could be missing! If Ramdelt is willing to take on the project,
then I vote for it! I would love to have a place to double check
before I walk out the door! Maybe it could have a spreadsheet look
that tells you if: a. the box has been taken out by placer for
service, b. one boxer reported it missing, look for it at your own
risk c. It's MISSING...verified by placer, and or several boxers!
I know this could get crazy, but if there is permission given by the
placer, I don't see the problem. In fact, it would help many
boxers! I've sworn off letterboxing under my breath on more than
one occasion because of the disheartening feeling when a box is gone
and I feel I wasted time that could have been spent looking for
another box that IS there! Placers have to be RESPONSIBLE to either
be able to check on the status of their own box or have a contact to
do it for them. If they aren't willing to do that, then why should
boxers take up their time to look for a box that is missing! A
reference list is a GREAT idea Ramdelt! I personally would be so
appreciative.

Autumn, I think a poll is a GREAT idea!

Hugs, Dixie Peach
