Good News and Bad News

From: gonefromnj (gonefromnj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-04-25 00:44:11 UTC

First, the good news: the Enginerd letterbox isn't a total loss.
It did stink of urine, but probably only got in through a small hole
in the outer ziploc. Nothing inside the box itself seemed to have
been affected. The logbook was damp, and was noted as such by the
next to last stamper-inner, but wasn't discolored and didn't smell
of urine. Nothing inside the inner ziploc bags smelled like they
had been peed on. I'm not sure how the logbook got damp, but am
relieved that can still be used. We pulled the box but will find a
more secure location to place it near that spot that won't run the
risk of being used as a urinal.

The bad news is that on our way to pull Enginerd, we stopped to
check on Have a Homebrew and it was nowhere to be found. It has
become apparent that I need to do some serious thinking about my
hiding places. Lemony went missing, Bite my Shiny... had a tendency
to wander, Enginerd got peed on and now Have a Homebrew has run
away. I believe I will be focusing on my F count over my P count
for a while, and maybe take notes.

I will keep working on improving my carving since I got a bunch of
new Speedball tips for my birthday (which was spent letterboxing,
imagine that). After finding Homebrew missing, I was ready to give
it all up, but finding boxes in Castle Rock, Boulder and Lyons, as
well as dropping off one hitchhiker and picking up another, I can't
seem to do it. I'm going to do my first hitchhiker and hope to drop
it off near Disney World in June.

I also still can't log into the LbNA member section.

Sorry for rambling, but had a lot to get off my chest. Saw some
familiar stamps this weekend, which always makes me smile.

Thanks for listening,
Jrsy Boy of The H-A's
