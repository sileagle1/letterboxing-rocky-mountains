Re: Orange PZKut

From: Judy (jowagnon@comcast.net) | Date: 2007-01-28 21:08:48 UTC
I've used some of the orange. I had a difficult time seeing where I
had already cut. Someone said to color the surface first to correct
that, but I haven't tried it.

As for the texture Aljan mentions, you might try this that I used on
some grade B WHITE to turn it into grade A. (I've not tried it on the
ORANGE.) Set you iron somewhere in between rayon/polyester and wool.
Put the PZKut between two sheets of very smooth paper right on the
kitchen counter. Gently press the iron on the top piece of paper. You
don't really need to press; it's the heat that does the work. Try it
just a few seconds and work up to accomplish what you want. Gently
peel the top paper away from the PZKut. The medium first melts a
little and then re-hardens, picking up the smoothness from the paper.

Boxer Lover(47)
