praise for CO letterboxers

From: gonefromnj (gonefromnj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2005-08-25 18:53:58 UTC
Antimony from Kentucky shared with me her Denver area LBing
experiences:

i thoroughly enjoyed the great LB in your area. thanks to you and
all the LBer's in your area for creating such great lb! if you are
ever down this way (cincinnati) let me know and i will hook you up
with a trip itinerary where you can box your heart out.

3 days all shortened by afternoon rain, about 40 miles hiked, 2300'
elevation gain total, 17 boxes gotten (9 misses) and 2 HH - all from
the greater denver vicinity.

particularly notable stamps by esmerelda (little raven, buffalo bill
cody lb).

scariest hike - rain cloud lb which is located along dinosaur
ridge. the clues say 30 minutes up to the box, but i went way
beyond that and still no box. there is supposed to be a trail that
splits to the left and everytime i dared to look that way it went
straight down to the valley floor. eventually reached the
summit and got too squeamish to continue (remember i am a
flatlander) and called it quits. i suspect that i was too
preoccupied with DYING to scrupulously look for the box. the
mountain bikers flying down the hill did NOT make me feel all
that safe.

the REAL rattlesnakes in rattlesnake gulch LB area were also quite
notable!

all-in-all maybe not the most numbers ( i have on a few occasions
gotten that many in 1 day!), but a great variety and types of
letterboxes and some breathtaking hikes - literally. you are very
fortunate to live in such a gorgeous location. i LOVED the open
spaces and how they have been interconnected.

again thanks to you and your local LBers for such a challenging trip
antimony aka mary
