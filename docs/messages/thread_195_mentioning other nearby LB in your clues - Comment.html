I did mention a nearby geocache in my last clue but I just
stumbled across that. Any letterboxer would find that anyway. I agree
with Silver Eagle in that secretive or even "harder" clues are easy
to tell from the straightforward clues. And I also agree that most
people with easier clues would appreciate the added traffic at their
box.
I had an idea for a box where the clue would be based on the
location of other boxes. "North of such and such, west of such and
such." So the other boxes would have to be located first. But I also
worried about mentioning someone elses box in my clue. If I do that I
think I will ask permission first. Just a courtesy really. To avoid
the surprise of reading about ones own box in someone else's clue.
For the really hard clues, I would ask permission to even talk
about them. If I solved A Bard's Tale (in my dreams, right?) I would
ask mapsurfer before I announced it on a list. Sometimes the author
might want to keep EVERYTHING secret about a box, even whether or not
it has been found.
