Well - I'm resigned to the fact that after five years of boxing, I need
a new journal. Mine is just too full, and never was exactly what I
wanted.

I'd love to get some recommendations on how others have handled this
rather painful process. I'm anal and want everything all together -
but I cringe at the idea of trying to cut and paste (physically) all my
stamped images from the old crappy book, into something new. Do I just
get over it and start fresh?

I'd love some recommendations on a where to get a new journal -
preferably something inexpensive, and what others are using. My old
one was a blank sketchbook from Borders or B&N - hardcover which is
both good and bad.

I want to get it going before the There and Back Again gathering in
August, so I can capture the fifty boxes (holy cow!!) and all the
exchanges! :)

Cheers,
Autumn

From: luvsmoab (klawry@citlink.net) | Date: 2008-06-01 21:16:16 UTC
My journals are from a local bookshop here in Moab. They are like sketchbooks, blank
inside but made of a nice textured paper, the perfect size so that I can paste photos and
ff's along with the stamp, and they open up totally flat for easy stamping. On one side I
stamp a couple of boxes, and the other side I make notes about the boxes or a cool photo
of the spots. They come with different colored covers that have etchings of petroglyphs
and I love them because they are all the same size. I am on my third journal. I bought
several in case they stop carrying them at that shop.

My first journal was a pain because it had a leather cover that wouldn't open all the way,
so it was hard to stamp into. It has a lot of my favorite boxes in it however, and is small
enough I just keep it tucked into my backpack with my other boxing stuff. My daughter
loves to look at all those old stamps and look at the pictures while trying to remember the
spots we went to when she was so little. I couldn't stand the thought of cutting apart that
first journal because it just has a lot of really cool stuff in there. I cut and pasted little
pockets for trinkets found with ff's and so it is a lot of fun to look in it. I say find a journal
in a size you really like and get a couple of them, keep the first journal the way it is, kinda
like an old friend to visit!

KAT

Re: Need a new logbook

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2008-06-03 04:47:02 UTC
If you go to either Meininger, or Guiry's, or other specialty art
supply shops, they usually have a decent array of journals that have
neat covers, good blank pages for stamping in, and are spiral bound
so they lie flat for stamping.

When we run out, we just go get another one, marking the dates on the
inside cover.

As for the journal you have now, is it that you don't like the cover
and how the pages are kept in it and you want to have it redone? Or
you want to try and move all the stamps that you have into one single
journal? I hand-bind books, and can give you several ideas for
creating your own if you are so inclined. =3 It's easier than it
sounds, I swear!

~RavenWolf
