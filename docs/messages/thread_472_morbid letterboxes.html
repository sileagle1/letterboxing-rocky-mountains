> Woohoo! What a fun note..thanks so much! Did you by any chance spot

>
> Here's how I transfer for stamp carving.. I know it's not the best
> way but it works for me.
> I haven't been able to find one of the old type of copy machines, so
> had to go with plan b. Or c or d depending on who you talk to.
> I print out my design from the computer or draw it by hand. When
> it's the way I want it I go over the lines with a pencil. I flip it
> onto the carving material, rub all over it back and forth and up and
> down and swirly and jiggy with a blender pen that I got at an art
> store. It's a clear fluid that's for transferring and blending..I
> just went into the art store and described it and they knew exactly
> what it was. While it's still kind of damp I rub the back with a
> folder thingie...the bone letter opener looking thing that you use
> to fold paper for books, etc. (got it at a craft store). You can
> use anything for that I suspect. I used to do it without the
> blender stuff, and it will transfer. But with the blender pen you
> get every smidge of pencil to transfer to the rubber (and some of
> the ink from the computer). At this point I know you could go ahead
> and carve, but I usually go over it all again on the rubber with a
> very fine point black marker. It doesn't smudge when you're carving
> and gives me sharper lines to carve along. It sounds like a lot of
> work but it's not really. You'll have fun! Especially if there's
> pie.
> I bet some of these other folks have some great systems. Anyone?!

> Here's how I transfer for stamp carving.. I know it's not the best
> way but it works for me.
> I haven't been able to find one of the old type of copy machines, so

> I print out my design from the computer or draw it by hand. When
> it's the way I want it I go over the lines with a pencil. I flip it
> onto the carving material, rub all over it back and forth and up and
> down and swirly and jiggy with a blender pen that I got at an art
> store. It's a clear fluid that's for transferring and blending..I
> just went into the art store and described it and they knew exactly
> what it was. While it's still kind of damp I rub the back with a
> folder thingie...the bone letter opener looking thing that you use
> to fold paper for books, etc. (got it at a craft store). You can
> use anything for that I suspect. I used to do it without the
> blender stuff, and it will transfer. But with the blender pen you
> get every smidge of pencil to transfer to the rubber (and some of
> the ink from the computer). At this point I know you could go ahead
> and carve, but I usually go over it all again on the rubber with a
> very fine point black marker. It doesn't smudge when you're carving
> and gives me sharper lines to carve along. It sounds like a lot of
