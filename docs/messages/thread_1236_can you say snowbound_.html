Things I've learned while being snowboundso far at least...

Frosty the Snowman is a Sicko. He's NOT cute. He's an evil twisted
individual who needs to be flattened by a snowplow, "soon-ish."
Along with his corncob pipe and button nose....We'll find those last
two come spring.....maybe.

"Black eyes" made out of coal sounds a LOT like "black ice."

Driving, like voting, is a privledge not a right in Denver. ha! Be
patient enough and you just might be "allowed to."

A snowplow making its way down one's Denver residential street is a
Betting Matter with your neighbors while you're out on the
street..shoveling, always shoveling. Most of us bet a plow is never
coming, but the postal person doesafter 7 days, or there abouts.
Aka "soon-ish."

"Climbing" a parking meter barely sticking out of 3-4 feet of snow
to attempt to put in coins can be amusing and might earn you some
honks and thumbs up because, well, that's just silly, but HAS been
done... Trust me on this one.

Watching a US Mail/FedEx/UPS truck get stuck is entertaining .if
viewed from the warmth of your home and you ain't the poor driver,
but it DOES get old after ..a week, bless their hearts. Saying to a
driver of said trucks while you're out shoveling "How you holding
up? Sure is nice to see you out on our street" earns you a big
beaming smile, but do NOT mention being stuck in the snow. On
either end of the convo!...Ever!

For you pirate types, "Grabbing the Booty" at least as insinuated by
Craig Ferguson on the Late Late show can SO mean something erm
else. I never thought about it THAT way beforedunno why. Must be
the benefit of watching re-runs and being bored out of my ever-
loving mind.

Milk, eggs, bread, wiper fluid and the use of a shovel are worth at
least their weights in gold, yet CAN be bartered Trust me on this
one.

Baking stuff, like pie, usually takes eggs or milk and sure warms up
the place! But must ALWAYS be shared :-)

The grocery store might have empty shelves but the liquor store
never does. This a good thing! DOH!

DIA = Death In an Airport. Nuff said. Ahem.

Dogs frolicking in the snow are unbelievably cute! 1-2 foot long
icicles hanging from one's roof while looking out at what looks like
a dawn glow with all that reflection....at 3:30 am is NOT to be
forgotten! Ever!

Sledding down the slope in front of the State ferking Capitol is,
well...ferking cool!

"Do you mind giving me a push?" is the new motto of Denver.

Happy New Year!

pre :-)
With a new snow shovel for digging out letterboxes....or making
super duper sandcastles....next time I find myself near a warm sunny
wnow-free! beach......

Happy New Year, Rocky Mountain boxers! (and Sharpeis and BCs and other
canines!)

Thought I'd check in on you guys, now that the excitement has died
down, and you're (PREhaps) out and about again. Boy, was I glad not
to be anywhere near the Denver airport in all that hullabaloo. I
listened to Car Talk last weekend and there was a woman who got
caught in the blizzard (she said the weather report called for the
storm to seriously hit in the mid-state in the afternoon, and she
was south in the morning), and had to drive a couple of hundred miles
in first gear in her auto trans Forester. She said it was so icy she
didn't dare touch the brakes, and sometimes, even in first, the rpms
were redlining. eek. Our one real storm of the season was the weekend
after Thanksgiving, and I was driving back from my sister's in Salinas
when I crashed into the truck that had spun into my lane. My Forester
gave it's life for me (the photos are pretty impressive!) and I'm
fine, not to mention infinitely grateful. Loving the new Outback, too.

These Sierra mountains sure could use some of your snow. This seems to
be the winter all the precip is passing us by. We did get to enjoy a
of couple weeks of single digit temps (pretty unusual for us), but we
need COVERAGE!!
