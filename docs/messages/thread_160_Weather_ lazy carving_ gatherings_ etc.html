Weather, lazy carving, gatherings, etc

From: (cadenza74@earthlink.net) | Date: 2004-02-15 23:22:48 UTC-07:00
hmm, I wonder if I would have time or money to make it to CO if you did it
on a long weekend . .
I never know what my schedule is these days, but it would sure be fun to
meet some of you finally.

I've also been a lot lazier than I anticipated this winter--where carving is
concerned, that is. I have to admit though. I just finished a stamp
tonight I am very proud of. It's of Chief Joseph, and I'd like to plant it
somewhere on the Nez Perce trail to honor him.

One of the disadvantages of living out here with no other letterboxers is
that I am able to share my stamps with so few people. I catch some coming
and going on their way to Yellowstone and a few have come for family
reunions. Now I find myself torn. I want to share the carving with some
others, but I hate taking away from the fun discovery of opening a box to
find a cool carving that you hadn't anticipated. I feel like you all would
be fun to share with, but I also realize that if anyone is more likely to
make it up to the region I'd likely plant in, it would be Rocky Mtn types.

I don't know. What think ye?
Cadenza


I can't WAIT to get out there and box! Though I must admit ... I had
planned to take the long winter months indoors as an opportunity to carve a
bunch, and get some boxes together (finish that Wheel series) ... but alas
... I've been a lazy bum! So - I'll be carving furiously this spring, and
planting, too.

We need to schedule another gathering when the weather breaks.
Best wishes,
Autumn
