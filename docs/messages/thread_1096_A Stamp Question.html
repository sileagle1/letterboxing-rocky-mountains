A Stamp Question

From: gonefromnj (gonefromnj@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-05-15 18:40:24 UTC
Just wonder how many of you put a backing, like a piece of wood, on
the back of the stamps you carve and do you just glue it on? I've
experimented with a couple different backings, but I've always just
used Elmer's carpenter's (or wood) glue to attach them. I've seen
some interesting hunks of wood attached to some stamps and was just
wondering how you have attached your backings.

Thanks, JrsyBoy




Re: [RMLetterboxing] A Stamp Question

From: Jana J. Riska (jjcadenza@gmail.com) | Date: 2006-05-15 13:05:13 UTC-06:00
check the glue package. Most I've seen only work to a certain temp and
Rocky Mtn temperatures definitely go below that. I used to back mine and
found the best thing was often contact cement (rubber cement), but you have
to use it properly. apply a thin coat to both sides, allow them to dry for
at least 15 minutes or so, then put the two together.

Personally though, I've stopped backing mine. They stamp fine without it.
Sometimes they stamp better without as you can control the surface area. We
can do our best to control the elements, but we can't control other
letterboxers or animals and how exposed they leave our precious bundles.
Water, snow, etc will eventually get into the best of boxes and can warp the
wood. I've found enough stamps with broken wood and torn stamps to decide
that while it looks pretty and keeps a bit of ink off my fingers, I don't
think it's worth the trade-off. I'm more than happy to bear the trademarks
of a letterboxer--over inky fingers, in order to preserve a stamp a bit
longer.

If you research on the big list and various other places, you will find that
this has been a raging debate for years. Everyone thinks they know what
works best. It's kind of funny to watch. Good luck making your own
decisions, but I would most definitely advise you to read the labels very
carefully on any glue you try to use. I started initially with superglue,
only to find out that it shatters in the cold! Just a word to the wise.

Cadenza

Re: [RMLetterboxing] A Stamp Question

From: Renee and Dave (haipnsuch@juno.com) | Date: 2006-05-15 13:14:47 UTC-06:00
Yoque backed a stamp with rubber cement and it melted and got really
gross. That stamp is in the "repair shop." We have backed two other
stamps with leather. They match the journal leather and we found that
the leather is pliable enough to not have issues with getting a good
stamp image. Of course, the leather could attract mold and that would
definitely reduce the life of the box. I think we used Goop as the glue
for that. We assume not back our stamps at this point unless it totally
adds to the box ... like all the cool backings found on some of
Esmerelda's boxes ... coal, marble, etc.
~Catamount Carvers

From: HER (fauxsum@hotmail.com) | Date: 2006-05-15 19:17:49 UTC
For the most part I don't put backings on the majority of my own
stamps - most (especially my urban boxes) are small enough it just
doesn't matter and only gets in the way of some cramped box space.
Slowly, however, I'm starting to work with larger and larger images,
and for those I DO put backings on the stamps - both 'cause it helps
make it easier to stamp in, and because I can continue my design theme
with the backing.

If it helps, since I know some of the H-As stamps are also PREtty big,
it really helps me stamping in when larger stamps have backings (say
like Enginerd, for example)which imho would have been almost impossible
to get into my book w/o messing it up and having to restamp.

I don't have enough experience yet to see how different kinds of glue
hold up long-term, and would be interested in knowing from other
posters what they use to affix the stamp to backing, when backings are
used.

preboxed

From: jelybean_lb (jelybean@adelphia.net) | Date: 2006-05-15 21:57:48 UTC
When I first started L'b-ing, I had never seen a backing until I was
travelling out of state. At the time, I was using a journal with
rough paper and really liked the crispness of the image from the
backed stamp, so started backing all of mine.

I use Krazy Glue - the brush-on kind, and it seems to work OK. I've
been doing this for about four years now and have never had a report
of a damaged backing, seperated stamp, or shattered glue, and I used
to live in NY where it gets pretty fargin' COLD in the winter.

Re: A Stamp Question

From: RavenWolf (treikya@aol.com) | Date: 2006-05-16 17:39:55 UTC
I back my stamps onto either 1/4 inch plexiglass (you can pick it up
for cheap at any hardware store) or 1/2 inch foam core, believe it or
not. Either medium seems to stand up to both the cold, and the heat. I
haven't had very good luck with rubber cement either. Seems to just
turn into goo if it gets too hot. I had our personal stamps mounted
onto plexiglass with rubber cement, and then left them in the car on a
hot day - they slid right off!

But it's a pretty personal thing. We have seen stamps backed on
everything from Es's marble, to wood, to the tops of mason jars!

~RavenWolf~
